# Appendix: Consolidated Evidence Gaps & Reconciliations

This guide is built from 693 claims across 165 study notes. Its **heaviest evidence** is on
root-zone thermal management, substrate physical properties/aeration, irrigation/leaching/
salinity, rootstock/dwarfing, pruning, and UC IPM pest/disease. Its **thinnest evidence** is
genuinely **Lisbon-specific container numbers** — most quantitative targets are extrapolated
from citrus-general or container-crop science. The per-section Evidence Gaps are consolidated
below, followed by the cross-source conflicts and how each was resolved for the worked-example pot.

## A. The overarching gap

**There is no end-to-end container-Lisbon trial in the evidence base.** Variety identity,
self-fertility/parthenocarpy, cold-kill threshold, and clonal-strain differences are
**Lisbon-specific and well-sourced** (UCR CVC, ISHS, UC IPM). But nearly every *container*
target — pot AFP, water volume/frequency, fertilizer rate, yield, lifespan — is inherited from
general citrus or container-crop science and **downscaled by inference**. Treat the numbers as
well-reasoned defaults to verify by direct measurement (pour-through pH/EC, leaf tissue, pot
weight), not as Lisbon-pot-calibrated constants.

## B. Recurring evidence gaps by theme

| Theme | Gap | Sections |
|---|---|---|
| **Container size/yield** | No pot-grown Lisbon size or yield data; "a few dozen fruit/yr" and "indefinite in pot" are extrapolations | §1, §4, §12, §18 |
| **Soil targets** | AFP/EC/pH targets are general container science; normalization carries ±0.3 pH and method-inferred EC confidence — a direct pour-through on the actual pot supersedes them | §3, §6, §10, §14 |
| **Fertilizer rate** | Only field/per-tree rates exist; the pot downscaling is explicitly assumption-based (§10.4) | §10 |
| **Irrigation quantity** | "~daily in summer" is extrapolated; orchard Kc doesn't map to a pot; no measured pot water use | §11 |
| **Rootstock fit** | Flying Dragon × Lisbon graft compatibility and the dwarfing-vs-salinity resolution rest partly on weak synthesis | §2 |
| **Pruning** | Lisbon/container pruning advice is mostly weak consumer sourcing; the open-vs-dense balance is qualitative | §7 |
| **Wind/structure** | All container wind hazards extrapolated from one balcony note; no staking specifics in the corpus | §8 |
| **Quality** | No legal maturity standard for Lisbon; quality benchmarks are from a fed Eureka study | §12 |
| **Pests/disease** | Management is citrus-general UC IPM (directly applicable, not Lisbon-trialed); few formal action thresholds; quarantine specifics current as of 2024 | §13, §16 |

| **Lifecycle** | Decline-cycle timeline (5–7 yr) and prevention cadence are synthesis estimates | §17 |

## C. The twelve cross-source reconciliations

These tensions surfaced across sources; the guide follows the **Resolution** column throughout.

| # | Tension | Resolution for the 19″×14″ west-facing Brentwood pot | Home |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Open canopy interior for light **vs** don't thin (sunburn) | Light **selective** interior thinning only; favor sunburn protection on the west wall; whitewash exposed bark | §7, §15 |
| 2 | Water ~every 3 days **vs** ~daily | Weather/exposure-driven by **pot weight**; ~daily in heat, every 2–3 d in cool marine spells | §11 |
| 3 | Shade cloth 30–50% **vs** flat 50% | **30–50%**, leaning 50% on west exposure >95–100°F | §15 |
| 4 | Repot/root-prune every 2–3 yr **vs** 3–5 yr | **~2–3 yr** for a 19″ pot on dwarfing rootstock | §5 |
| 5 | Salt-tolerant **vs** size-controlling rootstock | **Prioritize dwarfing (Flying Dragon)**; manage salt by leaching, not rootstock | §2 |
| 6 | Light pot cools roots **vs** white wall bounces heat | Light **pot**, avoid a reflective wall behind the canopy; shade cloth handles reflected load | §15 |
| 7 | pH 5.5–6.5 **vs** 6.0–7.0 | Target **~6.0–6.5**; at the high end use Fe-EDDHA, don't fight pH | §10, §14 |
| 8 | Carrizo/Troyer as rootstock | **Do not use** for Lisbon (incompatibility + salt/pH sensitivity) | §2 |
| 9 | Mal secco as a threat | **Not in California** — exclude as a local threat | §13 |
| 10 | Greasy spot: water late **vs** morning | **Water in the morning** so leaves dry fast | §11, §16 |
| 11 | Biochar 20% optimum **vs** 10–15% cap | **Cap 10–15%** given alkaline LA water | §6 |
| 12 | Field N/micro rates (lb/acre, lb/tree) | **Do not transfer** to a pot — labeled product + downscale | §10 |
