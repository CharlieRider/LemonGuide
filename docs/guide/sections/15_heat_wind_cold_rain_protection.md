# 15. Heat, Wind, Cold, and Rain Protection

> **Evidence callout.** **Strong** on the physiology and thresholds — root-temperature work
> (Ingram 1985), heat-stress photosynthesis data, container-color trials, and IFAS/UC freeze
> thresholds [A004](../../claim_table.html#claim-a004), [B076](../../claim_table.html#claim-b076), [A036](../../claim_table.html#claim-a036), [C033](../../claim_table.html#claim-c033). Tactic *specifics* (shade %, whitewash dilution, frost
> cloth) are a mix of **strong** extension and **weak** consumer sources [B080](../../claim_table.html#claim-b080), [B124](../../claim_table.html#claim-b124), flagged
> inline. This section is the **home** for protecting against weather extremes; root-temperature
> *targets* are §3, the bloom/flush *vulnerability windows* are §4/§9, wind *structural* hazards
> are §8, and sunburn-vs-pruning is §7.

A west-facing wall is a thermal amplifier: it delivers the most light (good for fruiting, §1)
but also the **hottest afternoon load on the most sunburn-prone tree there is** — citrus has
**~1 mm paper-thin bark and lemons are the most sunburn-vulnerable type** [B122](../../claim_table.html#claim-b122). The same
pot's great advantage is that, unlike an orchard tree, **it can be moved or shaded** for the few
days a year that matter [B149](../../claim_table.html#claim-b149). The two events to plan for are the **summer heat wave** and the
**rare winter frost** — Lisbon is heat-tough but, as a true lemon, **cold-tender** (§1).

## 15.1 Heat — Thresholds (strong)

| Threshold | Value | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Optimum growth/photosynthesis | **25–30°C (77–86°F)**; range ~10–35°C | B073 | Strong |
| Photosynthesis declines | Above **~30°C (86°F)** — before visible symptoms | B075 | Strong |
| Mild heat stress (leaf) | Begins **~36°C leaf temp**; leaves run hotter than air in sun | B074 | Strong |
| Severe | **38–40°C cut net photosynthesis 39.8–55.6%** over 25 days | B076 | Strong |
| **Root zone** | Growth slows >30°C, **ceases >~38°C**; black-pot root biomass **−71%**; injury ~50°C | A038, C085 | Strong / Moderate |
| Pot-color effect | Black pot **50.3°C** vs white **~36°C** (Δ~14°C); dark pots +8.9–14.7°C | A036, A037 | Strong |
| Heat symptom sequence | Inward leaf-edge curl → bleaching/yellowing → reduced set, fruit drop, fruit sunburn | B077 | Strong |

> **VPD nuance (carry from §11):** citrus is **isohydric** — hot, dry air (high VPD) closes
> stomata even with wet soil [B082](../../claim_table.html#claim-b082), [B083](../../claim_table.html#claim-b083); midday wilt is often VPD, not thirst. The real levers
> are **lowering heat load (shade, evaporative cooling, humidity), not more water** [B085](../../claim_table.html#claim-b085), [B084](../../claim_table.html#claim-b084).

## 15.2 Heat — Protection Tactics (strong/weak mix)

| Tactic | Specification | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Afternoon shade** | **30–50% shade cloth** when air >~95–100°F; place on the **west side** (most damaging exposure) | B080, B127, B128 | Strong / Weak |
| **Keep root zone cool** | **Light/white pot**, shade the sun-facing wall, **double-pot** (air gap), **mulch** 1–1.5 in | A039, C083, C082, B113 | Moderate |
| **Evaporative cooling** | Cycle misters (wet, let mostly evaporate, re-wet before dry) — dropped transpiration ~50%; **don't waterlog** the shallow pot | B036, B037, B038 | Strong / Moderate |
| **Particle film / whitewash** | Reflective kaolin/CaCO₃ on exposed fruit; **white latex (water-based, ~1:1 with water)** on exposed non-green bark | B079, B039, B124 | Strong / Weak |
| **Keep canopy dense + hydrated before heat** | Well-watered, well-shaded trees resist heat; water-stressed ones sunburn and drop fruit | B078 | Strong |
| **Don't prune/fertilize into a heat wave** | Avoid pushing vulnerable new growth; don't expose bark by thinning | B080, A080 | Strong / Moderate |
| Antitranspirant/reflective sprays | Reduce water loss/heat load but **can limit gas exchange — use cautiously** | B040 | Moderate |

> ⚠️ **Minor conflict (reconciled):** shade-cloth density is given as "30–50%" [B080](../../claim_table.html#claim-b080) and "50%"
> [B127](../../claim_table.html#claim-b127). **Use the 30–50% range**, leaning to 50% in a severe inland-push heat wave. Evaporative
> cooling **does not block UV** — pair it with shade or particle film for sunburn [B039](../../claim_table.html#claim-b039).

## 15.3 Cold — Thresholds (strong; Lisbon-specific)

| Threshold | Value | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Lisbon / true-lemon kill** | Damaged/killed at **mid-to-high 20s °F (~−2 to −4°C)** — most cold-tender common citrus | B049, C032, D009 | Strong |
| **Fruit freeze** | **~26–28°F** held several hours (4 h at 26°F ≈ 30% damage in oranges); fruit-laden tree less tolerant | C033, D010 | Strong |
| Comparators | Meyer ~22°F; mandarins ~20°F/4 h — **true lemons are more tender** | C034 | Strong |
| Tissue order | flowers > vegetative flush > fruit > hardened leaves > wood (most→least sensitive) | C031 | Strong |
| Depends on | **Duration + hardening state, not just the minimum reached** | C035 | Strong |
| **Container caveat** | Pots/young trees are **more frost-susceptible — roots are above ground and exposed** | D011 | Strong |

> ⚠️ **The binding number for the whole guide:** plan frost protection around the **mid-to-high
> 20s °F kill point** [B049](../../claim_table.html#claim-b049). West-LA hard frost is rare but not impossible, and the **pot makes
> the root zone more exposed than ground** [D011](../../claim_table.html#claim-d011) — so protection is planning, not a contingency.
> The winter main crop (§12) means fruit is hanging during the frost window (fruit freezes
> ~26–28°F [C033](../../claim_table.html#claim-c033)).

## 15.4 Cold — Protection Tactics (strong)

| Tactic | Specification | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cover** | Frost cloth/burlap **to the ground** (traps reradiated soil heat) | D012 | Strong |
| **Pre-water** | Water the soil **before** a freeze (moist soil holds/releases heat) | D012 | Strong |
| **Site for reradiated heat** | Near a **south/west wall** | D012 | Strong |
| **Move the pot** | A container can be **moved to shelter** for the event [B149](../../claim_table.html#claim-b149) | B149 | Moderate |
| **Harden, don't push** | **Avoid late-season N** that pushes soft cold-vulnerable growth | D013 | Strong |
| Rootstock help | Flying Dragon improves cold hardening (§2) | C014(§2) | Moderate |

The west-wall siting that creates the *heat* problem is also the *frost* defense (reradiated
heat) [D012](../../claim_table.html#claim-d012) — the same spot, managed seasonally.

## 15.5 Wind and Rain (cross-ref §8 / §3)

- **Wind** is its own desiccation/tip-over hazard — the **structural** home is §8 (secure the
  pot, building wall as windbreak, espalier; wind raises water use). Wind-margin scorch can be
  mistaken for salt/heat [D039/§8].
- **Rain / winter waterlogging:** the corpus has **no dedicated rain-protection claim**, but the
  drainage discipline of §3 covers it — citrus **resents winter waterlogging** [D091/§11], so
  keep the pot **elevated ≥½″** with clear drainage [A127/§3] and **never let it sit in a
  saucer** of rainwater. Overhead winter cover is plausible but **not in the evidence** (gap).

## 15.6 The West-LA Microclimate Advantage (weak, but real)

The **marine layer** (May–June and many summer mornings) **moderates daytime highs and buffers
heat stress** versus inland LA [C077](../../claim_table.html#claim-c077), [C074](../../claim_table.html#claim-c074). It cuts effective ET on overcast mornings [B022/§11]
and lowers the heat-wave frequency a Brentwood balcony faces — a genuine, if weakly sourced,
mitigation. The trade-off is the humidity/leaf-wetness it adds (greasy-spot risk, §13).
**Morning sun + afternoon shade is the ideal exposure pattern** [B129](../../claim_table.html#claim-b129) — which a west wall plus
an afternoon shade cloth approximates.

---

> ### 🪴 Worked example — 19″×14″ west-facing Brentwood / West LA balcony
> - **Heat is the frequent event.** On the west wall, the root zone can bake (black pot ~50°C
>   [A036](../../claim_table.html#claim-a036)) past the **>38°C root-stop** [A038](../../claim_table.html#claim-a038). Defenses stack: **light/white pot + double-pot +
>   1–1.5″ mulch** to keep roots cool [A039](../../claim_table.html#claim-a039), [C083](../../claim_table.html#claim-c083), [C082](../../claim_table.html#claim-c082), **30–50% afternoon shade cloth on the
>   west side** when air >95–100°F [B080](../../claim_table.html#claim-b080), [B128](../../claim_table.html#claim-b128), and **don't over-thin the canopy** (sunburn
>   shield, §7) [A123](../../claim_table.html#claim-a123). Whitewash exposed bark (1:1 latex) if it gets scorched [B124](../../claim_table.html#claim-b124).
> - **Don't over-water in heat** — much midday wilt is VPD, not thirst [B084](../../claim_table.html#claim-b084); cool the load
>   instead (shade, evaporative cooling) [B085](../../claim_table.html#claim-b085). The **marine layer helps** by trimming highs
>   [C077](../../claim_table.html#claim-c077).
> - **Cold is the rare-but-fatal event.** Around the **mid-to-high 20s °F** kill point [B049](../../claim_table.html#claim-b049),
>   **cover with frost cloth to the ground, pre-water, and use the wall's reradiated heat**
>   [D012](../../claim_table.html#claim-d012) — or **move the pot** to shelter [B149](../../claim_table.html#claim-b149). Protect hanging winter fruit (freezes
>   ~26–28°F [C033](../../claim_table.html#claim-c033)). **No late-season N** [D013](../../claim_table.html#claim-d013).
> - **Rain:** keep the pot elevated with clear drainage so winter rain doesn't waterlog it
>   (§3/§11) — citrus resents winter waterlogging.

## Key takeaways
- **Heat is the frequent hazard, cold the rare-but-fatal one.** Plan shade for summer and a
  frost cover for the **mid-to-high 20s °F** kill point [B049](../../claim_table.html#claim-b049).
- **Keep the root zone cool:** light/white pot, double-pot, mulch, shade the wall — black pots
  hit ~50°C and stop roots >38°C [A036](../../claim_table.html#claim-a036), [A038](../../claim_table.html#claim-a038).
- **30–50% afternoon shade** (west side) above ~95–100°F; **don't over-thin** the sunburn shield
  [B080](../../claim_table.html#claim-b080), [B128](../../claim_table.html#claim-b128), [A123](../../claim_table.html#claim-a123).
- **Much midday wilt is VPD, not thirst** — lower heat load, don't over-water [B084](../../claim_table.html#claim-b084), [B085](../../claim_table.html#claim-b085).
- **Frost: cover to the ground + pre-water + reradiated wall heat, or move the pot** [D012](../../claim_table.html#claim-d012), [B149](../../claim_table.html#claim-b149);
  **no late-season N** [D013](../../claim_table.html#claim-d013).
- **The marine layer is a real, free heat buffer** [C077](../../claim_table.html#claim-c077); morning-sun/afternoon-shade is ideal
  [B129](../../claim_table.html#claim-b129).

## Things not to do
- **Don't use a dark pot** on a west wall — root zone bakes (+9–15°C) [A037](../../claim_table.html#claim-a037), [A038](../../claim_table.html#claim-a038).
- **Don't over-thin the canopy** "for airflow" into summer — it exposes bark to sunburn [A080](../../claim_table.html#claim-a080), [A123](../../claim_table.html#claim-a123) (§7).
- **Don't over-water a wilting plant in a heat wave** — it's often VPD; cool the load [B084](../../claim_table.html#claim-b084), [B085](../../claim_table.html#claim-b085).
- **Don't prune or push N into a heat wave or late season** — tender new growth burns/freezes
  [B080](../../claim_table.html#claim-b080), [D013](../../claim_table.html#claim-d013).
- **Don't rely on Lisbon being "cold-hardy"** — it's the most cold-tender common citrus [B049](../../claim_table.html#claim-b049).
- **Don't let the pot sit in rainwater** — winter waterlogging (§3/§11).

## Evidence gaps
- **Tactic specifics (shade %, whitewash 1:1, frost cloth) are partly weak/consumer** [B124](../../claim_table.html#claim-b124), [B127](../../claim_table.html#claim-b127); the 30–50% shade range reconciles a minor source conflict, not a measured Lisbon trial.
- **No rain/overhead-cover protection claim** in the corpus — winter-rain handling is inferred
  from drainage principles (§3) only.
- **Marine-layer benefit is weakly sourced** [C077](../../claim_table.html#claim-c077) climate context, not a citrus measurement.
- **Evaporative-cooling transpiration data is from avocado** [B038](../../claim_table.html#claim-b038), extrapolated to citrus.
- **No container-Lisbon freeze trial** — thresholds are general lemon/citrus [C032](../../claim_table.html#claim-c032), [C033](../../claim_table.html#claim-c033).
