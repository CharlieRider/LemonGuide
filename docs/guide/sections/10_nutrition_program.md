# 10. Nutrition and Fertigation Program

> **Evidence callout.** This is the **keystone** quantitative section and is **strong** general
> citrus — IFAS/UC extension N-K-Ca-Mg rates, leaf sufficiency ranges, and chelate-by-pH
> chemistry [E096, D141, A056]. It is **weakly Lisbon-specific** (a couple of consumer/nursery
> NPK-ratio claims [E037, E021]); fruit-quality-vs-nutrition is lemon-specific [C092]. **Format
> decision (locked):** this is expressed as a **soil-pot granular / controlled-release / liquid
> citrus-fertilizer program**, *not* a hydroponic A+B / mmol-per-litre fertigation recipe — a
> bark-pot tree is not fertigated, and that format would be fabricated. **Rate decision
> (locked):** field/per-tree rates and EC ceilings are presented as the cited evidence; the
> default instruction is **"use a labeled citrus product per its directions,"** and any pot
> number appears only as an **assumption-flagged illustration**, never as a measured
> recommendation.
>
> **Normalization note (per build rules):** pH and EC here are on the **home-pot pour-through
> basis**. The normalizer maps substrate-extract/saturation readings to pour-through as
> **identity ± ~0.3 pH units** and **pour-through ≈ saturation-media-extract for EC** (NCSU
> PourThru, Cavins 2000) [normalized claims A040, B137, D134, D138]. So the pH and EC targets
> below can be read directly against a pour-through test on the 14″ pot (§14).

Two facts frame the whole program for this site: **(1) a free-draining 14″ pot leaches
nutrients fast**, and **(2) the dwarfing Flying Dragon rootstock is a *poor* nutrient
scavenger** [B104/§2] — citrus also has few, short root hairs and leans on mycorrhizae [B027].
The answer is **steady, complete, low-dose feeding ("weekly weakly") with micronutrients**
[B142, D099] — never infrequent heavy doses that spike salt in a confined root zone.

## 10.1 pH Target and the Alkaline-Water Problem (strong; normalized basis)

| Parameter | Target (pour-through) | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Substrate pH** | **~6.0–6.5** (broader citrus norm 5.5–7.0; Lisbon consumer refs 5.5–6.5) | A040, A066, B137, E021 | Strong / Weak |
| Why 6.0–6.5 | Moving pH from >7 down to 6.0–6.5 **frees K, P, Ca, Mn, Zn, Fe** | A040 | Strong |
| Zn risk | Zn precipitates / deficiency favored **above pH 6.5** | B009 | Strong |
| Fe chemistry | Fe²⁺ oxidizes to unavailable Fe³⁺ above pH ~5.3; visible deficiency typically pH **>7.4** | A057 | Strong |
| pH is dynamic | Irrigation alkalinity + fertilizer move pH continuously — **monitor** | A043 | Strong |

> ⚠️ **The central nutrition problem for West LA:** hard, alkaline tap water pushes substrate
> pH **up**, toward Fe/Mn/Zn lockout [A057, B009]. Manage it three ways: (1) keep pH ~6.0–6.5
> with **sulfur** (≈1 tsp 90% S per gal ≈ −1 unit [A041/§6]) or an **acid-forming citrus
> fertilizer** [E040]; (2) supply Ca via **gypsum** (Ca/S, *no* pH rise [C029]) rather than
> lime; (3) when chlorosis persists under alkaline water, use the **right chelate for the pH**
> (below). pH-adjustment products are detailed in §6; testing in §14.

## 10.2 EC / Salinity Ceilings (strong; normalized basis)

| Parameter | Value (pour-through) | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Media EC ceiling (working)** | **< ~1.5 dS/m** (≈1000 ppm TDS); keep feed solution below this too | E002, D138 | Strong |
| Detrimental | **> ~2.0 dS/m** generally injurious (root injury, tip/margin burn, chlorosis, wilt) | D134 | Strong |
| Citrus salt threshold | **ECe ≈ 1.4 dS/m**, then ~**13% yield loss per dS/m** above it | B053 | Strong |
| Pre-plant coir | Media EC **< 1.0 mS/cm** before planting | B137 | Moderate |
| Salt mechanism | Toxic **Cl⁻ / Na⁺** accumulation in leaves (not osmotic alone); Cl the key limiter (>~152 mg/L) | B063, B064 | Strong |

> ⚠️ **Salt-sensitive rootstock × hard water = leach on discipline.** Flying Dragon is
> salt-*sensitive* (§2). Keep EC under ~1.5 dS/m by **low-salt-index/controlled-release
> fertilizers** [D137], modest dosing, and a **routine leaching fraction** — full leaching
> protocol is the home of §14 (~15–30% leaching fraction each watering + monthly deep flush).

## 10.3 Program Format — Granular / CRF / Liquid (not fertigation A+B)

**Default instruction:** use a **labeled citrus fertilizer with included micronutrients
(Fe, Mn, Zn) per its directions** [E040, E022]; containers rarely supply micros [E040].

| Delivery | Cadence (per the evidence) | Role | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Controlled-release (CRF)** | **≥1×/yr** (e.g., ~March & August); release **speeds up in heat** | Baseline steady feed | E606, D089, A089 | Strong |
| Dry soluble granular | 3–6×/yr | Cheapest; uneven placement risk | E099 | Strong |
| **Liquid / "weekly weakly"** | **≥10×/yr** — small dilute doses to avoid salt spikes | Responsiveness; matches fast-leaching pot | E099, E639, E627 | Strong / Moderate |
| Topdressed CRF | Surface, **don't incorporate** (leaches less N) | Placement | A090 | Strong |

> **Why "weekly weakly":** splitting feed **reduces the salt spike and leaching loss** of a
> single heavy dose [A072] and suits a leaching pot + poorly-scavenging dwarf root [B142,
> D099]. This is the soil-pot analogue of fertigation's "frequent small doses" logic [E001] —
> *not* a hydroponic recipe. **NPK ratio:** citrus favors **2-1-1 or 3-1-1** (e.g., 6-3-3,
> 9-3-6); a Dwarf Lisbon is cited near **6-5-6** [E037, *moderate*].

## 10.4 Nitrogen — Cited Field Rates (do not fabricate a pot number)

**Per-tree field rates (the cited evidence):**

| Stage | Rate | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Young, Year 1 | **0.15–0.30 lb N/tree/yr** | E096 | Strong |
| Young, Year 2 | 0.30–0.60 lb N/tree/yr | E096 | Strong |
| Young, Year 3 | 0.45–0.90 lb N/tree/yr | E096 | Strong |
| Full-size (in-ground) | ~1.0 lb actual N/yr, split | D014, A071 | Strong |
| Conversion | 1 lb N ≈ 5 lb ammonium sulfate ≈ 100 lb composted manure | D015 | Strong |
| **Container caveat** | Dwarf/container trees with restricted roots need **proportionally less N** than full-size | D017 | Strong |
| **Do NOT use** | Per-acre rates (120–245 lb N/acre) for a pot | E097 | Strong |

> 🧮 **Assumption-based downscaling illustration (NOT a measured recommendation).** *If* one
> takes the young per-tree figure of **0.15–0.30 lb N/tree/yr** [E096] for a small container
> Lisbon, that is **~70–140 g N/yr**. Delivered "weekly weakly" over a ~Feb–Oct (≈9-month,
> ~36-week) feeding window, that is **~2–4 g N/week**. *Every step here is an assumption*
> (that the young per-tree rate applies to a 14″ pot; that the season is 36 weeks; that the
> product's %N and your dilution match). **It is an illustration of the order of magnitude,
> not a number to dose by — follow the product label and verify with leaf testing (§14).**

Modest **winter N supports spring bloom** [B046], but **avoid heavy late-season N** — it
roughens rind, delays coloring, hurts quality, and pushes cold-tender growth [D016, D013].

## 10.5 Potassium, Phosphorus, Calcium, Magnesium (strong)

| Nutrient | Target / proportion | Notes | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **K (as K₂O)** | **≈ equal to the N rate**; **+25%** if leaf K runs low | Drives **fruit size**; deficiency = small thin-rind fruit, splitting; leaf K >0.7%, optimum **1.2–1.7%** | D147, E098, D543, D586 | Strong |
| **P (as P₂O₅)** | Non-bearing **≈50–75% of N** | High soluble P suppresses mycorrhizae [B031] | E098 | Strong |
| **Ca** | Highest of N/Ca/Mg by mass; supply during **bloom/fruit set (~Apr–Aug)** | Cell-wall/rind strength; **gypsum or calcium nitrate**; easily leached + coir-depleted | E107, D090, D046, D049 | Strong / Moderate |
| **Mg** | **≈20% of N** if soil tests low | Chlorophyll core; deficiency = interveinal chlorosis on **older** leaves; **Epsom (no pH change)** or dolomite (raises pH) | E098, D047, D049 | Strong / Moderate |

> **Diagnostic shortcut:** **old-leaf** interveinal chlorosis = **Mg**; **new-leaf** chlorosis =
> Fe/Mn/Zn (pH lockout) [D047, A057]. Don't over-maximize K — very high K makes large, thick,
> coarse-peeled fruit; dial to target via leaf testing, not maximum [D543, D586].

## 10.6 Micronutrients and the Chelate-by-pH Rule (strong — critical for alkaline water)

Containers under alkaline water routinely run short of **Fe, Zn, Mn** [E040, A073]. Choose the
**iron chelate by your pH**:

| Substrate pH | Iron chelate that survives | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| < 6.0 | Any (EDTA/DTPA/EDDHA) — cheap **EDTA** suffices | A055, A081 | Strong |
| < 6.5 | EDTA / DTPA adequate and cheaper | A081 | Strong |
| **> 7.0–7.4** | **Fe-EDDHA only** (Fe-EDTA ~97.5% degraded at pH 7.5; Fe-EDDHA effective pH 4.0–9.0) | A056, A058, B006 | Strong |

**Application route by nutrient:**

| Nutrient | Best route | Why | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Fe** | **Soil-applied chelate** (right chelate for pH) | Foliar Fe poorly translocated | B010 | Strong |
| **Zn, Mn** | **Foliar** | Foliar ~80% efficient vs 10–20% soil; leaf conc. ~2× | B106, B107, B260 | Strong |
| **B** | Soil (sparingly) | Soil gave ~2× leaf B vs foliar; but leaches easily — don't overdo | B108 | Strong |

Foliar tips: spray in **cool/humid hours** (morning marine layer is ideal) on 2/3-to-fully
expanded young leaves, **solution pH 6–7** (urea above pH 7 risks ammonia burn); effect is
**temporary (one flush) and cannot replace soil N-P-K** [E593, B110, E592]. **AMF** improve
uptake of the lockout-prone P/Zn/Cu [B028] (§6).

## 10.7 Timing and Cadence (strong)

| Window | Action | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Feb–Oct** | Active feeding — monthly granular **or weekly-weakly liquid**; CRF ~March & August | D089, E627 | Strong |
| Three N splits | (1) Feb→flowering, (2) flowering→mid-June, (3) mid-June→mid-Oct | E100, D016 | Strong |
| Micronutrients | Every **4–6 weeks Feb–Oct**; Ca during bloom/fruit set (~Apr–Aug) | D090 | Strong |
| **Winter** | **Minimal/no feed** (modest N only if supporting bloom) — avoid late-season N | D089, D013, B046 | Strong |

> CRF **releases faster in hot, frequently-watered pots** [A089] — on a west wall, lean on
> split light liquid feeds rather than a single heavy CRF charge, and watch EC (§14).

## 10.8 Leaf Sufficiency Ranges (diagnostic; sampling home is §14)

| Nutrient | Sufficiency (% dry wt) | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| N | **2.5–2.7%** (study range 1.9–2.3%) | D141, E105 | Strong |
| P | 0.12–0.16% | D141 | Strong |
| K | **1.2–1.7%** (deficient <0.7%) | D141, D586 | Strong |
| Ca | 3.0–4.9% (study 2.1–2.8%) | D141, E105 | Strong |
| Mg | 0.30–0.49% (study 0.20–0.33%) | D141, E105 | Strong |

Sample **mature, non-fruiting spring-flush leaves 4–6 months old, 20–30 per area** [D139,
D140]. **Combine leaf + media testing** to separate a true deficiency from a pH-induced
lockout (adequate Fe but chlorotic at high pH) [D142]. Lemon **fruit quality is
nutrition-responsive, not fixed by variety** — adequate NPK raised acidity, TSS, and vitamin C
[C092, C095].

## 10.9 Organic Options (moderate)

Partial organic substitution sustained yield and quality — **fully organic not required**
[C094]. Combine **meals for steady baseline** (feather meal ≈12-0-0) with **liquids for quick
uptake** (fish emulsion ≈2-4-1, every 2–4 wk) [C104, C105, C107]. Organics are **lower
salt-index/lower burn risk** but **N release stalls when media <~22°C** [C108] — and citrus
roots barely grow that cool anyway (§3/§4).

---

> ### 🪴 Worked example — 19″×14″ west-facing Brentwood / West LA balcony
> - **The pH battle is the main event.** Hard alkaline tap water drives pH up toward Fe/Zn/Mn
>   lockout [A057, B009]. Use an **acid-forming citrus fertilizer with micros** [E040], keep
>   **pH ~6.0–6.5** (sulfur if it climbs [A041]), and **default to Fe-EDDHA** for any iron
>   chlorosis because pour-through pH may sit >7 [A058].
> - **Feed weekly-weakly Feb–Oct**, ≥10 light liquid doses [E099, E627], not heavy infrequent
>   feeds — protects the salt-sensitive Flying Dragon root and the leaching pot [B142].
> - **Keep pour-through EC < 1.5 dS/m** [E002, D138]; pair feeding with the §14 leaching routine
>   (15–30% leaching fraction + monthly flush) given hard water.
> - **Rate:** follow the labeled citrus product. *As an order-of-magnitude check only,* the
>   young per-tree figure (0.15–0.30 lb N/yr [E096]) ≈ ~2–4 g N/week over the season — **an
>   assumption-based illustration, not a dose** (§10.4); verify with leaf testing [D139].
> - **Ca via gypsum** (no pH rise [C029]); **Mg via Epsom** if old leaves yellow [D047, D049].
> - **Spray foliar Zn/Mn in the cool marine-layer morning** [B110]; keep solution pH 6–7 [E593].
> - **Ease off feeding into winter** [D089]; skip heavy late-season N (cold-tender growth +
>   poor rind) [D013, D016].

## Key takeaways
- **Feed steady, complete, and light ("weekly weakly") Feb–Oct** [D099, E099]; the leaching pot
  + poorly-scavenging dwarf root demand it [B142, B104].
- **Hold pH ~6.0–6.5** (pour-through) to keep Fe/Zn/Mn available [A040, B009]; alkaline LA water
  fights this [A057].
- **Keep pour-through EC < ~1.5 dS/m** (>2.0 injurious) [E002, D134]; use low-salt-index/CRF
  and leach (§14) — Flying Dragon is salt-sensitive.
- **Cite field/per-tree N (0.15–0.90 lb/tree/yr young)** [E096]; use a **labeled citrus product**
  and verify by **leaf testing** — pot numbers here are illustrations only.
- **K₂O ≈ N rate** (+25% if low) [D147]; **Ca highest by mass** (gypsum) [E107]; **Mg ≈20% of N**
  (Epsom; old-leaf chlorosis) [E098, D047].
- **Match iron chelate to pH** — **Fe-EDDHA above pH 7** [A058]; **Zn/Mn foliar**, **Fe soil**
  [B010, B107].

## Things not to do
- **Don't apply per-acre field rates to a pot** [E097]; **don't dose to a fabricated pot number.**
- **Don't feed heavy and infrequent** — salt spike + leaching loss in a confined root zone [A072].
- **Don't push heavy late-season/winter N** — poor rind/color, cold-tender growth [D016, D013].
- **Don't use Fe-EDTA under alkaline water** — it degrades; use Fe-EDDHA [A056, A058].
- **Don't let pour-through EC exceed ~2.0 dS/m** [D134]; leach instead (§14).
- **Don't rely on foliar feeding for N-P-K** — it's a temporary micro fix only [E592].
- **Don't over-apply soluble P** if relying on mycorrhizae [B031].

## Evidence gaps
- **No container-Lisbon fertilizer trial.** All rates are field/per-tree extension figures
  [E096, D014]; the downscaling to a 14″ pot is explicitly assumption-based (§10.4).
- **NPK-ratio and pH for "Dwarf Lisbon" are weak/moderate** consumer/nursery claims [E037,
  E021] and mildly conflict with broader citrus norms (reconciled to ~6.0–6.5).
- **Leaf sufficiency ranges are citrus-general** [D141, E105], not Lisbon-container-calibrated.
- **EC/pH normalization carries ±0.3 pH and method-inferred EC confidence** [normalized claims];
  a direct pour-through reading on the actual pot (§14) supersedes these.
