# 3. Root-Zone and Media Engineering

> **Evidence callout.** This section is **strong** on container-crop physics (UC ANR,
> Purdue, Clemson, peer-reviewed substrate science) but largely **extrapolated to citrus**:
> the air/water/porosity targets are general container-substrate science, not Lisbon
> trials. The one citrus-specific substrate study is the 2026 biochar trial [E696–E698].
> **Normalization note:** per the build rules, AFP/porosity figures here are read on the
> **14″-deep home-pot basis**. The normalizer left most AFP claims as "context-only"
> (substrate-bench values, not geometry-converted), so the operative correction is the
> *height rule* [A114](../../claim_table.html#claim-a114), [A115](../../claim_table.html#claim-a115): a shallow 14″ pot holds **more water and less air** than the
> deep columns used to measure published AFP — so we **design the bulk mix toward the high
> end (~15–20% AFP)** [E631](../../claim_table.html#claim-e631) to keep the wet bottom band above the hypoxia floor.
> Recipe/ingredients are the home of **§6**; this section sets the *targets and physics*.

The root zone of a 14″ pot is a small, shallow, fast-changing reservoir whose single
binding constraint is **oxygen at the bottom**. Citrus has the least heat-tolerant root
system of common woody ornamentals [A001](../../claim_table.html#claim-a001) and mounts only a *reactive* hypoxia response —
it has no aerenchyma, so it copes biochemically rather than avoiding low O₂ [B062](../../claim_table.html#claim-b062), [B061](../../claim_table.html#claim-b061).
The engineering goal is therefore **prevention**: build a profile that never goes anoxic at
the base, drains freely, and resists collapsing into fines over the years.

## 3.1 Container Geometry and Size (general container citrus)

| Parameter | Value | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Practical "sweet spot" | **~15-gal class** — big enough for healthy roots, light enough to move; moderate restriction improves fruit-per-canopy | D088 | Strong |
| Workable small-tree range | 14–16 in pots; step up when root-bound | A126 | Strong |
| Recommended final range | 18–36 in diameter; repot every 3–5 yr | B086 | Strong |
| Minimum depth | Pot **≥ ~12–15″ deep** | E032 | Weak |
| Step-up increment | Only **+2–5 in diameter** at a time; never jump to a much larger pot | D064, C022 | Weak |

**Why not "just use a bigger pot."** Over-potting is a root-rot trap: excess mix beyond the
root mass stays saturated and drives hypoxia/Phytophthora [D064](../../claim_table.html#claim-d064). Step up one size at a
time [D064](../../claim_table.html#claim-d064), [C022](../../claim_table.html#claim-c022). The **19″×14″ pot is ≈ 15-gal class** — squarely in the documented sweet
spot [D088](../../claim_table.html#claim-d088) and above the 12–15″ depth minimum [E032](../../claim_table.html#claim-e032), though at the small end of the
18–36″ final range [B086](../../claim_table.html#claim-b086), so plan a root-prune-and-refresh cadence rather than endless
up-potting (home: §5/§7).

**The height–water rule (decisive for a shallow pot):** container capacity and air space
both depend on pot height — taller pots drain more (more air, less water); shorter pots
hold more water and less air [A065](../../claim_table.html#claim-a065), [A115](../../claim_table.html#claim-a115). For an *identical* mix, AFP scales steeply with
height: ~0.5% in a tray, ~13% in a 4-in pot, ~20% in a 6-in pot [A114](../../claim_table.html#claim-a114). A 14″ pot is
wetter and lower-oxygen at its base than a deep half-barrel of the same mix — the reason
the mix must be engineered coarse.

## 3.2 Air, Water, and Porosity Targets (extrapolated container science)

| Property | Target | Note | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Air-filled porosity (AFP)** | **≥10% and ≤25%** at container capacity | <10% O₂-short; >25% dries too fast | A109 | Strong |
| AFP — design target for *this* pot | **~15–20% (aim ~20%)** | Shallow pot → larger saturated zone; push high | E631, E630 | Moderate |
| Total porosity | **≥50%** (AFP + WHC) | — | A111 | Strong |
| Hypoxia floor | Root respiration impaired **<~12% AFP**; hypoxia **<~10%** | Wet bottom band must stay above this | B011, B012 | Extrapolated |
| Minimum gas-filled pores | ≥10% of volume at field capacity, ≥10% of that gas being O₂ | — | B013 | Extrapolated |
| Water-holding (reference) | peat ~60–68%; coir ~73–80% of volume | Coir holds most | A091 | Strong |

> **AFP is necessary but not sufficient.** Oxygen *diffusion rate*, not AFP alone,
> correlates most closely with growth — a coarse, open mix beats one that merely tests at
> 12% AFP [A113](../../claim_table.html#claim-a113). Worked porosity examples that all clear the ≥40% water / ≥10% air minimum:
> UC Mix 11%/62%/73%, peat+perlite 20%/73%/93%, peat+vermiculite 13%/81%/94% (air/water/total)
> [A112](../../claim_table.html#claim-a112).

**The oxygen-physics floor (why roots refuse the wet zone).** Modelling of flooded roots
[E113–E116, *extrapolated*] shows why the saturated band is dead rooting space:
- Maximum root length rises with internal porosity (~107 mm at 10% → ~293 mm at 30%, diminishing past ~30%) [E113](../../claim_table.html#claim-e113).
- Oxygen demand is concentrated at the **growing tip** (apical respiration 3.8 vs 1.5 basal nmol O₂·g⁻¹·s⁻¹) — the front is starved first when the medium goes anoxic [E115](../../claim_table.html#claim-e115).
- Thick roots self-asphyxiate fastest: internal pO₂ fell from 20.6 kPa at the surface to 0.3 kPa at the core of a ~1900 µm root [E116](../../claim_table.html#claim-e116).

The takeaway: keep the *whole* profile aerated, because the perched zone will simply not be
colonized by healthy roots.

## 3.3 The Perched Water Table — and the Drainage-Layer Myth (strong, peer-reviewed)

A saturated "perched" band sits at the **bottom of the fine medium regardless of what is
beneath it** [E103](../../claim_table.html#claim-e103). The widespread "gravel/pot-shard drainage layer" advice is
counterproductive:

> ⚠️ **Do not add a bottom drainage layer.** In a coir-perlite-bark mix, *every* drainage-layer
> condition gave significantly **lower** water-holding capacity overall (p < 0.001) — i.e., a
> layer makes the media drier on average but **lifts the perched saturated band higher into
> the pot, shortening aerated rooting depth** [E102](../../claim_table.html#claim-e102), [E103](../../claim_table.html#claim-e103). Thicker layers (60 mm) reduced
> retention more than thin (30 mm), and the effect is medium-dependent and unpredictable
> [E104](../../claim_table.html#claim-e104). **The fix is a uniform coarse mix top-to-bottom**, not a layered profile.

**Drainage hardware (all strong/standard):**

| Practice | Rule | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Drainage holes | **Mandatory** — non-negotiable in a shallow pot | B090 | Strong |
| Elevate the pot | Raise **≥½ in** off the surface so holes clear standing water | A127 | Strong |
| Fabric sidewalls | Breathable walls drain/aerate the whole profile, shrinking the perched zone and raising effective AFP | B116 | Moderate |
| Particle grade | Coarse/chunky (#3) grade drains far better; fines create the soggy perched zone | D051 | Weak |
| Self-watering (SIP) | **Not recommended as primary system** — a coarse bark/pumice citrus mix wicks poorly; SIP goals conflict with citrus drainage goals; blocked overflow → perpetual saturation, hypoxia, Phytophthora | E639, E649, E637 | Weak |

## 3.4 Media Stability Over Time (the slow drift to hypoxia)

Organic components decompose into fines that hold excess water and **reduce AFP, drifting
the profile toward the hypoxia zone** [A030](../../claim_table.html#claim-a030). Concretely:
- Aging pine bark (≤1 → ≥6 months) raised container capacity **+21.4%** and cut air space
  **−17.2%** as coarse particles shift to fines [A044](../../claim_table.html#claim-a044).
- Media degrades and **sinks after ~5–6 years**; the tree settles into the pot [B145](../../claim_table.html#claim-b145).
- Topdressing **does not** fix a degraded lower profile — the perched zone concentrates
  fines and salt and needs periodic *deeper* replacement [C111](../../claim_table.html#claim-c111) (home: §6).

**Design for durability:** durable, slow-to-degrade components extend the interval —
pine/fir bark + pumice, a dwarfing rootstock, and a light/shaded pot [B141](../../claim_table.html#claim-b141), [A031](../../claim_table.html#claim-a031). Pumice
in particular is heavier, stays mixed, does not float or migrate, and outlasts perlite
(which floats up and crushes) over a multi-year tree [D562](../../claim_table.html#claim-d562), [D563](../../claim_table.html#claim-d563), [D565](../../claim_table.html#claim-d565). Limit fines and
start coarse and partially aged [A046](../../claim_table.html#claim-a046), [A045](../../claim_table.html#claim-a045) (ingredient choices: §6).

## 3.5 Root-Zone Temperature (targets here; protection is §15)

The root zone's *thermal* engineering belongs to the same shallow reservoir. Targets:

| Threshold | Value | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Optimum root-growth soil temp | **~26°C (79°F)**; most active 25–30°C (77–86°F) | A018 | Strong |
| Functional range | ~12–35°C (54–95°F); falls off sharply at both ends | A020 | Strong |
| Minimum for root elongation | **~12°C (54°F)** — below this growth stops | A019 | Strong |
| Chronic-heat ceiling | >34°C (93°F) sustained is harmful before acute injury; 40°C/6 h suppressed growth | A006, A005 | Strong |
| Acute lethal (20-min) | 51.6°C Carrizo / 52.5°C sour orange / 53.5°C Swingle (~125–128°F) | A004 | Strong |

A sun-facing pot is the problem: a black pot ran **50.3°C** vs a white pot **~36°C** (Δ~14°C)
in full sun [A036](../../claim_table.html#claim-a036), and dark pots run **+8.9–14.7°C** hotter at the root zone than light
ones [A037](../../claim_table.html#claim-a037). Pot color, shading, and double-potting are **heat-protection actions — home
§15**; here, simply note the root-zone *target* is ~26°C and the design should not let the
west-wall pot bake toward the >34°C chronic ceiling.

---

> ### 🪴 Worked example — 19″×14″ west-facing Brentwood / West LA balcony
> - **Pot class is right:** 19″×14″ ≈ 15-gal, the documented sweet spot [D088](../../claim_table.html#claim-d088), above the
>   12–15″ depth minimum [E032](../../claim_table.html#claim-e032). Treat it as a multi-year home with root-prune-and-refresh,
>   not a way-station to a bigger pot [B086](../../claim_table.html#claim-b086), [D064](../../claim_table.html#claim-d064).
> - **Engineer the mix coarse and uniform.** Because a 14″ pot is shallow, its effective
>   AFP is lower than bench values [A114](../../claim_table.html#claim-a114), [A115](../../claim_table.html#claim-a115); design the bulk mix toward **~20% AFP**
>   [E631](../../claim_table.html#claim-e631) with coarse bark + pumice so the wet bottom band stays above the **~10–12%
>   hypoxia floor** [B011](../../claim_table.html#claim-b011), [B012](../../claim_table.html#claim-b012). Recipe in §6.
> - **No drainage layer.** A gravel/shard layer would *raise* the perched band into this
>   already-shallow pot [E102](../../claim_table.html#claim-e102), [E103](../../claim_table.html#claim-e103). Use one uniform coarse mix, **elevate the pot ≥½″**
>   [A127](../../claim_table.html#claim-a127), and keep drainage holes clear [B090](../../claim_table.html#claim-b090).
> - **Marine-layer humidity + west-wall heat:** a fabric pot or light-colored/shaded pot
>   both helps aeration [B116](../../claim_table.html#claim-b116) and keeps the root zone off the >34°C ceiling [A006](../../claim_table.html#claim-a006) (heat
>   tactics: §15). Skip self-watering containers — they wick poorly with a citrus mix and
>   risk a permanently saturated base [E639](../../claim_table.html#claim-e639), [E649](../../claim_table.html#claim-e649).
> - **Plan for drift:** expect AFP to fall and the mix to sink over ~5–6 yr [A044](../../claim_table.html#claim-a044), [B145](../../claim_table.html#claim-b145);
>   refresh media at each root-prune (§6).

## Key takeaways
- The binding constraint is **bottom-of-pot oxygen**; citrus can't avoid hypoxia, only
  react to it [B062](../../claim_table.html#claim-b062) — prevent it by design.
- Target **AFP ≥10–25%** generally [A109](../../claim_table.html#claim-a109), but **design this shallow pot to ~15–20%**
  [E631](../../claim_table.html#claim-e631) so the wet base stays above the **~10–12% hypoxia floor** [B011](../../claim_table.html#claim-b011), [B012](../../claim_table.html#claim-b012); keep
  **total porosity ≥50%** [A111](../../claim_table.html#claim-a111).
- **No drainage layer** — it lifts the perched water band higher and shortens aerated depth
  (p<0.001) [E102](../../claim_table.html#claim-e102), [E103](../../claim_table.html#claim-e103). Use a uniform coarse mix; elevate the pot ≥½″ [A127](../../claim_table.html#claim-a127), [E103](../../claim_table.html#claim-e103).
- The 19″×14″ pot (~15-gal) is correctly sized [D088](../../claim_table.html#claim-d088); step up only +2–5″ at a time [D064](../../claim_table.html#claim-d064).
- Root-zone optimum is **~26°C** [A018](../../claim_table.html#claim-a018); keep the west-wall pot off the **>34°C** chronic
  ceiling [A006](../../claim_table.html#claim-a006) (actions in §15).

## Things not to do
- **Don't add gravel, shards, or a "drainage layer"** at the bottom [E102](../../claim_table.html#claim-e102).
- **Don't over-pot** to a much larger container — the excess mix stays saturated and rots
  roots [D064](../../claim_table.html#claim-d064).
- **Don't use a fine, water-retentive or wetting-agent indoor potting mix** — it keeps the
  base waterlogged [C010](../../claim_table.html#claim-c010), [D051](../../claim_table.html#claim-d051) (see §6).
- **Don't run a self-watering/SIP setup as the primary system** for citrus [E639](../../claim_table.html#claim-e639), [E649](../../claim_table.html#claim-e649).
- **Don't expect topdressing to rescue a degraded lower profile** — it won't [C111](../../claim_table.html#claim-c111).

## Evidence gaps
- **Almost no citrus-specific porosity/AFP targets.** The 10–25% / 15–20% AFP figures are
  general container science extrapolated to citrus [A109](../../claim_table.html#claim-a109), [E631](../../claim_table.html#claim-e631); the only citrus substrate
  trial is the 2026 biochar study [E696](../../claim_table.html#claim-e696) (substrate amendments are detailed in §6).
- **Hypoxia thresholds (10–12% AFP) are fruit-tree-general, not Lisbon** [B011–B013].
- **Oxygen-diffusion root-length figures are modelling on non-citrus roots** [E113–E116].
- **No measured AFP for a real Lisbon 14″ pot** — the height correction is inferred from
  the general height rule [A114](../../claim_table.html#claim-a114), [A115](../../claim_table.html#claim-a115), not from a pour-through/AFP reading on this pot
  (testing protocol: §14).
