# 16. Environmental and Pest Protection

> **Evidence callout.** **Strong** — almost entirely UC IPM, CDFA, and extension, much of it
> lemon/California-specific [E646](#claim-e646), [E064](#claim-e064), [D110](#claim-d110). This section is the **home for *management*** —
> the IPM tactics, biocontrol, spray rules, and quarantine compliance; **diagnosis/identification
> is §13** (cross-referenced, not repeated). The governing principle throughout: **a single
> balcony pot is an IPM-friendly system** — biological control and physical/cultural tactics do
> most of the work, and broad-spectrum sprays do more harm than good.

The master rule for this site comes straight from the corpus: **broad-spectrum insecticides
flare spider mites and break biological control** [E082](#claim-e082), [E090](#claim-e090). The default posture is therefore
**conserve natural enemies, control ants, use selective/contact materials only when needed, and
spray nothing at bloom**. Layered on top is one non-negotiable: **ACP/HLB vigilance and
quarantine compliance**, because that is the one pest that ends the tree.

## 16.1 The IPM Hierarchy (apply in this order)

| Tier | Action | Why | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Conserve natural enemies** | They control aphids (~6 wk), whiteflies, mites, CLM unaided | E069, E088, E079 | Strong |
| 2 | **Control ants** | Ants farm/protect honeydew pests and **break biological control** — highest-leverage action | E646 | Strong |
| 3 | **Physical / cultural** | Hosing, hand-removal, barriers, skirt-pruning, open canopy for airflow | E070(§13), E667, E059 | Strong |
| 4 | **Selective/contact materials** | Oils, soaps, Bt, iron-phosphate, spinosad — only when warranted | D110, E093, E095 | Strong |
| 5 | **Broad-spectrum insecticide** | **Avoid** — flares mites, kills beneficials | E082, E090 | Strong |

## 16.2 ACP / HLB — The Existential Priority (strong; California-specific)

Diagnosis is §13 (asymmetric mottle; ~45° psyllid; waxy nymph tubules). **Management/compliance:**

| Action | Specification | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Scout every new flush** | ACP targets tender flush year-round — Lisbon's multi-flush habit means continuous scouting | E064, E121 | Strong |
| Treat ACP | Systemic/foliar insecticides, **horticultural oils**, timed to flush; encourage **Tamarixia radiata**; don't disrupt natural enemies | E064 | Strong |
| **Never move the tree** | All LA County under ACP quarantine since 2009; HLB present across LA-area counties; **do not move citrus/budwood out of the quarantine area** | D054, D055, D057, D008 | Strong |
| **Report suspected HLB** | HLB is incurable and fatal; **CDFA 1-800-491-1899**; CDFA inspects/removes; 5-mile quarantine on confirmation | D058, D056 | Strong |
| Buy clean | CCPP-certified budwood from a reputable nursery (insect-proof increase) — don't import ACP/HLB | E044(§5), E045 | Strong |

> ⚠️ **This is the one pest that kills the tree and is reportable.** Everything else on this list
> is manageable; HLB is not curable [D058](#claim-d058). Scout flush, keep the tree put, buy certified.

## 16.3 Ant Control — The Keystone Tactic (strong)

| Method | Specification | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Trunk/pot barriers** | Sticky bands (e.g., Tanglefoot) + **skirt-pruning** to block the ant bridge to the canopy — easy on an isolated pot | E647 | Strong |
| **Baits (preferred chemical)** | Ants carry slow-acting bait to the colony; **boric-acid liquid bait stations** are an organic option, refilled often | E648 | Strong |
| When | Monitor in spring; if sooty mold/scale appears, **stop the ants first** | E648 | Strong |

Controlling ants restores the biological control that suppresses scale, mealybug, aphid, and
whitefly [E646](#claim-e646) — which is why it sits above spraying.

## 16.4 Selective Materials and Spray Rules (strong)

| Material | Targets | Critical rules | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Horticultural / narrow-range oil** | Soft-bodied pests, scale crawlers, mealybugs, mite/whitefly nymphs; some disease (greasy spot, powdery mildew); dormant smother | **Do NOT spray near/above ~90°F or on drought-stressed plants** (burns foliage); apply **dawn/dusk** (bee + burn safety) | D110, D113, D111, D112 | Strong |
| Insecticidal soap | Same soft-bodied pests, short residual | Thorough coverage incl. undersides; spare natural enemies | D110 | Strong |
| **Neem oil** | Soft-bodied pests | Practically non-toxic to birds/mammals/(dry, off-bee-hours) bees | D114 | Strong |
| **Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis)** | Caterpillars (orangedog) | Spares bees/birds/beneficials | E095 | Strong |
| Iron-phosphate bait | Snails | Reduces numbers (decollate snail biocontrol is killed by other baits) | E067 | Strong |
| Spinetoram/spinosad/abamectin | Thrips (if ever needed) | Spare natural enemies; avoid broad-spectrum that flares mites | E086 | Strong |

> ⚠️ **The west-wall oil trap:** the most useful home material (horticultural oil) is **phytotoxic
> above ~90°F** [D111](#claim-d111) — exactly when a west-facing balcony is hottest. Spray oils **early morning
> on a cool day**, never into a heat wave (§15).

## 16.5 Pest-by-Pest Management (diagnosis → §13)

| Pest | Management | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Spider mites** (likely dominant, hot wall) | **Hose foliage incl. undersides**, reduce dust/water stress; miticide only if severe (no formal threshold); **never broad-spectrum** (flares them) | E082, E081 | Strong |
| **Bud/silver/rust mite** (coastal lemon) | Usually held by predators/weather; oil or wettable sulfur **only if distortion severe** (mind heat/oil cautions) | E074 | Strong |
| **CLM** | **Tolerate, let parasitoids work**; don't prune off mined leaves (still photosynthesize, host parasitoids); closer attention only while young | E079 | Strong |
| Aphids | Hose off; natural enemies clear in ~6 wk; insecticide rarely warranted | E069 | Strong |
| **Scale / mealybug** | Narrow-range **oil timed to crawlers**; control ants; keep open canopy for airflow | E093 | Strong |
| Whitefly | Biological control usually sufficient; **don't spray** (imidacloprid/pyrethroids/carbaryl flare it + mites); wash sooty mold, control ants | E090 | Strong |
| Thrips | Low priority (cosmetic ring-scar); monitor twice weekly petal fall→1.5″ fruit; selective material only | E086, E085(§13) | Strong |
| **Snails** | **Copper band on the pot** (1–2 ft up, ~8″ overlap) repels for years; skirt-prune the bridge; hand-remove at night; iron-phosphate bait | E666, E667 | Strong |
| **Orangedog** | **Hand-pick** (acute on small canopy); **Bt** for heavier pressure; tolerate light feeding (adult is a desirable native butterfly) | E095 | Strong |

## 16.6 Disease Management (diagnosis → §13)

| Disease | Management | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Phytophthora** | **Resistant rootstock (trifoliate Flying Dragon, §2) + drainage/irrigation management**; fungicides (fosetyl-Al/Aliette, K-phosphonate, mefenoxam) only for confirmed problems | A118 | Strong |
| Brown rot / gummosis | **Skirt low branches** off the pot rim (remove splash zone); keep graft dry/above line (§5); deadwood removal | A022(§13), A099, A119(§5) | Strong |
| **Greasy spot** | Improve air circulation; **water at soil early so leaves dry** (not overhead/late); remove fallen leaves; copper only if persistent | E076 | Strong |
| Sooty mold | **Treat the honeydew pest + ants**; wash off mold | E089(§13), E646 | Strong |
| Mal secco | **None needed in California — not present** [B048/§13] | B048 | Strong |

**Mycorrhizae (AMF)** added at potting/refresh can **suppress Phytophthora** and improve root
activity [B030](#claim-b030) (§6) — a low-risk cultural defense.

## 16.7 Environmental Protection (cross-refs)

The "environmental" half overlaps the weather sections: **heat/cold/sun protection is §15**
(shade, whitewash, frost cloth, light pot), **wind/tip-over is §8**, **drainage/waterlogging is
§3/§11**. The pest-relevant environmental levers that live here: **skirt-pruning + open canopy**
(airflow cuts fungal disease and blocks ant/snail bridges) [A024](#claim-a024), [E667](#claim-e667), **mulch off the trunk**
(no crown rot) [C086](#claim-c086), and **early soil-level watering** (dry leaves → less greasy spot) [E076](#claim-e076).

---

> ### 🪴 Worked example — 19″×14″ west-facing Brentwood / West LA balcony
> - **HLB first:** scout **every flush** for ACP [E064](#claim-e064), **never move the pot** out of the LA
>   quarantine area [D057](#claim-d057), buy **CCPP-certified** [E045](#claim-e045), and keep **CDFA 1-800-491-1899** handy
>   [D058](#claim-d058). This is the only pest that ends the tree.
> - **Control ants** with a sticky band on the pot/trunk + boric-acid bait [E647](#claim-e647), [E648](#claim-e648) — it does
>   more for scale/mealybug/whitefly than any spray [E646](#claim-e646).
> - **Likely chronic here:** spider mites (hot wall) → **hose undersides**, never broad-spectrum
>   [E082](#claim-e082); scale/mealybug (sheltered) → **oil timed to crawlers**, but **only on a cool morning
>   <90°F** [E093](#claim-e093), [D111](#claim-d111); coastal **bud/silver mite** (mostly tolerate) [E074](#claim-e074); **snails** →
>   **copper band around the pot** [E666](#claim-e666).
> - **Orangedog:** check the small canopy and **hand-pick** — a few larvae can strip it [E095](#claim-e095).
> - **Greasy spot risk from the marine layer** → water at the soil **early** so leaves dry [E076](#claim-e076).
> - **Phytophthora:** you've already prevented it with Flying Dragon + drainage (§2/§3); no
>   routine fungicide [A118](#claim-a118).
> - **Spray nothing at bloom** — protect the ~44% bee bonus (§9) [C043](#claim-c043).

## Key takeaways
- **Conserve natural enemies and control ants first** — ants breaking biocontrol is the
  highest-leverage problem; baits + a pot/trunk sticky band fix it [E646](#claim-e646), [E647](#claim-e647), [E648](#claim-e648).
- **ACP/HLB is the existential priority:** scout every flush, **never move the tree**, buy
  certified, **report HLB to CDFA 1-800-491-1899** [E064](#claim-e064), [D057](#claim-d057), [D058](#claim-d058).
- **Avoid broad-spectrum insecticides** — they flare mites and break biological control [E082](#claim-e082), [E090](#claim-e090).
- **Oils/soaps are the workhorse selective tools** — but **never above ~90°F** or on stressed
  plants; spray dawn/dusk [D110](#claim-d110), [D111](#claim-d111), [D112](#claim-d112).
- **Likely balcony pests:** spider mites (hose), scale/mealybug (oil to crawlers), bud/silver
  mite (tolerate), snails (copper band), orangedog (hand-pick) [E082](#claim-e082), [E093](#claim-e093), [E074](#claim-e074), [E666](#claim-e666), [E095](#claim-e095).
- **Phytophthora is prevented, not sprayed** — rootstock + drainage [A118](#claim-a118); **greasy spot** →
  early soil-level watering [E076](#claim-e076).

## Things not to do
- **Don't move the potted tree out of the ACP/HLB quarantine area** [D057](#claim-d057).
- **Don't spray broad-spectrum insecticide** (imidacloprid/pyrethroid/carbaryl) — flares mites
  and whitefly, kills beneficials [E090](#claim-e090), [E082](#claim-e082).
- **Don't apply oil/soap above ~90°F or on a drought-stressed tree** — foliage burn [D111](#claim-d111).
- **Don't spray anything at bloom** — protect pollinators (§9) [D112](#claim-d112), [C043](#claim-c043).
- **Don't routinely fungicide** for Phytophthora — prevent with rootstock + drainage [A118](#claim-a118).
- **Don't ignore ants** — they sustain the honeydew-pest complex [E646](#claim-e646).

## Evidence gaps
- **Management is citrus-general UC IPM**, not container-Lisbon-trialed (though directly
  applicable) — no balcony-specific efficacy data.
- **No formal action thresholds** for the key home pests (spider mite, thrips) — judge by
  damage/trend [E081](#claim-e081).
- **Quarantine specifics evolve** — the CDFA boundaries/contacts [D054–D058] are as of 2024 and
  should be re-verified before moving any plant material.
- **Greasy-spot pressure for this microclimate is weakly characterized** [E075/§13].
