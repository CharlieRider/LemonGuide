# 6. Container Media and Topdress Systems

> **Evidence callout.** Component *behavior* (bark stability, peat/coir water-holding and pH,
> wetting agents, pH amendment rates) is **strong** container science [A031, A091, A092, A041,
> A042]. The specific *recipes*, however, are **moderate-to-weak** nursery/consumer formulas
> [C009, B088, D050, D107] that converge on the same bark-dominant structure rather than one
> validated mix. The one citrus-specific substrate trial is the 2026 biochar study [E696].
> This section is the **home** for the media recipe, amendments, mulch, and topdress; the
> *targets and physics* those serve (AFP ~15–20%, no drainage layer, perched water) are §3;
> pH/EC *targets* and the nutrition program are §10; salt flushing is §14.

A container Lisbon's mix must do four things at once: **drain and aerate** (§3), **resist
collapse into fines** for years, **hold enough water** that a shallow west-wall pot doesn't
dry in hours, and **rewet easily** after it does. The answer across every source is the same
skeleton: **durable bark for structure + a minority of fine organic for water/CEC + a
permanent mineral aggregate for aeration.**

## 6.1 Component Functions (strong container science)

| Component | Role | Key properties | Watch-out | Claims | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Aged pine/fir bark** (backbone) | Coarse structure, drainage, slow decay | Stable months–years; "large-container" mixes 50–60% coarse bark | Fines (20–30%) cause waterlogging; cellulose barks (alder/poplar/oak) decay fast, immobilize N | A031, A069, A045, A108(media) | Strong |
| **Peat** | Water/CEC | WHC 60–68%; pH **3.0–4.5** (needs liming) | Hydrophobic when dry — needs wetting agent | A091, A092, A067 | Strong |
| **Coir** | Water/CEC, rewets easily | WHC 73–80%; pH **5.8–6.9** (no liming); far less hydrophobic than peat | Raw coir = Na/Cl/K-loaded, Ca/Mg-poor — must be buffered; keep **<50%** | A091, A092, A093, A094, A095 | Strong / Moderate |
| **Pumice** (preferred aggregate) | Permanent aeration | pH 7.0–7.4; heavy, stays mixed, doesn't float/break down | Heavy (balcony weight) | D121, D564, D565 | Weak |
| **Perlite** | Aeration, lightweight | pH 6.5–7.5; excellent aeration | **Floats up and crushes** over time | D122, D563 | Weak |
| **Calcined clay** (Turface) | Aeration + water reservoir | ~74% internal porosity; ~26% solids/39% water/35% air; durable, pH-neutral, modest CEC | Heavy | E055, E056, E057 | Moderate |
| **Compost / worm castings** | Slow N, micros, microbes, **CEC** | Buffers nutrient swings in low-CEC mix | **Keep ≤10%** or it clogs aeration | D109, C106 | Weak / Moderate |
| **Biochar** (optional) | Root growth, OM, CEC, moisture | **20% v/v optimum** raised citrus root length 25–39% | Raises pH (watch Fe/Mn/Zn lockout) | E696, E698, E110 | Strong (citrus) |

## 6.2 Base Recipes (moderate-to-weak; they converge)

All four published formulas are **bark-dominant with a minority fine fraction and a mineral
aggregate** — pick one and adjust:

| Recipe | Composition | Source | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **5-1-1** | ~5 bark : 1 citrus/cactus mix : 1 aeration | Four Winds (CA nursery, climate-matched) | C009 | Moderate |
| **4:1** | 4 aged pine bark : 1 peat/coir | Clemson HGIC | B088 | Strong |
| **50/30/20** | 50% bark fines : 30% coir(or peat) : 20% coarse perlite/pumice | Biology Insights | D050 | Weak |
| **40/30/20/10** | 40% pine bark : 30% potting soil/coir : 20% perlite/pumice : 10% compost/castings | Growfruitly | D107 | Weak |

> **Practical synthesis (not a new number — a selection).** Use a **bark-dominant mix
> (~50–60% coarse aged pine/fir bark)** [A069] with **~20–30% buffered coir** [B138, A095],
> **~20% pumice** for permanent aeration [D564], and **≤10% compost/castings** for CEC [D109].
> This matches the convergent recipes above while satisfying the §3 physics target (push AFP
> toward ~15–20% in a shallow pot). Substitute **calcined clay or 20% biochar** for part of
> the aggregate if balcony weight and pH are managed [E057, E696].

**Worked porosity reference (for sanity-checking a mix):** UC Mix 11%/62%/73%, peat+perlite
20%/73%/93%, peat+vermiculite 13%/81%/94% (air/water/total) — all clear the ≥10% air / ≥40%
water minimum [A112] (full physics: §3).

**Avoid:** standard indoor potting mixes with chemical wetting agents that hold water around
roots too long [C010]; garden soil; cactus/palm mixes drain better than standard potting mix
but are not a complete substitute [A128/§3].

## 6.3 Coir Pre-Treatment (do this or pay for it later)

Raw coir's exchange sites are loaded with **Na⁺/K⁺ and depleted in Ca²⁺/Mg²⁺**; unbuffered,
it **dumps Na/K and locks out Ca/Mg** [B135] — a real risk given hard LA water.

| Step | Specification | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rinse | Fresh-water rinse (coastal-grown coir carries high salt) | B138 | Moderate |
| Buffer | Soak saturated coir in a **Ca/Mg solution 8–24 h** to displace native Na/K | B136 | Moderate |
| Targets | Coir **pH 5.5–6.5**, media **EC < 1.0 mS/cm** before planting | B137 | Moderate |
| Fraction | Use **~20–30% coir** in the bark/pumice mix | B138 | Moderate |

## 6.4 Wetting Behaviour — Keep the Mix Rewettable (strong, extrapolated)

A west-wall pot *will* dry hard; the mix must come back on one watering:
- At 25% moisture, **peat with no wetting agent failed to reach container capacity even after
  10 irrigations** [A052]; medium/high wetting agent restored it in ~3 events [A052].
- **Perlite rewet fully in one event**; coir rewets far more easily than dried peat [A054,
  A093].

**Implication:** favor a bark/pumice/coir mix (rewets well); if peat is used, it **must**
carry a wetting agent. (Rewetting tactics under heat: §11/§15.)

## 6.5 pH and Ca/Mg Amendments (strong; targets are §10/§14)

| Goal | Amendment | Rate / property | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Lower pH** | 90% sulfur prills | **~1 tsp / 1-gal container ≈ −1 pH unit** (1–2 lb/yd³) | A041 | Strong |
| **Raise pH** | Dolomitic lime | **~1 Tbsp / gal** to buffer raw bark (calcitic faster than dolomitic; fine reacts faster) | A042, A070 | Strong |
| Ca + S, **no pH change** | Gypsum (CaSO₄·2H₂O) | 22% Ca, 18% S, water-soluble — ideal at pH 6.0–6.5; also displaces Na for flushing | C028, C029, C030 | Moderate |
| Ca (+N) | Calcium nitrate | Adds N + Ca | D049 | Moderate |
| Mg, no pH change | Epsom salt (MgSO₄) | Mg without pH shift | D049 | Moderate |
| Mg + raise pH | Dolomitic lime | Mg while raising pH | D049 | Moderate |

> ⚠️ **Why this matters for hard LA water:** Ca/Mg are easily leached from free-draining pots
> and specifically depleted by unbuffered coir [D489]. But alkaline tap water pushes pH *up*,
> risking Fe/Mn/Zn lockout — so prefer **gypsum (Ca/S, no pH rise)** over lime when pH is
> already ≥6.0–6.5 [C029]. pH targets and the nutrition program are the home of §10.

## 6.6 Mulch (strong, extension-sourced)

| Parameter | Specification | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Evaporation cut | **~33% (up to ~50%)** reduction in soil-water loss | C087 | Strong |
| Layer thickness | **1–1.5 in (2.5–4 cm)** — thinner than a garden bed | C089 | Strong |
| Materials | Coarse pine bark, wood chips, or coco; thin compost top-dress trickles a little NPK | C090 | Strong |
| Co-benefits | Shades/cools upper root zone; insulating air gap; reduces soil splash (less foliar disease) | C082, C088, C091 | Moderate / Strong |
| **Keep off the trunk/graft** | Avoid crown rot | C086, E023 | Moderate / Weak |

Mulch also serves heat protection (§15) and irrigation efficiency (§11).

## 6.7 Topdress and Refresh Systems (weak but consistent)

Container mix inevitably degrades — organic matter is consumed, **salts build up**, the
surface compacts where water enters [E052].

| System | Method | Cadence | Limit | Claims | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Topdress** | Scrape top **1–2 in** old mix, replace with fresh (~1 castings : 2 compost) | ~Every 2 yr, start of growing season | **Does not remove salts**; doesn't fix the lower profile | E053, E054, C111 | Weak |
| **Topdressed CRF** | Lay controlled-release fertilizer on the surface, don't incorporate | Per product | Topdressed CRF leached **less N** than incorporated | A090 | Strong |
| **Partial replacement** | Replace outer shell + topdress at each root-prune | ~Every 2–3 yr (§5) | Preserves feeder roots | C109 | Weak |
| **Full (bare-root) replacement** | Reset all media | Only for degraded/salty mix or disease | Stressful | C110 | Weak |

> ⚠️ **Topdressing is not salt management.** It refreshes the surface and trickles nutrients
> but **cannot flush accumulated salt or repair the perched-zone fines** [E054, C111]; pair it
> with deep leaching (§14) and the periodic deeper media replacement at root-prune (§5).

**AMF inoculant:** citrus has few, short root hairs and **relies heavily on mycorrhizae**
[B027]; AMF improve uptake of the less-mobile, lockout-prone nutrients (P, Zn, Cu) [B028],
boost growth especially under moderate water stress [B029], and can suppress Phytophthora
[B030] — add at potting/refresh. **Caveat:** high soluble-P fertilizer suppresses AMF
colonization [B031], so don't over-apply soluble P if relying on it.

---

> ### 🪴 Worked example — 19″×14″ west-facing Brentwood / West LA balcony
> - **Mix:** ~50–60% coarse aged pine/fir bark + ~20–30% **buffered** coir + ~20% pumice +
>   ≤10% compost [A069, B138, D564, D109] — bark-dominant for multi-year drainage, coir for
>   easy rewetting on a fast-drying wall [A093], pumice (not perlite) so aeration doesn't
>   float away [D565]. Optionally swap part of the aggregate for **20% biochar** [E696]
>   (watch the pH rise [E110]).
> - **Buffer the coir** (Ca/Mg soak, rinse) before planting [B136, B138] — non-negotiable with
>   hard LA water and a salt-sensitive Flying Dragon rootstock (§2).
> - **pH lever:** alkaline tap water drifts pH up; keep Ca up with **gypsum** (no pH rise)
>   rather than lime [C029]; if pH climbs, **~1 tsp 90% sulfur per gallon ≈ −1 unit** [A041]
>   (targets: §10/§14).
> - **Mulch 1–1.5 in** coarse bark, off the trunk [C089, C086] — cuts evaporation ~33% [C087]
>   on the hot wall (§15).
> - **Refresh cadence:** topdress between cycles, but do the real work — partial media
>   replacement + **deep salt flush** — at the 2–3-yr root-prune [C109, §5, §14]; topdress
>   alone won't remove LA-water salt [E054].

---

## Key takeaways
- Use a **bark-dominant mix** (~50–60% coarse aged pine/fir bark) with a **minority** of
  buffered coir, **pumice** aggregate, and **≤10% compost** [A069, D109, D564] — all four
  published recipes converge on this [C009, B088, D050, D107].
- **Buffer coir** (Ca/Mg soak) or it dumps Na/K and locks out Ca/Mg [B135, B136]; keep coir
  **<50%** [A095].
- Keep the mix **rewettable** — dried peat without a wetting agent may never recover [A052];
  bark/pumice/coir rewet well [A054, A093].
- Adjust pH with **sulfur (−1 unit per tsp/gal)** [A041] or lime [A042]; supply **Ca via
  gypsum** when pH is already ≥6.0 (no pH rise) [C029].
- **Mulch 1–1.5 in**, off the trunk — cuts evaporation ~33% [C087, C089, C086].
- **Topdress refreshes but does not flush salt** [E054, C111]; do real refresh + leaching at
  root-prune (§5/§14).

## Things not to do
- **Don't use unbuffered coir**, or coir as the majority fraction [B135, A095].
- **Don't use a fine, wetting-agent indoor potting mix or garden soil** [C010].
- **Don't over-incorporate fertilizer** — topdressed CRF leaches less N than incorporated
  [A090].
- **Don't pile mulch against the trunk/graft** — crown rot [C086].
- **Don't rely on topdressing to control salt** — it can't [E054].
- **Don't over-apply soluble P** if relying on mycorrhizae [B031].

## Evidence gaps
- **No validated container-Lisbon recipe** — the four formulas are nursery/consumer
  convergence, not a measured comparison [C009, B088, D050, D107].
- **Biochar data is citrus-general greenhouse** (rootstock seedlings), not a Lisbon
  fruiting-pot trial, and it raises pH [E696, E110].
- **Calcined clay specs are manufacturer (label-grade)** [E055–E057]; durability/weight not
  independently verified for this use.
- **Topdress/refresh cadence is weak consumer sourcing** [E053, E054]; the "~2 yr" and
  Ca/Mg-buffer times [B136] are rules of thumb, not Lisbon-tested.
