# 12. Harvest Maturity and Fruit Quality

> **Evidence callout.** Lisbon harvest *timing and cues* are **weakly sourced** (consumer/
> nursery: Specialty Produce, Wikifarmer, Four Winds) [C047](#claim-c047), [C048](#claim-c048), [E012](#claim-e012), though corroborated
> by **strong** UCR for the winter-crop pattern [D026](#claim-d026). The fruit-*quality* physiology
> (non-climacteric behavior, light→sugar, deficit/girdling effects) is **strong/moderate**
> general-citrus and lemon science [C049](#claim-c049), [A097](#claim-a097), [E126](#claim-e126). Yield expectations for a 14″ pot are
> **extrapolated** [D037](#claim-d037), [E123](#claim-e123). This section is the **home** for harvest and fruit quality;
> bloom→fruit timeline detail is §4, the nutrition that drives quality is §10, frost damage to
> fruit is §15.

The single most important harvest fact: **lemons are non-climacteric — they do not ripen
significantly off the tree** [C049](#claim-c049). There is no "pick early and ripen on the counter." Instead,
Lisbon's great convenience is that **fruit holds well on the tree and can be picked
progressively as needed** [C050](#claim-c050), [E014](#claim-e014) — the tree is its own storage.

## 12.1 Maturity Timeline (Lisbon / lemon)

| Marker | Value | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bloom → harvest | **≈6–9 months** (range 4–13 by cultivar/climate) | E007, C016, D035 | Weak / Moderate |
| Main crop | **Winter / early spring** | D026, E012, C133 | Strong / Weak |
| California harvest peak | **~January–February**; picked late fall → early spring | E013 | Weak |
| Rind color progression | Green → yellow as fruit reaches **~3–4 in**; full color/maturity last | E009 | Weak |
| First bearing | Grafted **~2–3 yr** (dwarf as early as yr 2); most citrus 2–5 yr | E010, D080 | Weak / Moderate |
| Mild-climate pattern | A scattered "dribble" of fruit year-round on top of the main crop | C133, D026 | Weak / Strong |

Fruiting wood forms on **last summer's growth and the inner/protected canopy** — so heavy
late-spring/summer cuts destroy the coming crop [C102](#claim-c102) (pruning timing: §7).

| Cue | Indicator | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Size | Full size for the variety | C048 | Weak |
| Color | Bright/deep yellow, glossy skin | C048 | Weak |

> **Practical:** because the fruit waits on the tree [C050](#claim-c050), there is no rush at any single
> date — pick a few at a time across the winter window as each reaches full color and weight.

## 12.4 Fruit Quality — What You Can Influence (strong/moderate)

| Lever | Effect | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Light into the canopy** | **Shaded interior fruit have lower soluble solids (sugar)** — fruit draw sugar from nearest leaves; open the interior for light | A097 | Strong |
| **Adequate nutrition** | Lemon quality is **nutrition-responsive** — adequate NPK raised acidity, TSS, vitamin C (benchmarks: acidity ~6.25%, TSS ~9.45%, vit C ~4.27 mg/100 g in a fed Eureka study) | C092 (§10) | Strong |
| **Mild water deficit** (field) | Improved TSS, vitamin C, proline, storage life — **but** see §11 caveat (don't deficit a 14″ pot) | A028 | Strong |
| **Girdling (summer)** | Increases fruit size and internal quality (higher sugars, color, firmness) | E126 | Moderate |
| **GA₃** | Improves fruit size, peel smoothness, rind color/firmness; delays rind senescence | A105 | Strong |
| Climate | Warmer/humid climates → larger, juicier, higher-acid, more peel oil; **coastal LA is moderate** (good, not tropical-max) | D036 | Weak |
| **Stress (negative)** | Reduced fruit size is an early drought sign; stress at flowering → drop; stress in sizing → small fruit | B018, A027 | Strong |

> **Tie-ins:** the "open interior for sugar" lever [A097](#claim-a097) is the same open-center/dense-exterior
> pruning of §7; quality-by-nutrition is §10; the deficit and girdling cautions are §11 and §9.
> For a balcony, the safe quality levers are **light + steady complete nutrition**, not deficit
> irrigation or girdling.

| Condition | Holding | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Room temperature | **~1–2 weeks** | C045 | Weak |
| Refrigerated | **up to ~1 month** | C045 | Weak |
| Zest / juice | Freeze for longer | C045 | Weak |

Because the tree stores the crop [C050](#claim-c050), on-tree holding is usually better than picking early
to store — pick as you use them.

## 12.6 Cold and Fruit (cross-ref §15)

Citrus **fruit freezes at sustained 26–28°F** (4 h at 26°F ≈ 30% damage in oranges) [D010](#claim-d010) —
relevant because the winter main crop coincides with West-LA's rare frost window. Protect
hanging fruit during a freeze event (tactics: §15).

---

> ### 🪴 Worked example — 19″×14″ west-facing Brentwood / West LA balcony
> - **Expect the main crop in winter/early spring**, peaking ~Jan–Feb [E013](#claim-e013), [D026](#claim-d026), plus a
>   light year-round dribble in the mild climate [C133](#claim-c133) — and **a few dozen fruit/yr** once
>   mature [E123](#claim-e123).
> - **Don't pick early** — lemons won't ripen off the tree [C049](#claim-c049); **hold on the tree and pick
>   progressively** when deep-yellow, glossy, and heavy with slight give [C048](#claim-c048), [C050](#claim-c050).
> - **For sweeter/better fruit, get light into the canopy** [A097](#claim-a097) (open-center pruning, §7) and
>   **feed steadily and completely** [C092](#claim-c092) (§10) — the safe levers. Skip deficit irrigation
>   [A028 vs §11] and girdling [E126 vs §9] as quality tricks in a 14″ pot.
> - **Protect hanging winter fruit from a hard frost** (sustained 26–28°F damages fruit [D010](#claim-d010))
>   — easy with a movable pot (§15).
> - **Coastal-LA quality is "good, not tropical-max"** [D036](#claim-d036) — realistic, juicy, high-acid
>   Lisbon fruit.

## Key takeaways
- **Lemons are non-climacteric** — don't pick early; they don't ripen off the tree [C049](#claim-c049).
- **Hold fruit on the tree, pick progressively** across the winter/early-spring main crop
  (CA peak ~Jan–Feb) [C050](#claim-c050), [E013](#claim-e013), [D026](#claim-d026).
- **Ripeness cues:** full size, deep-yellow glossy, heavy with slight give [C048](#claim-c048).
- **Realistic pot yield: a few dozen fruit/yr** — field per-tree figures don't apply [D037](#claim-d037), [D034](#claim-d034).
- **Quality levers that are safe in a pot:** light into the canopy (sugar) [A097](#claim-a097) + steady
  complete nutrition [C092](#claim-c092); deficit/girdling are field tricks, not balcony ones (§9, §11).
- **Protect winter fruit from frost** (26–28°F damages it) [D010](#claim-d010) (§15).

## Things not to do
- **Don't pick lemons early** to ripen indoors — they won't [C049](#claim-c049).
- **Don't expect orchard yields** from a 14″ pot [D037](#claim-d037), [D034](#claim-d034).
- **Don't make heavy late-spring/summer cuts** — they remove the coming crop's fruiting wood
  [C102](#claim-c102) (§7).
- **Don't deficit-irrigate or girdle a 14″ pot** for "quality" — the reservoir is too small and
  the tree too small (§11, §9).
- **Don't leave fruit unprotected during a hard frost** [D010](#claim-d010) (§15).

## Evidence gaps
- **No legal/quantified maturity standard** (e.g., juice-content or TSS:acid harvest index) for
  Lisbon in the corpus — ripeness cues are qualitative/consumer-sourced [C048](#claim-c048).
- **Harvest timing and yield are weak/extrapolated** [E012](#claim-e012), [D037](#claim-d037); "a few dozen fruit/yr" is an
  inference, not a measured container-Lisbon figure [E123](#claim-e123).
- **Quality benchmarks (TSS/acid/vit C) are from a fed Eureka NPK study** [C092](#claim-c092), not
  Lisbon-container-measured.
- **Girdling/deficit quality gains are field data** [E126](#claim-e126), [A028](#claim-a028), explicitly not recommended for
  this pot.
