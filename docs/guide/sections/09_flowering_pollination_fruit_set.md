# 9. Flowering, Pollination, and Fruit Set

> **Evidence callout.** This section is **strong** and unusually **Lisbon-specific**: the
> self-fertility and parthenocarpy figures are peer-reviewed (ISHS Acta Hort.) [C040](../../claim_table.html#claim-c040), [C041](../../claim_table.html#claim-c041),
> induction thresholds are from extension and flowering reviews [B041](../../claim_table.html#claim-b041), [B091](../../claim_table.html#claim-b091), [B092](../../claim_table.html#claim-b092), and the
> girdling and pollinator data are peer-reviewed [E124](../../claim_table.html#claim-e124), [C043](../../claim_table.html#claim-c043). This section is the **home** for
> the *mechanisms and tactics* of bloom and set; the *seasonal timeline* and natural drop waves
> are §4 (restated here), irrigation at bloom is §11, nutrition is §10, and heat/cold protection
> of flowers is §15.

The headline for a balcony grower is the best news in the whole guide: **Lisbon is self-fertile
and the most parthenocarpic of the common lemons (~20% fruit set with no pollination at all)**
[C040](../../claim_table.html#claim-c040), [C041](../../claim_table.html#claim-c041), [B148](../../claim_table.html#claim-b148). A single isolated pot will crop, and it can set *some* fruit even when heat
wrecks the pollen. The binding constraint on set is therefore **not pollination — it is
carbohydrate** [C037](../../claim_table.html#claim-c037): fewer than **2% of flowers** ever become harvestable fruit [B045](../../claim_table.html#claim-b045), [C037](../../claim_table.html#claim-c037),
and the survivors are decided by carbohydrate competition among fruitlets, not by pollen.

## 9.1 Reproductive Biology (Lisbon-specific, strong)

| Trait | Value | Implication | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Self-fertility | Self-fertile — **no pollinizer needed** | One lone pot crops | C040, C018 | Strong / Moderate |
| **Parthenocarpy** | **~20% fruit set with no pollination** — greatest of common lemons | Sets fruit even if heat ruins pollen | C041, B148 | Strong / Weak |
| Self-pollination set | 20–40% when self-pollinated; cross-pollination gave similar rates | Cross-pollination adds little | C042 | Strong |
| Bee access | Open (insect-accessible) flowers set **~44% more** fruit than closed | Bees boost yield, not required | C043 | Strong |
| Seeds | Near-seedless fruit from self/parthenocarpic set | No pollinizer needed for usable fruit | C134 | Weak |

> **So what?** Cross-pollination and a second tree buy almost nothing here [C042](../../claim_table.html#claim-c042); **encouraging
> bee access at bloom buys ~44%** [C043](../../claim_table.html#claim-c043). Parthenocarpy is the insurance policy against a
> bloom-time heat wave that damages pollen [B097](../../claim_table.html#claim-b097), [B148](../../claim_table.html#claim-b148).

## 9.2 Floral Induction (summary; timeline home is §4)

Induction *precedes* visible bloom by **2–3 months** and is triggered by cold **or** drought
[A010](../../claim_table.html#claim-a010), [B093](../../claim_table.html#claim-b093):

| Trigger | Threshold | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cold** | **15°C (59°F) for ≥1.5 months** flowers; 25°C for 7–9 mo stays vegetative; cold range 59–68°F | B091, B041, A010 | Strong |
| **Water deficit** | Leaf Ψ ≈ **−2 MPa for 40–60 days**, then rewater | B092, A012 | Strong |
| Partial deficit suffices | **50% of crop demand** induced as well as zero irrigation, without severe damage | B043 | Strong |
| Resumption | Floral development resumes **7–10 days** after deficit ends (up to ~4 wk) | B044 | Strong |
| **Lisbon carbohydrate note** | Starch/glucose showed **no consistent correlation** with flowering intensity *in Lisbon* | B096 | Strong |

> ⚠️ **Do not force bloom by drying a 14″ pot.** The drought-induction thresholds are field-scale
> (leaf Ψ −2 MPa for weeks) [B092](../../claim_table.html#claim-b092); a shallow pot's tiny reservoir hits damaging stress in 1–2
> days, and stress *at bloom* raises flower/fruit drop and cuts yield [B055/§4, A061]. The mild
> West-LA climate supplies cold induction [B041](../../claim_table.html#claim-b041); rely on the normal watering rhythm, not
> deliberate drought [A013](../../claim_table.html#claim-a013) (§11).

## 9.3 Fruit Set Is Carbohydrate-Limited (strong)

| Fact | Value | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Flower-to-fruit conversion | **<2%** | B045, C037 | Strong |
| Limiting factor | **Carbohydrate competition** among fruitlets + auxin/ethylene abscission — *not* pollination | C037 | Moderate |
| Post-bloom drop causes | Poor pollination, **water/temperature stress, inadequate N, carbohydrate limitation** | A061 | Strong |
| Heat on pollen | High temperature reduces pollen viability and pollen-tube growth → lower set/yield (pollen among most heat-sensitive processes) | B097 | Strong |

Because the limit is carbohydrate and abscission, the productive levers are the ones that
protect carbohydrate supply and avoid stress at bloom — covered next.

## 9.4 Levers to Improve Set (safest first)

| Lever | Effect | Notes | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Avoid stress at bloom/set** | Prevents drop | Keep water and N steady; don't let the reservoir dry at bloom | A061, E128 | Strong / Moderate |
| **Balanced nutrition** | Supports set | Steady feeding (§10) | E128 | Moderate |
| **Pollinator access** | **+~44% set** | Let bees reach open flowers at bloom | C043 | Strong |
| **Girdling (scoring) at petal fall / end of bloom** | Raises carbohydrate availability → markedly improves set; **works even in parthenocarpic cultivars** | Reported yield gains **+73% to +400%** | E124, E125 | Moderate |
| GA₃ at **full bloom** | Supports flower-bud development, reduces premature drop, improves set | **Timing-sensitive** — see warning | A104 | Strong |
| GA₃ + 2,4-D fall sprays | Cut fruit drop ~25% | PGR program | A106 | Strong |

> ⚠️ **PGR timing flips the effect — and PGRs are optional for Lisbon.** GA₃ *improves* set when
> sprayed at full bloom [A104](../../claim_table.html#claim-a104) but **suppresses flowering when applied during the inductive
> period** [A107](../../claim_table.html#claim-a107), [B047](../../claim_table.html#claim-b047); 25 mg/L GA₃ cut sweet-orange flowering 45–76% [B095](../../claim_table.html#claim-b095). If maximizing
> bloom, **avoid GA-containing products in fall/winter** [B047](../../claim_table.html#claim-b047). Because Lisbon is self-fertile
> and parthenocarpic, **PGRs are optional, not necessary** [A108/§1] — start with the cultural
> levers (no stress, bees, nutrition), then girdling, before reaching for hormones.

> ⚠️ **Girdling caveat:** girdling is a real wound on a small tree — peer-reviewed for orchards
> [E124](../../claim_table.html#claim-e124), not validated on a container Lisbon. Treat the +73–400% as field figures; use it
> cautiously and only after the safe levers.

## 9.5 Alternate Bearing (general citrus; softened in lemon)

| Mechanism | Detail | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pattern | Heavy ON year → sparse OFF year, chiefly from **reduced flowering after a heavy crop** | A047 | Strong |
| Cause | Carbohydrate reserves inversely related to crop load; heavy set leaves less wood to flower next year | A048 | Strong |
| Mechanism | Heavy crop **suppresses the summer/fall vegetative shoots that carry next year's bloom** | A049 | Strong |
| Management | **Thin excessive set, feed steadily, protect late-season flushes** | A050 | Strong |
| Lemon note | Lemon's **everbearing habit softens** alternate bearing | A050, E011 | Strong / Weak |

## 9.6 Natural Drop (restated from §4)

Heavy bloom and heavy drop are normal and self-thinning: post-bloom drop, **"June drop" ~1–2
months after bloom**, and pre-harvest drop thin the crop to canopy capacity (§4). With <2%
conversion [B045](../../claim_table.html#claim-b045), do not mistake normal drop for failure.

---

> ### 🪴 Worked example — 19″×14″ west-facing Brentwood / West LA balcony
> - **One pot is enough.** Lisbon self-fertility + ~20% parthenocarpy [C040](../../claim_table.html#claim-c040), [C041](../../claim_table.html#claim-c041) means no
>   second tree and a crop even if a heat wave damages pollen [B148](../../claim_table.html#claim-b148), [B097](../../claim_table.html#claim-b097).
> - **The mild marine climate supplies cold induction** (winter min near 15°C) [B041](../../claim_table.html#claim-b041), [B091](../../claim_table.html#claim-b091) — you
>   don't need to drought-force bloom, and you *shouldn't* in a 14″ pot [B092 caveat].
> - **Protect set, don't force it:** keep water and N **steady through bloom and the fruit-sizing
>   window** [A061](../../claim_table.html#claim-a061), [E128](../../claim_table.html#claim-e128) — the #1 controllable cause of drop is stress at bloom (§11, §10).
> - **Let the bees in** for ~44% more set [C043](../../claim_table.html#claim-c043) — don't spray insecticide at bloom (§16).
> - **Skip PGRs by default** [A108](../../claim_table.html#claim-a108); if pushing yield, **girdle at petal fall** cautiously
>   [E124](../../claim_table.html#claim-e124) before reaching for GA₃ — and never use GA products in fall/winter or you'll suppress
>   next bloom [B047](../../claim_table.html#claim-b047).
> - **Expect heavy drop** (<2% set [B045](../../claim_table.html#claim-b045)) and a **few dozen fruit/yr** at maturity (§4, §12).

## Key takeaways
- **Lisbon is self-fertile and the most parthenocarpic lemon (~20% set, no pollination)** [C040](../../claim_table.html#claim-c040), [C041](../../claim_table.html#claim-c041) — a lone pot crops, and parthenocarpy insures against heat-damaged pollen [B148](../../claim_table.html#claim-b148), [B097](../../claim_table.html#claim-b097).
- **Set is carbohydrate-limited, not pollination-limited** — <2% of flowers fruit [C037](../../claim_table.html#claim-c037), [B045](../../claim_table.html#claim-b045).
- **The mild climate supplies cold induction** (15°C ≥1.5 mo) [B091](../../claim_table.html#claim-b091); **do not drought-force
  bloom** in a shallow pot [B092 caveat].
- **Best levers, safest first:** no stress at bloom → balanced nutrition → **bee access (+44%)**
  → girdling (+73–400%, cautiously) [E128](../../claim_table.html#claim-e128), [C043](../../claim_table.html#claim-c043), [E124](../../claim_table.html#claim-e124).
- **PGRs are optional and timing-sensitive** — GA₃ helps at full bloom but suppresses flowering
  in the inductive period; avoid GA products in fall/winter [A104](../../claim_table.html#claim-a104), [A107](../../claim_table.html#claim-a107), [B047](../../claim_table.html#claim-b047).

## Things not to do
- **Don't add a second tree for pollination** — cross-pollination buys almost nothing [C042](../../claim_table.html#claim-c042).
- **Don't drought-stress the pot to force bloom** — field thresholds don't transfer; bloom-time
  stress drops fruit [B092](../../claim_table.html#claim-b092), [A061](../../claim_table.html#claim-a061).
- **Don't spray insecticide at bloom** — it costs the ~44% bee bonus [C043](../../claim_table.html#claim-c043) (§16).
- **Don't use GA-containing products in fall/winter** if you want bloom — they suppress induction
  [B047](../../claim_table.html#claim-b047), [B095](../../claim_table.html#claim-b095).
- **Don't panic at heavy drop** — <2% set is normal [B045](../../claim_table.html#claim-b045) (§4).

## Evidence gaps
- **The ~20% parthenocarpy figure is partly weakly/indirectly cited** [B148](../../claim_table.html#claim-b148) though corroborated
  by peer-reviewed ISHS [C041](../../claim_table.html#claim-c041); exact container-Lisbon set rates are unmeasured.
- **Girdling yield gains (+73–400%) are orchard field data** [E125](../../claim_table.html#claim-e125), untested on a container
  Lisbon — extrapolation flagged.
- **Induction thresholds are general citrus** [B091](../../claim_table.html#claim-b091), [B092](../../claim_table.html#claim-b092); how the specific West-LA marine
  microclimate drives Lisbon induction is uncharacterized, and **Lisbon shows no consistent
  carbohydrate–flowering correlation** [B096](../../claim_table.html#claim-b096), complicating prediction.
- **No container-Lisbon pollinator or PGR trial** — bee +44% [C043](../../claim_table.html#claim-c043) and GA₃ effects [A104](../../claim_table.html#claim-a104) are
  general lemon/citrus.
