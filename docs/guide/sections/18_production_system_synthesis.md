# 18. Container Lisbon Lemon Production System (Synthesis)

> **Evidence callout.** This section **synthesizes** the preceding 17 — it introduces almost no
> new claims; the integrating "production system" statements are a **weak** 2026 repository
> synthesis [E117–E123] resting on the stronger underlying claims cited throughout. Its job is to
> assemble the whole system for the **19″×14″ west-facing Brentwood pot** and to consolidate the
> **12 resolved conflicts** that recur across sections. Every number here has its evidentiary
> home in an earlier section (cited inline); this is the map, not new territory.

The container Lisbon system is the management of **four coupled constraints** that the rest of
the guide treats one at a time:

1. **Vigor** — Lisbon is the most vigorous CA lemon (§1); a 14″ pot fights it for life.
2. **A shallow, fast-swinging root zone** — small reservoir, perched water, root heat (§3).
3. **Hard alkaline water** — salt accumulation + Fe/Zn/Mn lockout (§10, §11, §14).
4. **An exposed west wall** — heat the frequent hazard, rare frost the fatal one (§15).

Every design choice below is an answer to one or more of these, and the choices are
**interlocking** — the dwarfing rootstock that solves vigor creates the salt-sensitivity that
forces the leaching discipline, which depends on the coarse mix that solves the root zone.

| Constraint | Design answer | Key numbers | Home |

## 18.2 The Twelve Resolved Conflicts (consolidated)

Every cross-section tension and its adopted resolution for this pot:

| # | Tension | Resolution adopted | Sections |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Open canopy for light **vs** keep dense for sunburn | **Light selective interior thinning only**; favor sunburn protection on the west wall; whitewash exposed bark | §7, §15 |
| 2 | Water every 3 days **vs** ~daily | **Weather/exposure-driven, by pot weight** — ~daily in heat, every 2–3 d in cool marine spells | §11 |
| 3 | Shade cloth 30–50% **vs** flat 50% | **30–50%**, leaning 50% on west exposure >95–100°F | §15 |
| 4 | Repot 2–3 yr **vs** 3–5 yr | **~2–3 yr** for a 19″ pot on dwarfing rootstock | §5 |
| 5 | Salt-tolerant stock **vs** size-controlling stock | **Prioritize dwarfing (Flying Dragon)**; manage salt by leaching, not rootstock | §2, §11 |
| 6 | Light pot cools roots **vs** white wall bounces heat onto canopy | **Light-colored pot**, avoid a highly reflective wall behind canopy; shade cloth handles reflected load | §15, §3 |
| 7 | pH 5.5–6.5 (Lisbon) **vs** 6.0–7.0 (citrus) | **Target ~6.0–6.5**; at the high end use Fe-EDDHA, don't fight pH | §10, §14 |
| 8 | Carrizo/Troyer as rootstock | **Do not use** for Lisbon (Eureka-type incompatibility + salt/pH sensitivity) — Flying Dragon/C-35 | §2 |
| 9 | Mal secco as a threat | **Not in California** — exclude as a local threat (biosecurity note only) | §13 |
| 10 | Greasy spot: water late **vs** morning | **Water in the morning** so leaves dry fast | §11, §16 |
| 11 | Biochar 20% optimum **vs** 10–15% cap | **Cap 10–15%** given alkaline LA water (pH-rise risk) | §6 |
| 12 | Field N/micro rates (lb/acre, lb/tree) | **Do not transfer to a pot** — labeled product + downscale, flagged | §10 |

> The two **structural** resolutions that shape everything else are **#5** (take dwarfing, manage
> salt) and **#1** (open interior but keep the sun shield) — the rest are calibration.

## 18.3 Cause-and-Effect Chains (why the choices interlock)

- **Dwarfing → salt-sensitivity → leaching:** Flying Dragon solves vigor [B101](#claim-b101) but is
  salt-sensitive [C124](#claim-c124) → on hard water you *must* leach 15–30% + monthly flush [B069](#claim-b069), [C127](#claim-c127) and
  monitor leachate EC [D134](#claim-d134) → which only works in a free-draining coarse mix [C129](#claim-c129).
- **Shallow pot → perched water + root heat → coarse mix + cool pot:** the 14″ depth raises the
  saturated zone and bakes the root zone [A114](#claim-a114), [A036](#claim-a036) → coarse bark/pumice at ~20% AFP [E631](#claim-e631) +
  light/shaded/double pot [A039](#claim-a039), [C083](#claim-c083) keeps the base above the 10–12% hypoxia floor [B011](#claim-b011) and
  below the >34–38°C root ceiling [A006](#claim-a006), [A038](#claim-a038).
- **Dwarf root scavenges poorly + pot leaches fast → feed weekly-weakly:** [B104](#claim-b104), [B142](#claim-b142) → small
  frequent complete feeds, not heavy infrequent ones [A072](#claim-a072), [E099](#claim-e099).
- **West wall = light (good) + heat (bad):** clears the ≥6 h sun bar [A131](#claim-a131) but pushes heat → the
  same wall's reradiated warmth is the frost defense [D012](#claim-d012), managed seasonally with shade vs
  cover.

## 18.4 Realistic Expectations (Lisbon-specific synthesis)

| Outcome | Expectation | Claim |
|---|---|---|
| Variety fit | **Strong** — most climate-versatile common lemon, zone 10b–11a, self-fertile, high-acid fruit | E117 |
| Yield | **A few dozen fruit/yr** at maturity, winter main crop | E123 |
| Lifespan | **Indefinite in the 19″ pot** *if* the 2–3-yr cycle is kept; otherwise 5–7-yr decline | E122, B139 |

> **The honest bottom line:** a 19″×14″ west-facing Brentwood balcony is a **strong fit** for
> Lisbon [E117](#claim-e117), yielding a few dozen fruit a year for many years — **conditional on the
> maintenance discipline** (leach, feed weekly-weakly, 2–3-yr root-prune, scout for ACP). The
> climate is forgiving; the pot is not.

---

> ### 🪴 Worked example — the complete spec, 19″×14″ west-facing Brentwood / West LA balcony
> **Tree:** CCPP-certified **Lisbon 'Yen Ben' on Flying Dragon**, budded (not own-root). [§1, §2, §5]
> **Pot:** ~15-gal-class **light-colored rigid** pot, elevated ≥½″, off the concrete, optionally
> double-potted; no drainage layer. [§3, §15, §17]
> **Mix:** ~50–60% coarse aged pine/fir bark + 20–30% buffered coir + ~20% pumice + ≤10% compost;
> ~15–20% AFP; pH amended to 6.0–6.5 (gypsum for Ca). [§6, §3]
> **Water:** by pot weight (~daily summer, less in marine spells), early morning, deep to a
> **15–30% leaching fraction**; **monthly deep flush**; never a SIP. [§11]
> **Feed:** weekly-weakly acid-forming citrus fertilizer + micros, Feb–Oct; **Fe-EDDHA** for
> chlorosis; ease off in winter. [§10]
> **Prune:** open interior / dense exterior, thinning cuts, after winter harvest; pull rootstock
> suckers. [§7]
> **Protect:** 30–50% afternoon shade >95–100°F; frost cloth + pre-water + move pot at mid-20s °F.
> [§15]
> **Pests:** control ants, conserve enemies, **scout every flush for ACP**, never move the tree,
> report HLB (CDFA 1-800-491-1899). [§16]
> **Cycle:** late-winter root-prune (shave 1–2″) + media refresh + deep salt flush **every 2–3 yr**
> → holds the tree indefinitely. [§5, §17]
> **Expect:** a few dozen fruit/yr, winter main crop; one self-fertile pot, no pollenizer. [§9, §12]

## Key takeaways
- The system manages **four coupled constraints** — vigor, shallow root zone, hard water, west
  wall — and the design choices **interlock** (dwarfing → salt mgmt → coarse mix → leaching).
- The **two structural resolutions:** take **dwarfing and manage salt** (#5), and **open the
  interior but keep the sun shield** (#1).
- **Variety fit is strong** [E117](#claim-e117); expect **a few dozen fruit/yr** [E123](#claim-e123) and **indefinite life
  in the pot** *if* the **2–3-yr prevention cycle** is kept [E122](#claim-e122), [B139](#claim-b139).
- **The climate is forgiving; the pot is not** — the maintenance discipline is the whole game.

## Things not to do
- **Don't break the interlock** — e.g., taking Flying Dragon (for size) then skipping leaching
  (its salt sensitivity) guarantees decline [C124](#claim-c124), [B139](#claim-b139).
- **Don't substitute a vigorous salt-tolerant rootstock** to dodge leaching — it defeats the pot
  (#5) [C124](#claim-c124).
- **Don't treat this as a low-maintenance plant** — it needs more frequent water/feed/attention
  than in-ground [D093](#claim-d093).
- **Don't let any single conflict resolution slip** — they were chosen for *this* pot (§18.2).
