# 5. Transplanting and Establishment

> **Evidence callout.** Tree-quality and disease-certification advice is **strong**
> (UCR CCPP) and Lisbon-specific [E044](#claim-e044), [E047](#claim-e047); the "don't bury the graft union" rule is
> **strong** general citrus [A119](#claim-a119). Most repotting/root-pruning technique, however, is
> **weak** (consumer-horticulture, widely repeated) [C019](#claim-c019), [D066](#claim-d066), [D076](#claim-d076) — directionally
> reliable but not from controlled trials. The repot-*interval* and repot-*timing* sources
> mildly conflict and are reconciled below. This section is the **home** for the
> transplant/repot/root-prune cycle and root-bound correction; canopy pruning is §7, media
> recipe is §6, container sizing is §3, sun acclimation overlaps §15.

Establishment has two distinct events: the **one-time planting** from the nursery, and the
**recurring root-prune-and-refresh cycle** that holds a vigorous Lisbon in a fixed pot for
years. Both hinge on the same rule: citrus tolerates root disturbance **in dormancy**, when
roots and shoots are not flushing, and re-establishes quickly as the active season opens
[C071](#claim-c071) (phenology: §4).

## 5.1 Selecting and Inspecting the Tree (Lisbon-specific, strong)

| Check | What to look for | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Disease certification | Budded from **CCPP-certified disease-free budwood** (registered budwood tested annually for CTV/HLB/vein enation, every 3 yr viroids, every 6 yr psorosis) | E044 | Strong |
| Graft union | **Clean, healed graft union above the soil line** | E047 | Strong |
| Roots | **No circling/girdling roots** — slide the tree out and check | E047 | Strong |
| Foliage/pests | No scale, sooty mold, or HLB-mottled leaves | E047 | Strong |
| Form | Sturdy, well-branched | E047 | Strong |

Rootstock/strain selection (Flying Dragon + Yen Ben) is the home of §1–§2; this is the
*physical* inspection at purchase.

## 5.2 Sun and Heat Acclimation (general container citrus — do this first)

A nursery tree grown under shade/greenhouse light **will sunburn and drop leaves if moved
abruptly into full sun** [C005](#claim-c005) — directly relevant to a hot west wall.

| Step | Specification | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ramp exposure | Start in bright shade / **1–2 h direct sun**, increase over **~2–3 weeks** | C006 | Moderate |
| Protect during adjustment | Shelter below **~40°F** or above **~85°F**; shade above **95–100°F** | C007 | Moderate |
| Expect leaf drop | Some leaf drop during transition is **normal**; new leaves emerge adapted to higher light | C008 | Moderate |

This acclimation overlaps heat/cold protection (§15); do it **before** committing the tree
to the west-facing position.

## 5.3 Planting / Repotting Technique (general + Lisbon)

| Step | Specification | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ease out root ball | Slide out; **tease/shave circling outer roots** | D066, D067 | Weak |
| Set depth | **Crown/graft union above the soil line** — never bury the graft | D066, A119, C112 | Weak / Strong |
| Backfill | Fresh **fast-draining** mix (§6); water in thoroughly to **eliminate air pockets** | D066, D072 | Weak |
| Root–canopy balance | **Match canopy pruning to root disturbance** | D068, C070 | Weak |
| Keep root ball intact (planting only) | For fastest recovery when *not* deliberately root-pruning, disturb minimally | D073 | Weak |

> ⚠️ **Burying the graft union invites crown/trunk gummosis (Phytophthora).** Keep the union
> and crown **dry and above the soil line**, and avoid splashing media onto the trunk [A119](#claim-a119)
> (disease detail: §13). Keep mulch off the trunk/graft too [C086](#claim-c086).

**Aftercare (Lisbon, weak but consistent):**
- Transplant shock lasts **~1–2 weeks**; some leaf drop/yellowing/wilting is normal recovery [D071](#claim-d071).
- **Hold fertilizer ~4–6 weeks**; keep out of harsh direct sun with stable temps ~1 week, then return to full light and feeding [D072](#claim-d072).

## 5.4 The Root-Prune-and-Refresh Cycle (the core of holding size)

For a tree **at target size**, the strategy is *not* endless up-potting — it is **root-prune
+ media refresh in the same pot** [B147](#claim-b147), [D067](#claim-d067), [E122](#claim-e122).

| Parameter | Specification | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Interval (this pot) | **~Every 2–3 years** (see conflict below) | B143, C019, E122 | Moderate / Weak |
| Timing | **Late winter / early spring, before the growth flush** (dormancy) | D065, C112, C071 | Weak |
| Amount (routine) | **Shave 1–2 in** of outer/circling roots all around | C020, C070, B140 | Weak / Moderate |
| Amount (severe root-bound) | Up to **~1/3 of root mass** is safe | D077 | Weak |
| Box cut (established mat) | Slice the outer circling layer on **4 sides + bottom** — most effective; shallow slicing does little | D076 | Weak |
| Pot after pruning | Return to the **same pot** (or ≤2 in larger if still growing) | C022, D067 | Weak |
| Pair with | Proportional canopy reduction (§7) + deep salt flush (§14) | D068, B140 | Weak / Moderate |

> ⚠️ **Conflict — repot interval (reconciled).** Sources give **2–3 yr** (UC MG / Simply
> Trees) vs **3–5 yr** (Clemson [B086](#claim-b086)); lemons are often cited at 3–4 yr [B143](#claim-b143). **Reconciliation
> (claim-table row 4):** interval scales with pot size and vigor — for a **19″ pot on a
> dwarfing rootstock, use ~2–3 yr** root-prune + refresh. Early in life, up-sizing every 1–2 yr
> is fine until the target pot is reached [D082](#claim-d082); thereafter hold by root-pruning [B147](#claim-b147).

> ⚠️ **Conflict — repot timing (reconciled).** "Late winter/early spring" [D065](#claim-d065) vs
> "spring–early summer" [D069](#claim-d069) vs "after harvest, before next bloom" [D070](#claim-d070). All three point
> to the **same window: dormant-to-early-active growth, and *not* during bloom or deep winter**.
> Repotting a tree about to bloom risks flower drop [D070](#claim-d070); winter repotting recovers slowly
> [D069](#claim-d069). **Use late winter/early spring** [D065](#claim-d065) for West LA, before the spring flush (§4).

## 5.5 Partial vs. Full Media Replacement (general container)

| Approach | What it does | When | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Top-dress** | Scrape top **1–2 in** of old mix, replace with fresh (~1 part castings : 2 parts compost) | Gentlest; ~every 2 yr; **does not remove salts** | E641, E642 | Weak |
| **Partial replacement** | Replace outer/old shell of mix + topdress at each root-prune | Routine — less stressful, preserves most feeder roots | C109 | Weak |
| **Full (bare-root) replacement** | Resets degraded media, flushes salt | **Reserve for degraded/salty mix or disease**; more stressful | C110 | Weak |

Top-dressing alone **cannot fix a degraded lower profile or remove accumulated salt** [E642](#claim-e642), [C111](#claim-c111) (perched-zone physics: §3) — combine with the deeper partial replacement at root-prune
and with salt flushing (§14).

## 5.6 Diagnosing and Correcting Root-Bound Trees (general container)

| Sign | Indicates | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Roots emerging from drainage holes; water runs straight through or pools on top; stunting; faster wilting | Root-bound — repot sooner | B144 | Moderate |
| Slide-out shows circling/girdling roots (esp. above soil line or wrapping trunk); solid root mat, little mix | Overdue | D074 | Weak |

Circling roots **won't straighten on their own** but **stop circling once cut/redirected**
[D075](#claim-d075); left uncorrected they progressively cut uptake and can strangle the trunk over years
[D078](#claim-d078), [B146](#claim-b146). Correct with the box cut [D076](#claim-d076).

## 5.7 Pot Type and Root Architecture (general container, mixed strength)

| Pot type | Root effect | Trade-off for a hot balcony | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Fabric / air-pruning** | Root tips desiccate and self-terminate at the wall → lateral branching, **no circling**; better drainage/aeration, shrinks perched zone | **Dries out fastest and runs cooler** — a liability on a hot balcony with a small reservoir | B115, B116, D086, B117 | Moderate / Strong |
| Copper-coated (CPC) / air-pruning (APC) | Produce fibrous root pruning at the soil–wall interface; standard nonpruning pots do not | Peer-reviewed nursery trial | D115, D556 | Moderate |
| Standard rigid pot | Allows circling; needs manual root-pruning | Holds moisture longer (asset in heat) | (implied) D074 | Weak |

**AMF inoculant:** adding arbuscular mycorrhizal fungi at potting/repotting can suppress
Phytophthora and improve fruit quality and root activity [B030, *extrapolated*] — a low-risk
addition at each refresh.

---

> ### 🪴 Worked example — 19″×14″ west-facing Brentwood / West LA balcony
> - **At purchase:** insist on a **CCPP-certified** Lisbon with a clean healed graft above the
>   soil line and no circling roots [E044](#claim-e044), [E047](#claim-e047); slide it out and look before you buy.
> - **Acclimate before committing to the west wall:** ramp from 1–2 h sun over ~2–3 weeks
>   [C006](#claim-c006); expect some leaf drop [C008](#claim-c008). This avoids day-one sunburn on the hottest exposure.
> - **Plant with the graft proud of the mix** [A119](#claim-a119), water in to kill air pockets, then
>   **hold feed ~4–6 weeks** and shade ~1 week [D072](#claim-d072).
> - **Hold size with a ~2–3-yr cycle** [B143, row 4]: late-winter root-prune (shave 1–2″, or
>   box-cut a mat), partial media refresh, deep salt flush (§14 — important with hard LA
>   water), proportional canopy prune (§7), back into the **same 19″ pot** [B147](#claim-b147), [D067](#claim-d067).
> - **Pot choice trade-off:** a fabric pot kills circling roots and aerates [B115](#claim-b115), [B116](#claim-b116) but
>   **dries fastest on a hot west wall** [B117](#claim-b117), [D086](#claim-d086) — on this exposure a light-colored rigid
>   pot (manual root-prune) may hold moisture better; if using fabric, plan more frequent
>   watering (§11) and shading (§15).

## Key takeaways
- Disturb roots **in late-winter dormancy** [D065](#claim-d065), [C071](#claim-c071); citrus recovers fast into the active
  season (§4).
- **Never bury the graft union** — keep it dry and above the line to avoid gummosis [A119](#claim-a119).
- Hold a fixed-size Lisbon by **root-prune + media refresh in the same pot every ~2–3 yr**
  [B143](#claim-b143), [B147](#claim-b147), not endless up-potting.
- Routine root-prune = **shave 1–2 in**; a severe mat tolerates removing **up to ~1/3** via a
  **box cut (4 sides + bottom)** [C020](#claim-c020), [D077](#claim-d077), [D076](#claim-d076).
- **Match canopy pruning to root removal** to keep root:canopy balance [D068](#claim-d068).
- Buy a **CCPP-certified** tree and **inspect roots before purchase** [E044](#claim-e044), [E047](#claim-e047).

## Things not to do
- **Don't bury the graft union** or splash media onto the trunk [A119](#claim-a119).
- **Don't repot during bloom** (flower drop) or in deep winter (slow recovery) [D070](#claim-d070), [D069](#claim-d069).
- **Don't jump to a much larger pot** — excess saturated mix rots roots [D064](#claim-d064) (§3).
- **Don't rely on top-dressing** to fix degraded lower mix or remove salts — it can't [E642](#claim-e642), [C111](#claim-c111).
- **Don't leave circling roots uncut** expecting them to straighten — box-cut them [D075](#claim-d075), [D076](#claim-d076).
- **Don't default to a fabric pot on a hot west wall** without planning for its faster
  drying [B117](#claim-b117), [D086](#claim-d086).

## Evidence gaps
- **Repotting/root-pruning technique is almost entirely weak consumer sourcing** [C019](#claim-c019), [D066](#claim-d066), [D076](#claim-d076); no controlled citrus trial defines the safe root-removal fraction for a dwarf Lisbon
  (the "1/3" and "1–2 in" figures are extension/consumer rules of thumb [D077](#claim-d077), [C020](#claim-c020)).
- **Repot interval and timing conflict mildly** [B143 vs B086; D065 vs D069]; reconciled by
  pot size/vigor and season, not by a measured Lisbon study.
- **AMF benefit is extrapolated**, not from a container-Lisbon trial [B030](#claim-b030).
- **No data on transplant-shock duration for a Lisbon on Flying Dragon specifically** — the
  ~1–2 wk figure is lemon-general [D071](#claim-d071).
