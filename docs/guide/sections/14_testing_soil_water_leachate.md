# 14. Soil, Media, Water, and Leachate Testing

> **Evidence callout.** **Strong** — IFAS/UC/VCE/OSU extension protocols for pour-through EC,
> leaf-tissue sampling, water-quality thresholds, and leaching-fraction measurement [D134, D139,
> B064, B068]. None is Lisbon-specific (all citrus-general). This section is the **home for
> *how to measure***; the *targets* those tests check against live in §10 (pH/EC/nutrition) and
> §11 (irrigation/leaching) and are restated here so the section stands alone.
> **Normalization note (central to this section):** the guide's pH/EC targets are expressed on
> the **home-pot pour-through basis** precisely so a grower's pour-through reading is directly
> comparable. The normalizer maps lab saturation/extract values to pour-through as **pH identity
> ± ~0.3 units** and **pour-through ≈ saturated-media-extract for EC** (Cavins et al. 2000, NCSU
> PourThru) [normalized claims D134, A040, B137]. Soil-extract or different-pot numbers are
> *not* directly comparable to your pot's pour-through — that conversion is what these targets
> already bake in.

Testing converts the whole guide from guesswork to measurement. For a salt-sensitive Flying
Dragon (§2) on hard alkaline LA water, the **two tests that earn their keep are pour-through EC
(catches salt before it burns) and pour-through pH (catches the Fe/Zn/Mn lockout drift)** —
both cheap, both repeatable on the actual 14″ pot.

---

## 14.1 Pour-Through Test — pH and EC (the workhorse)

The pour-through is the home-pot method the guide's targets are calibrated to (Cavins/NCSU)
[D134](#claim-d134). Principle: irrigate to container capacity, wait ~30–60 min, pour a known volume of
distilled water on top, collect the leachate, read pH and EC.

| Reading | Target (pour-through) | Action threshold | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **EC** | **< ~1.5 dS/m** (≈1000 ppm TDS) | **> ~2.0 dS/m → injurious**: reduce fertilizer concentration/frequency **and leach** | E002, D134, D138 | Strong |
| **pH** | **~6.0–6.5** | Drifting **>6.5–7** → Zn/Fe/Mn lockout: acidify (sulfur, §6) / switch to Fe-EDDHA (§10) | A040, B009(§10) | Strong |
| pH stability | — | pH is **dynamic** (irrigation alkalinity + fertilizer shift it) — **re-test, don't assume** | A043 | Strong |

> **Why pour-through, not a cheap soil probe:** the targets in §10 are on this basis; a probe
> reading a different basis isn't comparable. Combine the EC reading with the **visible salt
> cue** — a **white crust on the rim/surface** signals accumulation [C126](#claim-c126).

## 14.2 Leaching-Fraction (LF) Measurement (strong)

LF is how you *verify* the §11 leaching routine is actually flushing salt.

| Step                     | Method                                                                               | Claim | Strength |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----- | -------- |
| Definition               | **LF = water leached ÷ water applied**                                               | B070  | Strong   |
| Measure                  | Collect drainage on a warm sunny day (average ≥3 pots in a nursery; one pot at home) | B070  | Strong   |
| **Target LF**            | **0.15–0.30**; start ~0.5 while learning a new setup                                 | B068  | Strong   |
| **Scale LF to water EC** | EC <1.0 → LF ~0.1; **EC 1.5 → ~0.2; EC >2.0 → ~0.3**                                 | B069  | Strong   |
| Adjust irrigation time   | TA = t × (LFT − LFM)                                                                 | B071  | Strong   |
| Payoff                   | Targeting 10–20% LF cut one nursery's water use **43%**                              | B072  | Strong   |

## 14.3 Irrigation-Water Quality Test (strong — do this once for your tap)

Hard LA tap water is the upstream salt source; test it once and it explains most EC/pH drift.

| Analyte            | Threshold of concern                                                                                         | Claim | Strength |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----- | -------- |
| **Chloride (Cl⁻)** | Yield declines when irrigation **Cl > ~152 mg/L (4.3 meq/L)** — the key limiter, worse than Na               | B064  | Strong   |
| **Sodium (Na⁺)**   | Injury reported as low as **~115 ppm**; slow-building (held in roots/trunk, released to leaves after 3–4 yr) | B065  | Strong   |
| Boron (B)          | Toxic accumulation in leaves (with Cl/Na)                                                                    | B063  | Strong   |
| EC of water        | Drives the LF you must run (§14.2)                                                                           | B069  | Strong   |
| Mechanism          | Salts **ride the transpiration stream, concentrate as water evaporates**                                     | B066  | Strong   |

> A water report (from the utility or a $ test) gives Cl, Na, B, EC, alkalinity, and hardness —
> enough to set both the **LF** [B069](#claim-b069) and whether to pre-treat coir / lean on gypsum (§6).

## 14.4 Leaf-Tissue Test — the Nutrition Ground Truth (strong)

Leaf analysis is **more reliable than soil/media testing alone** for tree crops [E106](#claim-e106), and it
is how you separate a real deficiency from a pH-induced lockout.

| Protocol step               | Specification                                                                                             | Claim | Strength |
| --------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----- | -------- |
| What to sample              | **Mature, non-fruiting spring-flush leaves, 4–6 months old** (most stable); avoid immature leaves         | D139  | Strong   |
| How many                    | **20–30 leaves** per sampling area to average variation                                                   | D140  | Strong   |
| **Combine with media test** | Leaf + media/pH together separate a true deficiency from a lockout (adequate Fe but chlorotic at high pH) | D142  | Strong   |

**Sufficiency ranges (% dry weight)** — the values your lab report is read against (also §10):

| Nutrient | Sufficiency                        | Claim      |
| -------- | ---------------------------------- | ---------- |
| N        | **2.5–2.7%** (alt. study 1.9–2.3%) | D141, E105 |
| P        | 0.12–0.16%                         | D141       |
| K        | **1.2–1.7%** (deficient <0.7%)     | D141       |
| Ca       | 3.0–4.9% (alt. 2.1–2.8%)           | D141, E105 |
| Mg       | 0.30–0.49% (alt. 0.20–0.33%)       | D141, E105 |

## 14.5 Moisture Monitoring (home is §11 — summary)

| Method                       | Use                                                                             | Claim            | Strength |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------- | -------- |
| **Pot weight (gravimetric)** | Calibration reference + simplest single-pot gauge — light pot = water           | B059             | Strong   |

> ### 🪴 Worked example — 19″×14″ west-facing Brentwood / West LA balcony
> - **Run a pour-through EC + pH monthly** (more often in summer feeding): target **EC <1.5,
>   pH 6.0–6.5** [D134](#claim-d134), [A040](#claim-a040). EC creeping toward 2.0 → leach harder and dilute feed [D138](#claim-d138);
>   pH creeping >6.5–7 → sulfur / Fe-EDDHA (§6/§10). A **white rim crust** is the naked-eye cue
>   [C126](#claim-c126).
> - **Test the tap water once.** Brentwood water is hard/alkaline; getting **Cl, Na, B, EC,
>   alkalinity** sets your **leaching fraction** (likely toward **LF ~0.3** if EC >2.0 [B069](#claim-b069))
>   and confirms why gypsum + coir-buffering matter (§6).
> - **Verify the leach actually works:** measure **LF = leached ÷ applied** [B070](#claim-b070) on a warm
>   day — aim 0.15–0.30, higher for this hard water.
> - **Leaf-test once a year** (mature 4–6-month spring-flush leaves, 20–30 of them [D139](#claim-d139), [D140](#claim-d140))
>   and read against the sufficiency table; **pair with the pour-through pH** so a high-pH
>   lockout isn't misread as a deficiency [D142](#claim-d142).
> - **Heft the pot daily** as the moisture gauge (§11) [B059](#claim-b059).

## Key takeaways
- **Pour-through pH + EC is the workhorse test** — and the basis the guide's targets are
  calibrated to (pH 6.0–6.5, EC <1.5 dS/m) [D134](#claim-d134), [A040](#claim-a040), [E002](#claim-e002).
- **Measure LF = leached ÷ applied** to confirm leaching works; **scale LF to water EC** (up to
  ~0.3 for hard water) [B070](#claim-b070), [B069](#claim-b069).
- **Test the tap once** — Cl >152 mg/L and Na ~115 ppm are the thresholds of concern [B064](#claim-b064), [B065](#claim-b065).
- **Leaf-test annually** (mature 4–6-mo spring-flush leaves, 20–30) against the sufficiency
  ranges, **paired with media pH** to separate deficiency from lockout [D139](#claim-d139), [D141](#claim-d141), [D142](#claim-d142).
- **A white rim crust = salt accumulation** — leach [C126](#claim-c126); **pH is dynamic — re-test** [A043](#claim-a043).

## Things not to do
- **Don't compare a soil-extract or generic-probe reading to the guide's targets** — they're on
  the pour-through basis; convert or use pour-through [D134](#claim-d134).
- **Don't assume pH is stable** — alkaline water and fertilizer move it continuously [A043](#claim-a043).
- **Don't diagnose a deficiency from leaves alone** at high pH — confirm with media pH (lockout
  vs true shortage) [D142](#claim-d142).
- **Don't skip a one-time water-quality test** — it explains most EC/pH drift and sets the LF
  [B064](#claim-b064), [B069](#claim-b069).
- **Don't ignore a white rim crust** — it's visible salt; leach [C126](#claim-c126).

## Evidence gaps
- **All protocols are citrus-general extension**, not Lisbon- or this-pot-calibrated [D134](#claim-d134), [D139](#claim-d139).
- **Normalization carries ±0.3 pH and method-inferred EC confidence** [normalized claims]; a
  direct pour-through on the actual pot supersedes any converted figure.
- **Sufficiency ranges differ between sources** (e.g., N 2.5–2.7% vs 1.9–2.3%) [D141 vs E105] —
  treat as ranges, not precise cutoffs.
- **No container-Lisbon dataset** ties a specific pour-through EC/pH to a measured Lisbon
  response — the targets are inherited from general citrus.
