# 4. Phenology and Seasonal Growth Cycle

> **Evidence callout.** The *biological* phenology here is on a **strong** general-citrus
> footing (root-growth/temperature work, peer-reviewed flowering reviews, IFAS extension),
> but the **Lisbon-specific** flushing pattern and the bloom→harvest duration come from
> **moderate-to-weak** trade/consumer sources [C015](../../claim_table.html#claim-c015), [E007](../../claim_table.html#claim-e007) and are flagged. This section is
> the *home* for the phenological **timeline and growth cycle**; the *mechanisms and tactics*
> of fruit set are §9, fertilizer timing is §10, irrigation timing is §11, pruning timing is
> §7, and the month-by-month operating calendar is §19. Numbers here are restated so the
> section stands alone.

A container Lisbon does not have a single "season" — it is **everbearing**, carrying fruit
at several developmental stages at once [E011](../../claim_table.html#claim-e011), [E599](../../claim_table.html#claim-e599), with a heavy main bloom plus secondary
flushes. The practical consequence runs through the whole guide: **care cannot be switched
off seasonally** [E011](../../claim_table.html#claim-e011). Below ground, roots and shoots flush in *alternation* — the tree
partitions resources to tops **or** roots, not both at once [A009](../../claim_table.html#claim-a009) — so the calendar is
really two interleaved cycles.

---

## 4.1 The Two Interleaved Cycles (general citrus)

| Cycle | Pattern | Drivers | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Root growth** | Continuous **Feb–Nov** under non-limiting moisture; tracks soil temperature | Soil temp; moisture | A007 | Strong |
| Root temperature window | Active **~12–35°C**; optimum **~26°C**; stops <~12°C | Root-zone temp | A020, A018, A019 | Strong |
| **Shoot flushes** | Discrete flushes alternating with root flushes | Warm temp + moisture | A009 | Strong |
| Flush ↔ root trade-off | Tree allocates to shoots **or** roots, not simultaneously | Carbohydrate partitioning | A009 | Strong |

This matters for timing every intervention: a heavy shoot flush is *not* the moment roots
are extending, and vice versa. Root-disturbing work (repot, root-prune) is therefore timed
to **dormancy (late winter/early spring) before the growth flush** [D065](../../claim_table.html#claim-d065), [C071](../../claim_table.html#claim-c071) (home: §5),
so the tree re-establishes roots as the active season opens.

> expect a mild marine-influenced site to blur it toward year-round flushing [C017](../../claim_table.html#claim-c017).

## 4.3 Floral Induction → Bloom (summary; mechanism home is §9)

Induction *precedes* visible growth and can be triggered two ways:

| Trigger | Threshold | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cold** | **59–68°F (15–20°C)** in fall/winter | B041 | Strong |
| **Water deficit** | **~45–60 days** of moderate drought, then rewater (stress-then-release) | B042, A012 | Strong |
| Induction lead time | Occurs **2–3 months before bud break**; differentiation at bud release | B093 | Strong |
| Resumption after deficit | Floral development resumes **7–10 days** after the deficit ends (up to ~4 wk) | B044 | Strong |

> ⚠️ **Do not deliberately drought-stress a shallow pot to force bloom.** The drought-induction
> data is field-scale; a 14″ pot's tiny reservoir reaches damaging stress in 1–2 days, and
> water stress *at bloom* raises flower/fruit drop and cuts yield [B055](../../claim_table.html#claim-b055), [A062](../../claim_table.html#claim-a062). Note also
> 50% of crop demand induced as well as zero irrigation without severe damage [B043](../../claim_table.html#claim-b043) —
> over-stressing is unnecessary. Container bloom flushes are more safely nudged by the normal
> dry-down-then-water *rhythm* of routine watering [A013, *extrapolated*] (see §9, §11).

## 4.4 Bloom → Fruit → Harvest Timeline (general lemon / Lisbon)

| Stage | Duration / marker | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bloom → harvest | **≈6–9 months** (full range 5–13 mo by cultivar/climate/bloom) | E007 | Weak |
| Cell-expansion / rapid-growth phase | **~4–6 months** — the period of **highest water and nutrient demand** | E008 | Weak |
| Fruit sizing marker | Rind green → yellow as fruit reaches **~3–4 in**; full color/maturity last | E009 | Weak |
| First bearing | Grafted trees **~2–3 yr**; seedlings 5–6 yr | E010 | Weak |
| Realistic pot yield | **A few dozen fruit/yr** at maturity (19″ pot), winter main crop | E123 | Weak |

The rapid-growth phase [E008](../../claim_table.html#claim-e008) is the non-negotiable window for steady water and feeding —
stress here drops fruit and shrinks the survivors [B055](../../claim_table.html#claim-b055), [E008](../../claim_table.html#claim-e008). Detailed maturity indices
(juice, color, acid) are the home of **§12**.

## 4.5 Natural Fruit Drop (general citrus — expect it, don't panic)

Heavy bloom and heavy drop are normal: **<2% of citrus flowers become harvestable fruit**
[B045](../../claim_table.html#claim-b045). Drop comes in defined waves:

| Drop period | Timing | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Post-bloom drop | At/just after bloom | B032, C038 | Strong / Moderate |
| "June drop" | **~1–2 months after bloom** | B032 | Strong |
| Pre-harvest drop | Late season | B032 | Strong |

These two-to-three waves thin the crop to canopy capacity [C038](../../claim_table.html#claim-c038); causes include poor
pollination, water/temperature stress, inadequate N, and carbohydrate limitation [A061](../../claim_table.html#claim-a061).
The actionable levers (steady moisture/N at bloom, pollinator access, girdling) are §9.

## 4.6 Alternate Bearing and Tissue-Vulnerability Windows (general citrus)

- **Alternate bearing:** a heavy crop suppresses the summer/fall vegetative shoots that
  carry *next* year's bloom [A049](../../claim_table.html#claim-a049); manage by thinning excessive set, feeding steadily, and
  protecting late-season flushes [A050](../../claim_table.html#claim-a050). **Lemon's everbearing habit softens this** [A050](../../claim_table.html#claim-a050), [E011](../../claim_table.html#claim-e011).
- **Vulnerability follows tissue type, both directions.** Cold susceptibility order:
  **flowers > vegetative flush > fruit > hardened leaves > woody tissue** [C031](../../claim_table.html#claim-c031); heat
  sensitivity follows the same order (reproductive tissue most sensitive) [B099](../../claim_table.html#claim-b099). So the
  **bloom and tender-flush windows are the high-risk dates** for both frost and heat events
  (protection: §15) — a container can be moved or shaded then far more easily than an
  orchard tree [B149](../../claim_table.html#claim-b149).
- **Pest pressure tracks flushing:** continually flushing lemons sustain heavier citrus
  leafminer and aphid pressure, and every new flush is the scouting target for Asian citrus
  psyllid [E078](../../claim_table.html#claim-e078), [E069](../../claim_table.html#claim-e069), [E661](../../claim_table.html#claim-e661) (home: §16).

## 4.7 The Phenological Year (biological calendar; operational calendar = §19)

| Season (West-LA, approx.) | Above ground | Below ground | Watch-for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Late winter (Jan–Feb)** | Floral induction complete; main bloom beginning | Roots starting (~Feb) [A007](../../claim_table.html#claim-a007) | Frost risk on buds/flowers (most cold-sensitive) [C031](../../claim_table.html#claim-c031); repot window before flush [D065](../../claim_table.html#claim-d065) |
| **Spring (Mar–May)** | Main bloom → fruit set; heavy spring flush | Root flush active | Post-bloom drop [B032](../../claim_table.html#claim-b032); CLM/ACP on flush [E078](../../claim_table.html#claim-e078) |
| **Early summer (Jun)** | "June drop" thins crop [B032](../../claim_table.html#claim-b032) | Root growth continues | Don't mistake June drop for failure |
| **Summer (Jul–Aug)** | Secondary flush; fruit cell-expansion (high demand) [E008](../../claim_table.html#claim-e008) | Root growth (to Nov) | Heat events scorch sun-side fruit/flush [B099](../../claim_table.html#claim-b099), [B280](../../claim_table.html#claim-b280); steady water |
| **Autumn (Sep–Nov)** | Autumn flush; fruit coloring | Root growth tapering | Protect late flush (next year's bloom wood) [A049](../../claim_table.html#claim-a049) |
| **Winter (Dec)** | Winter main crop maturing/harvest | Roots ~stopping (<12°C) [A019](../../claim_table.html#claim-a019) | Cold induction 59–68°F sets next bloom [B041](../../claim_table.html#claim-b041); frost protection [C031](../../claim_table.html#claim-c031) |

> ### 🪴 Worked example — 19″×14″ west-facing Brentwood / West LA balcony
> - **Expect near-year-round flushing**, not a clean single season — the mild marine
>   climate pushes Lisbon toward continuous fruiting [C017](../../claim_table.html#claim-c017), so feeding and scouting run
>   ~Feb–Oct without a true "off" switch [E011, D089-cross-ref-§10].
> - **Plan a winter main crop** [D022](../../claim_table.html#claim-d022) of a **few dozen fruit/yr** once mature [E123](../../claim_table.html#claim-e123) — set
>   expectations: this is a balcony tree, not an orchard.
> - **Time root work for late winter (Jan–Feb), before the spring flush** [D065](../../claim_table.html#claim-d065), so the
>   tree rebuilds roots as the Feb–Nov root season opens [A007](../../claim_table.html#claim-a007) (technique: §5).
> - **Do not force bloom by drying the pot** — the reservoir is too small and bloom-time
>   stress costs yield [B055](../../claim_table.html#claim-b055); rely on the normal watering rhythm [A013](../../claim_table.html#claim-a013) (§11).
> - **Mark the high-risk dates:** bloom (late winter/spring) and tender flushes are the most
>   frost- *and* heat-sensitive tissues [C031](../../claim_table.html#claim-c031), [B099](../../claim_table.html#claim-b099) — the easy win of a pot is moving/shading
>   it on those days [B149](../../claim_table.html#claim-b149) (§15).
> - **June drop is normal** [B032](../../claim_table.html#claim-b032); <2% of flowers ever make fruit [B045](../../claim_table.html#claim-b045) — don't over-react.

## Key takeaways
- Lisbon is **everbearing** with a winter main crop [E011](../../claim_table.html#claim-e011), [D022](../../claim_table.html#claim-d022); **care can't be seasonal**
  [E011](../../claim_table.html#claim-e011).
- Roots extend **~Feb–Nov** [A007](../../claim_table.html#claim-a007) and **alternate** with shoot flushes [A009](../../claim_table.html#claim-a009) — time
  root-disturbing work for late-winter dormancy [D065](../../claim_table.html#claim-d065).
- Lisbon shows **~3 flushes/yr** (main bloom late winter–early spring) [C015](../../claim_table.html#claim-c015), blurring toward
  near-year-round in mild climates [C017](../../claim_table.html#claim-c017).
- Bloom→harvest is **~6–9 months** [E007](../../claim_table.html#claim-e007); the **~4–6-month rapid-growth phase is peak
  water/nutrient demand** [E008](../../claim_table.html#claim-e008).
- **Heavy drop is normal** (<2% of flowers set; post-bloom, June, pre-harvest waves) [B045](../../claim_table.html#claim-b045), [B032](../../claim_table.html#claim-b032).
- **Bloom and tender flushes are the most cold- *and* heat-sensitive windows** [C031](../../claim_table.html#claim-c031), [B099](../../claim_table.html#claim-b099).

## Things not to do
- **Don't deliberately drought-stress the pot to force flowering** — field induction data
  doesn't transfer to a 14″ reservoir, and bloom-time stress drops fruit [B042-caveat, B055].
- **Don't repot/root-prune just before or during bloom** — it risks flower drop [D070](../../claim_table.html#claim-d070);
  use the late-winter window [D065](../../claim_table.html#claim-d065).
- **Don't panic at post-bloom or June drop** — it's the normal thinning to canopy capacity
  [B032](../../claim_table.html#claim-b032), [C038](../../claim_table.html#claim-c038).
- **Don't let the rapid-growth (sizing) phase go dry or underfed** — drop and small fruit
  follow [E008](../../claim_table.html#claim-e008), [B055](../../claim_table.html#claim-b055).
- **Don't assume a single harvest season** — plan for everbearing, continuous care [E011](../../claim_table.html#claim-e011).

## Evidence gaps
- **Lisbon's flush count and bloom timing are moderately/weakly sourced** [C015](../../claim_table.html#claim-c015), [C017](../../claim_table.html#claim-c017) and
  not from a controlled California container study.
- **Bloom→harvest duration and fruit-development phases are from a consumer phenology
  reference** [E007–E010], lemon-general not Lisbon-container-measured.
- **No container-Lisbon yield data** beyond the synthesis estimate of "a few dozen fruit/yr"
  [E123, *weak*].
- **Floral-induction thresholds are general citrus** [B041](../../claim_table.html#claim-b041), [B042](../../claim_table.html#claim-b042); how a mild marine-layer
  west-LA microclimate actually drives Lisbon induction is uncharacterized in the corpus.
