# 13. Disease, Pest, and Physiological Disorder Diagnostics

> **Evidence callout.** This is **strong** — almost entirely UC IPM, CDFA, and university
> extension, much of it lemon-specific [E062](#claim-e062), [E075](#claim-e075), [B048](#claim-b048). This section is the **home for
> *diagnosis*** — identifying what is wrong and telling look-alikes apart; the **management/IPM
> tactics** (oils, baits, copper bands, Bt, quarantine compliance) are the home of **§16** and
> are only pointed to here. Root-zone causes (hypoxia, salt) trace to §3/§10; watering-disorder
> discriminators to §11.

The recurring diagnostic theme for a container Lisbon: **most symptoms have a benign or
abiotic look-alike**, and treating the wrong cause makes things worse. The two highest-stakes
calls are **(1) over- vs under-watering** (opposite fixes) and **(2) HLB vs nutrient chlorosis**
(one is a death sentence, one is a feeding tweak). Get those right first.

---

## 13.1 The Four Differentials That Matter Most

| Symptom | Look-alike A | Look-alike B | How to tell | Claims |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Wilting** | Thirst (mix **dry**, light pot) | Root rot / overwatering (mix **wet**, heavy pot, roots brown/mushy/sour) | Check moisture at 3–4″ depth + heft pot before watering | D030, D031, D103, D032 |
| **New-leaf chlorosis** | **HLB** — blotchy mottle **asymmetric** across midrib; fruit lopsided/bitter | Micronutrient (Fe/Mn/Zn) lockout — **symmetric** pattern | Asymmetry = HLB; symmetric = nutrition/pH | E062, E063 |
| **Interveinal chlorosis** | **Mg** deficiency on **older** leaves | Fe/Mn/Zn on **new** leaves | Leaf age locates it (§10) | D047(§10), A057 |
| **Leaf-margin scorch** | Salt (Cl/Na) burn | Wind desiccation **or** heat | Rule out exposure (wind) and EC (leachate test §14) before assuming salt | B054(§10), D039 |

## 13.2 Physiological / Abiotic Disorders (general citrus; the container ones dominate)

| Disorder | Diagnostic signs | Underlying cause | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Underwatering** | Leaves dull, curl/cup, dry crisp from tip, **drop while firmly attached**; soil light/dry; fast recovery on watering | Thirst | D030, B017 | Moderate / Strong |
| **Overwatering / root rot** | Leaves **yellow and drop, soft/limp, wilt despite wet mix**; surface mold/algae; darkened water-soaked crown; **roots brown/black, mushy, sour** | Saturation → hypoxia → Phytophthora | D031, D104, D103 | Moderate / Weak |
| **Waterlogging chlorosis** | Interveinal chlorosis under saturation | **Induced Fe-uptake failure** (flooding down-regulates Fe-acquisition genes), not only O₂ lack | A063, A064 | Strong |
| **Hypoxia decline** | Chlorosis, root rot, retarded growth, excess fruit abscission | Root O₂ limit → 3–37.5% ATP deficit; citrus declines, doesn't adapt | B014, B015 | Extrapolated |
| **Heat injury** | Flower/young-fruitlet drop, leaf rolling, leaf senescence, growth inhibition | Supra-optimal heat | B098 | Strong |
| **Bark sunburn** | Bark scald on sun-exposed wood, esp. **west-facing** | Over-pruning exposes bark | C027, C330(§7) | Weak |
| **Wind desiccation** | Leaf-margin scorch independent of soil moisture | Exposure | D039 | Extrapolated |
| **Natural fruit drop** | 3 waves: post-bloom, **June drop (~1–2 mo, ~10% of drop)**, pre-harvest; June-drop fruit separate at abscission zone C | Carbohydrate self-thinning; <2% set is normal | B032, B033, B034 | Strong |
| **Stress-stacked drop** | Drop worse than the natural baseline | Heat/drought/low-carb raise ethylene:auxin → abscission | A059, A060, B035 | Strong |
| **Transplant leaf drop** | Some leaf drop ~1–2 wk after potting/move | Normal shock/acclimation | C008 | Moderate |

> ⚠️ **The single biggest container killer is overwatering/root rot, and it *mimics* thirst**
> (wilt + yellowing) [D031](#claim-d031), [D104](#claim-d104). **Wilt + wet mix = stop watering, suspect rot**, not thirst
> [D032](#claim-d032). Recovery: emergency repot into fresh fast-draining mix, cut rotted roots, withhold
> water until the mix dries, then restrained watering; improve drainage [D106](#claim-d106) (§5/§11).
> And **waterlogging chlorosis is fixed by restoring drainage/aeration, not by adding chelate**
> [A064](#claim-a064).

## 13.3 Diseases (general + Lisbon-specific)

| Disease | Diagnostic signs | Notes / risk here | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **HLB (Huanglongbing)** | **Asymmetric blotchy leaf mottle**; small lopsided fruit, aborted seeds, bitter; tree declines/dies in a few years | **Incurable, fatal**; present across LA-area counties; **report to CDFA 1-800-491-1899** | E062, E063, D055, D058 | Strong |
| **Phytophthora root rot / gummosis** | Root rot, crown/trunk gummosis, fruit **brown rot**; can kill tree | Driven by **prolonged saturation/poor drainage** — the shallow-pot threat; spreads in standing water | A116, A117 | Strong |
| Phytophthora brown rot (fruit) | Spores splash from media onto low fruit | Skirt low branches off the pot rim (§7) | A022 | Strong |
| **CTV (tristeza)** | Virus; mitigated by certified budwood + tolerant rootstock | Lisbon (all CA citrus) is susceptible | B051 | Strong |
| **Greasy spot** | Yellow → greasy brown-black blisters on **leaf underside**; premature leaf drop | Favored by **warm temps + prolonged leaf wetness/humidity** — a marine-layer risk; far milder in arid CA than FL | E075, C076 | Strong / Weak |
| Sooty mold | Black coating on leaves/fruit | **Symptom of honeydew pests** (aphids/scale/mealybug/whitefly) + ants — cosmetic; treat the pest/ants (§16) | E089, E091 | Strong |
| **Mal secco** | — | Lisbon **is susceptible** (high xylem vessel density) — **but NOT present in California; do not treat as a CA threat** | B048 | Strong |
| Crown rot | Rot at trunk base | From buried graft / mulch on trunk (§5/§6) | C086 | Moderate |

**Lisbon susceptibility summary [B050](#claim-b050):** Phytophthora (worse on vigorous lemon rootstocks —
another reason for trifoliate Flying Dragon, §2), greasy spot in humid coastal air, and coastal
**bud/silver mite**. Its toughness is abiotic, *not* disease immunity (§1) [B052](#claim-b052).

## 13.4 Pests — Identification (management is §16)

| Pest | Diagnostic signs | Balcony relevance | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Asian citrus psyllid (ACP)** | Adults feed at **~45° angle** on tender flush; nymphs make **white waxy tubules**; eggs on new growth tips | **The existential pest** — HLB vector; scout **every flush** | E061, E064 | Strong |
| Citrus leafminer (CLM) | **Serpentine mines** in young leaves; curling/distortion | Heavy on multi-flush coastal lemons; mainly a young-tree concern | E077, E078 | Strong |
| **Spider mites** | Yellow-brown **stippling**, worst on recent flush; bronzing when heavy | Thrive **hot/dry/dusty** — likely dominant summer pest on a west wall | E080, E082 | Strong |
| **Eriophyid bud/silver/rust mites** | Microscopic (need ≥20× lens); diagnose **by damage** — silvered rind (rust mite, fruit ≥0.5″), deformed buds/foliage (bud mite) | Classic **coastal-lemon** problem; mostly cosmetic | E071, E072, E073 | Strong |
| Aphids | Cluster on **tender new growth**, curl leaves, honeydew → sooty mold/ants | Mainly young/flushing trees; not the HLB vector | E068, E069, E070 | Strong |
| Citrus thrips | Scabby grayish **ring scar** around stem end | Susceptible **petal fall → ~1.5″ fruit**; cosmetic | E083, E084 | Strong |
| Scale / mealybug | Honeydew → sooty mold; armored scale encrusts bark/fruit (no honeydew) | Likely **chronic low-grade** on a sheltered balcony; scout inside canopy | E091, E092 | Strong |
| Whitefly | Honeydew → sooty mold | Usually under biological control; indirect damage only | E087, E089 | Strong |
| **Brown garden snail** | Climbs trunk, chews leaves/bark (ring-barks twigs)/rind; hides in canopy by day | Abundant in coastal SoCal | E065 | Strong |
| **Orangedog caterpillar** | Large larva camouflaged as **bird dropping** | **Acute** — a few can defoliate a small canopy fast | E094 | Strong |

> **Honeydew → sooty mold → ants is one linked complex:** ants farm and protect the
> honeydew-producers and break biological control [E646/§16]. If sooty mold appears, the
> diagnostic chain is **find the honeydew pest, and look for ants** — management in §16.

---

> ### 🪴 Worked example — 19″×14″ west-facing Brentwood / West LA balcony
> - **Watch list for this site:** **ACP/HLB** (existential — scout every flush [E064](#claim-e064)),
>   **spider mites** (hot west wall, summer [E082](#claim-e082)), **bud/silver mite** (coastal lemon
>   [E073](#claim-e073)), **CLM** (multi-flush [E078](#claim-e078)), **scale/mealybug + sooty mold** (sheltered balcony
>   [E093](#claim-e093)), **orangedog** (acute on a small canopy [E094](#claim-e094)), **snails** (coastal [E065](#claim-e065)), and
>   **root rot** from the shallow wet pot [A117](#claim-a117).
> - **Before treating, run the differentials:** wilt → check mix wet/dry [D032](#claim-d032); new-leaf mottle
>   → **asymmetric = suspect HLB, report to CDFA 1-800-491-1899** [E062](#claim-e062), [D058](#claim-d058); symmetric →
>   feed/pH (§10). Margin scorch → rule out wind/heat before salt [D039](#claim-d039) (then leachate test §14).
> - **Marine layer raises greasy-spot/sooty-mold risk** [E075](#claim-e075), [C076](#claim-c076) — diagnostic cue to water
>   at the soil early so leaves dry (§11/§16).
> - **Mal secco:** Lisbon is genetically susceptible [B048](#claim-b048) but it is **not in California** —
>   don't misattribute dieback to it.

## Key takeaways
- **Diagnose before you treat** — most symptoms have an abiotic look-alike; wrong fix worsens it.
- **Wilt + wet mix = root rot, not thirst** [D032](#claim-d032), [D031](#claim-d031) — the top container killer; it mimics drought.
- **HLB = asymmetric blotchy mottle + lopsided bitter fruit** [E062](#claim-e062), [E063](#claim-e063); symmetric chlorosis
  is nutrition/pH (§10). HLB is **fatal and reportable (CDFA 1-800-491-1899)** [D058](#claim-d058).
- **Phytophthora is a saturation disease** [A117](#claim-a117) — the shallow-pot threat; prevent by drainage
  (§3) and a tolerant rootstock (§2).
- **Likely chronic balcony pests:** spider mites (hot wall), bud/silver mite (coastal), CLM,
  scale/mealybug; **acute:** orangedog [E082](#claim-e082), [E073](#claim-e073), [E094](#claim-e094).
- **Sooty mold means a honeydew pest + ants** — trace the chain [E089](#claim-e089), [E091](#claim-e091).

## Things not to do
- **Don't water a wilting plant before checking if the mix is wet** — may be rot [D032](#claim-d032).
- **Don't add iron chelate for waterlogging chlorosis** — fix drainage instead [A064](#claim-a064).
- **Don't assume new-leaf mottle is "just nutrition"** — check for HLB asymmetry first [E062](#claim-e062).
- **Don't treat for mal secco in California** — it isn't here [B048](#claim-b048).
- **Don't spray broad-spectrum insecticide** as a first response — it flares mites and breaks
  biological control [E082](#claim-e082) (§16).

## Evidence gaps
- **Diagnostics are general citrus / lemon, not container-Lisbon-specific** — symptom thresholds
  (e.g., spider-mite action levels) are not formally established [E081](#claim-e081).
- **Eriophyid and many pests are diagnosed by damage, not the organism** (microscopic) [E071](#claim-e071).
- **Greasy-spot/marine-layer risk for this site is weakly sourced** [C076](#claim-c076); severity in arid
  coastal CA is uncertain [E075](#claim-e075).
- **Root-rot recovery protocol is weak consumer sourcing** [D106](#claim-d106); no container-Lisbon trial.
