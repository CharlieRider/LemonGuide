# 2. Rootstock and Tree Selection

> **Evidence callout.** This section is on a **strong** footing. The dwarfing mechanism and
> Flying Dragon size figures come from peer-reviewed and extension sources [B101, B103,
> D127, A074]; the rootstock comparison and salinity ranking are from university and
> government ag references [C122](../../claim_table.html#claim-c122), [D125](../../claim_table.html#claim-d125), [A083](../../claim_table.html#claim-a083). The single most important *decision* here —
> resolving the dwarfing-vs-salinity trade-off for alkaline LA tap water — is **explicitly
> a conflict in the corpus** [C124](../../claim_table.html#claim-c124), [C127](../../claim_table.html#claim-c127) and is flagged below; the resolution leans on a
> *weak* repository synthesis [E118](../../claim_table.html#claim-e118) plus management (leaching, §11/§14) rather than on a
> rootstock that wins both axes, because none exists [D125](../../claim_table.html#claim-d125).

Rootstock choice is the highest-leverage decision in the entire guide for a container.
Lisbon's own vigor (§1) means that on a vigorous rootstock it will fight a 14-inch pot for
its whole life; on a true dwarfing rootstock the same scion stays small *genetically and
permanently* [C115](../../claim_table.html#claim-c115), not by starvation [A077](../../claim_table.html#claim-a077). The scion (Lisbon) sets the fruit; the
rootstock sets the size, the salt/Phytophthora behavior, and the cold hardiness [D125](../../claim_table.html#claim-d125).

---

## 2.1 The Dwarfing Decision (general citrus, well-quantified)

Tree size spans a huge range purely by rootstock — from Alemow at **~60% larger** than
sour orange down to Flying Dragon at **~80% smaller** [A074](../../claim_table.html#claim-a074). For a pot you want the
bottom of that range.

| Rootstock | Size effect | Tree size | Lineage | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Flying Dragon** | **66–75% reduction**; the *only* true dwarfing citrus rootstock | scion <2.5 m; **~4–8 ft** | Trifoliate selection | B101, D127, C012 | Strong / Strong / Moderate |
| C-35 citrange | Semi-dwarf; **~17% reduction** (~83% of Carrizo) | ~10–15 ft in-ground | Trifoliate hybrid | A015, A017 | Moderate |
| Carrizo / Troyer | Standard (medium-to-large); *not* dwarfing | standard | Citrange | B118, D128 | Strong |
| Swingle citrumelo | Standard (medium-to-large); not size-controlling | standard | Trifoliate hybrid | D005 | Strong |
| Volkameriana | Very vigorous, large, fast | large | Lemon-type | B001 | Moderate |
| Rough lemon | Very vigorous; large trees | large | Lemon-type | A084 | Moderate |
| Alemow (*C. macrophylla*) | Largest canopy (+60% vs sour orange) | largest | — | C065, A084 | Strong |

**How dwarfing works (so you don't fight it):** Flying Dragon and the trifoliates dwarf
via **reduced root→shoot hydraulic conductance and lowered auxin/gibberellin** [B103](../../claim_table.html#claim-b103), [C114](../../claim_table.html#claim-c114), [C113](../../claim_table.html#claim-c113) — on Eureka lemon, Flying Dragon produced the lowest IAA [B103](../../claim_table.html#claim-b103). It is a
stable, genetically-rooted trait that persists for the tree's life [C115](../../claim_table.html#claim-c115), [A077](../../claim_table.html#claim-a077) — not
starvation [A077](../../claim_table.html#claim-a077). The dwarfing effect holds across vigorous scions including **Lisbon**
[C013](../../claim_table.html#claim-c013). Flying Dragon also slows winter metabolism, improving cold hardening [C014](../../claim_table.html#claim-c014).

> ⚠️ **Management consequence (don't miss this):** dwarfing rootstocks are **less
> responsive to N, K, Ca, Mg, and micronutrients** from the medium [B104](../../claim_table.html#claim-b104). Feed steadily
> and completely; do not rely on aggressive root scavenging (see §10).

## 2.2 Rootstock Profiles for Lemon (Lisbon-relevant)

Common lemon rootstocks are the trifoliates and hybrids (Troyer/Carrizo, Swingle),
rough lemon, Volkamer, and sour orange [A082](../../claim_table.html#claim-a082).

| Rootstock | Vigor/size | Phytophthora | Salinity | Cold | Notes for a pot | Claims |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Flying Dragon** | Dwarf (best) | Tolerant (trifoliate) | Sensitive | Good hardening | **First choice** for container Lisbon | B101, D028, C014, C124 |
| C-35 | Semi-dwarf | Tolerant | Sensitive | Good | Acceptable if FD unavailable | A015, A016, C122 |
| Carrizo/Troyer | Standard | Resistant | **Sensitive** (toxic-ion accumulation), intolerant of high-lime | Moderate | Salinity + lemon-compatibility cautions | B118–B120, B121 |
| Swingle | Standard | Good (below pure trifoliate) | — | — | Not size-controlling | D002, D005 |
| Volkameriana | Very vigorous | **Prone** to root rot | Moderate | — | Salt-hardy but too big | B001, B002, B004 |
| Alemow | Largest | — | Most tolerant | **Cold-sensitive**, poor in drought | Worst for a shallow fast-drying pot | C064–C067 |
| Sour orange | — | — | — | — | Susceptible to tristeza (CTV) | A085 |

**Two compatibility/strain cautions:**
- **Carrizo is *not* compatible with Eureka-type lemons; lemon–citrange combinations need
  verification** [B121](../../claim_table.html#claim-b121). This is a reason to buy a *known-good* nursery combination rather
  than an unverified graft.
- Vigorous rootstocks (rough lemon) keep growing at warmer root temps than slow ones
  [A021](../../claim_table.html#claim-a021) — a minor point that slightly offsets, but does not justify, their excess size.

## 2.3 The Central Conflict: Dwarfing vs. Salinity ⚠️

This is the keystone trade-off for coastal/inland-LA hard, alkaline tap water.

> **Salt-tolerance ranking** [C122](../../claim_table.html#claim-c122): Cleopatra mandarin & Alemow (most) > Volkameriana
> (moderate) > Swingle > **Carrizo/Troyer & trifoliate (sensitive)**.
>
> **The trade-off** [C124, *Strong*]: the most salt-tolerant lemon rootstocks (Alemow,
> Volkamer) are the **most vigorous and least size-controlling**; the size-controlling
> trifoliates (Flying Dragon, C-35) are the **most salt-sensitive**. No rootstock wins both
> axes [D125](../../claim_table.html#claim-d125).

**Resolution adopted by this guide (per repository synthesis [E118, *Weak*]):** choose
**Flying Dragon for size control** and manage salinity *agronomically* rather than buying
size with a vigorous salt-tolerant stock that defeats the entire container purpose. That
means:
- Leach the pot deliberately to flush accumulated salts (home: §11 Irrigation, §14
  Testing) — a salt-sensitive rootstock *raises* the importance of leaching [A083](../../claim_table.html#claim-a083).
- Monitor leachate EC (§14) so salt stress is caught before it injures the sensitive root.
- Prevent — don't merely tolerate — root-zone stress: citrus mounts a *reactive*, not
  pre-adaptive, hypoxia response and has no aerenchyma [B062](../../claim_table.html#claim-b062), so aeration and drainage
  (§3) beat relying on rootstock tolerance.

This resolution is a deliberate bet that the grower will leach and monitor. A grower
unwilling to manage salt could justify C-35 (semi-dwarf, slightly less extreme) but should
**not** drop to a vigorous salt-tolerant stock for a 14″ pot.

## 2.4 Propagation & Tree Form (general citrus / Lisbon)

| Method | Result | Container verdict | Claim | Strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Budded on rootstock** (T-bud/chip bud) | Standard lemon propagation; confers size control, disease/salt tolerance, cold hardiness from rootstock | **Required** to get dwarfing — "Lisbon on Flying Dragon small, on Volkamer huge" | D130, D131 | Strong |
| Rooted cutting (own-root) | Easy/cheap; bushy, low-branching tree | **Forfeits all rootstock benefits** — no dwarfing, less disease/salt tolerance, more Phytophthora risk | D132 | Strong |
| Air layering | Vegetative option | Same own-root drawbacks | D133 | Strong |
| Seed | Not true-to-type; long juvenility | Do not use | D133 | Strong |

T-budding needs the bark to be "slipping" (active growth, **late spring/summer** in CA);
chip budding is used when not slipping [D130](../../claim_table.html#claim-d130). For nearly all home growers the operative
advice is simpler: **buy a tree already budded on the right rootstock** [A125](../../claim_table.html#claim-a125).

## 2.5 Buying the Tree (Lisbon-specific)

- **Buy on a dwarf rootstock** to minimize the size-control burden from day one [A125](../../claim_table.html#claim-a125), [E046](../../claim_table.html#claim-e046) — choose a size-controlling/dwarfing trifoliate (Flying Dragon), *not* a vigorous
  lemon rootstock [E046](../../claim_table.html#claim-e046).
- A "Dwarf Lisbon" (Lisbon on a dwarfing rootstock) is documented as well-suited to
  containers — manageable while keeping Lisbon's fruit quality and hardiness [D539,
  *Weak*].
- Pair the dwarfing rootstock with a **less-thorny, more compact scion strain (Yen Ben,
  CRC 4067)** and disciplined post-harvest pruning [E118](../../claim_table.html#claim-e118) (strain selection is the home of
  §1; pruning is §7).
- Combined with a dwarfing rootstock, **root pruning** keeps a vigorous scion manageable
  in a modest pot for many years [C072, *Weak*] (home: §5/§7) — and pot-binding itself
  curbs leaf size and vigor [D063, *Weak*].
- **Avoid Meyer as a substitute** if you specifically want a true acid lemon: Meyer is a
  lemon×orange hybrid — sweeter, lower-acid, more cold-tender — popular for pots but a
  different fruit [E036](../../claim_table.html#claim-e036).

> ### 🪴 Worked example — 19″×14″ west-facing Brentwood / West LA balcony
> - **Specify: Lisbon (Yen Ben strain) budded on Flying Dragon.** Flying Dragon gives a
>   ~4–8 ft tree [D127](../../claim_table.html#claim-d127) / 66–75% reduction [B101](../../claim_table.html#claim-b101) — the only stock that genuinely fits a
>   14″ pot, and it adds Phytophthora tolerance [D028](../../claim_table.html#claim-d028) and better cold hardening [C014](../../claim_table.html#claim-c014),
>   both useful on an exposed marine-layer wall.
> - **Accept the salinity trade-off and manage it.** Flying Dragon is salt-*sensitive*
>   [C124](../../claim_table.html#claim-c124); Brentwood tap water is hard and alkaline. The plan is to **leach regularly and
>   monitor leachate EC** (§11, §14), not to swap to a vigorous salt-tolerant stock.
> - **Feed completely and steadily** — the dwarf root is a poor scavenger [B104](../../claim_table.html#claim-b104) (§10).
> - **Buy budded, not own-root** [D131](../../claim_table.html#claim-d131); verify the nursery's rootstock — own-root or an
>   unverified graft forfeits the dwarfing and disease/salt benefits [D132](../../claim_table.html#claim-d132).
> - If Flying Dragon is genuinely unavailable, **C-35** is the fallback (semi-dwarf, ~17%
>   reduction, still trifoliate-tolerant) [A015](../../claim_table.html#claim-a015), [A016](../../claim_table.html#claim-a016) — expect a larger tree and more
>   pruning/root-pruning.

## Key takeaways
- **Flying Dragon is the only true dwarfing citrus rootstock** (~4–8 ft, 66–75% smaller)
  and the default for container Lisbon [D127](../../claim_table.html#claim-d127), [B101](../../claim_table.html#claim-b101); C-35 is the semi-dwarf fallback [A015](../../claim_table.html#claim-a015).
- Dwarfing is **genetic and permanent** (hydraulic/hormonal), not starvation [B103](../../claim_table.html#claim-b103), [C115](../../claim_table.html#claim-c115), [A077](../../claim_table.html#claim-a077) — but the dwarf root **feeds poorly**, so fertilize completely [B104](../../claim_table.html#claim-b104).
- **Dwarfing and salt tolerance trade off** [C124](../../claim_table.html#claim-c124); for alkaline LA water, keep the dwarf
  rootstock and manage salt by leaching + EC monitoring (§11/§14), not by upsizing the stock.
- **Buy a tree budded on the right rootstock** [A125](../../claim_table.html#claim-a125), [D131](../../claim_table.html#claim-d131); own-root/seedling forfeits
  size control and disease/salt tolerance [D132](../../claim_table.html#claim-d132).

## Things not to do
- **Don't choose a vigorous salt-tolerant rootstock** (Volkamer, Alemow, rough lemon) to
  dodge the salt problem — it produces a tree far too large for a pot [A074](../../claim_table.html#claim-a074), [C065](../../claim_table.html#claim-c065), [C124](../../claim_table.html#claim-c124).
- **Don't buy own-root / rooted-cutting / seed-grown Lisbon** for a container — no dwarfing,
  worse Phytophthora and salt behavior [D132](../../claim_table.html#claim-d132), [D133](../../claim_table.html#claim-d133).
- **Don't assume any Carrizo-budded lemon is fine** — Carrizo is not compatible with
  Eureka-type lemons and is salt/lime-sensitive [B121](../../claim_table.html#claim-b121), [B120](../../claim_table.html#claim-b120).
- **Don't rely on rootstock "tolerance"** to survive a waterlogged or salty pot — citrus
  hypoxia/salt response is reactive; prevent it via aeration/drainage/leaching [B062](../../claim_table.html#claim-b062).

## Evidence gaps
- **No pot trial of Lisbon-on-Flying-Dragon in a 14″ container.** The ~4–8 ft figure
  [D127](../../claim_table.html#claim-d127) is for in-ground/standard culture; the actual restrained size in this pot is
  inferred, not measured (see §7).
- **Flying Dragon × Lisbon graft compatibility** is asserted via the general "holds across
  lemons/Lisbon" claim [C013](../../claim_table.html#claim-c013) and a nursery "Dwarf Lisbon" product [D098, *weak*]; no
  long-term compatibility study is in the corpus.
- **The dwarfing-vs-salinity resolution rests on a weak synthesis** [E118](../../claim_table.html#claim-e118) plus management
  assumptions; the corpus has no container study quantifying how much leaching offsets
  Flying Dragon's salt sensitivity (see §14).
- **C-35's exact size on a dwarfing-stock budget in a pot** is not given; only in-ground
  ~10–15 ft [A015](../../claim_table.html#claim-a015).
