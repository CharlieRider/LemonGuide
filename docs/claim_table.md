# Lisbon Lemon Container Guide — Master Claim Table

**Purpose:** Single source of truth for the guide. Every quantitative target or decision
rule in `lisbon_lemon_guide.md` must trace to a Claim ID here, which traces to a study
note in `..\lisbon-lemon-studies\`. Built by fan-out extraction over all 165 study notes.

**Evidence Strength:** Strong = university extension / govt ag / peer-reviewed / direct
product label · Moderate = commercial or nursery production guide, or repeated across
credible sources · Weak = consumer horticulture or indirect inference · Extrapolated =
container-crop science or citrus-general, not Lisbon-specific.

## Conflicts & Reconciliations

These tensions surfaced across sources. The guide should follow the **Resolution** column
and cite both underlying claims where relevant.

| # | Tension | Sources | Resolution for a 19"×14" west-facing Brentwood pot |
|---|---------|---------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| 1 | Open the canopy interior for light/sugar **vs** don't thin (dense canopy prevents sunburn) | IFAS selective-pruning vs UC SLO container pruning | Light, **selective** interior thinning only; never hard-thin an exposed west pot; whitewash any newly exposed bark. Favor sunburn protection over maximal light. |
| 2 | Water ~every 3 days (coastal) **vs** ~daily (hot west-facing pot) | UC SLO / UC MG vs heat-stress + container drying notes | Frequency is **weather- and exposure-driven, not fixed** — water to a leaching fraction when the pot tests dry by weight/probe; expect near-daily in heat waves, every 2–3 days in cool marine spells. |
| 3 | Shade cloth 30–50% **vs** flat 50% | Citrus Industry (Tier 1/3) vs consumer sources (Tier 5) | Use **30–50%**, leaning 50% on the west exposure when air temp >95–100°F; trust the extension range over the consumer flat figure. |
| 4 | Repot/root-prune every 2–3 yr **vs** 3–5 yr | UC MG / Simply Trees vs Clemson | Interval scales with pot size and vigor; for a **19" pot on dwarfing rootstock, ~2–3 yr** root-prune + media refresh. |
