## Summary
Clemson extension factsheet on home container citrus covering pot sizing, media, root-zone temperature optimum, and watering. Provides baseline horticultural targets that bound the design space for a shallow patio container.

## Key Findings
- Recommended final container range: **18–36 inches diameter**; repot **every 3–5 years**. The 19" pot sits at the small end of this range — acceptable but with little margin, reinforcing the need for disciplined media and root management.
- Optimal **root-zone temperature for fruit production: 65–85°F (18–29°C)**. The west-facing balcony will routinely push the root zone above 85°F in summer, into the suboptimal band.
- Suggested homemade mix: **4 parts aged pine bark : 1 part peat/coir** for organic matter plus drainage. Bark fraction supplies the coarse aeration a 14"-deep pot needs.
- Watering: **near-daily during hot periods**; check the **top 2–3 inches** of media before watering; never let the root ball fully dry or stay saturated for extended periods.
- Drainage holes are mandatory to prevent root rot — non-negotiable in a shallow pot with a large perched-water zone.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Directly applicable. Confirms the 19" pot is workable but at the small end of the recommended size band, and that summer root-zone temperature on a west balcony will exceed the 65–85°F optimum, making thermal management and a bark-heavy aerated mix the priorities.
