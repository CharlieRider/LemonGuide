## Summary
Technical reference on gypsum (calcium sulfate dihydrate) as a citrus amendment that supplies calcium without moving pH. Relevant for a container where pH is already in range but coir/leaching create a calcium gap and salt build-up needs a displacing cation.

## Key Findings
- Gypsum (CaSO₄·2H₂O) is roughly **22% calcium and 18% sulfur**, delivered in **water-soluble** form.
- **Supplies Ca and S without changing pH** (unlike lime, whose carbonate raises pH) — ideal when the substrate is already at citrus's target pH 6.0–6.5.
- Improves drainage/structure in compacted or sodic media and **displaces sodium** from exchange sites, flushing it out with irrigation.
- Citrus tolerates poor drainage badly; gypsum is used specifically to relieve anaerobic, compacted root zones.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Useful targeted amendment. A modest gypsum addition supplies the extra calcium a buffered-coir or bark mix needs and helps displace accumulated sodium from hard-water irrigation in the shallow pot — all without disturbing the pH that controls micronutrient availability. Field application rates (kg/ha) do not transfer; use as a light incorporated amendment per product label for containers.
