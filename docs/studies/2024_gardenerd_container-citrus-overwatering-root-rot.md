## Summary
Practical diagnosis and recovery guidance for overwatered container citrus and Phytophthora-type root rot. Directly addresses the failure mode most likely to kill a citrus tree in a shallow, slow-draining pot during the wet season.

## Key Findings
- **Root-rot roots are brown/black, soft, mushy, with a sour smell**; healthy roots are firm and white.
- Above-ground signs: **yellowing (lower leaves first), wilting despite moist soil, soft limp leaves, surface mold/algae, darkened water-soaked crown** — overwatering presents *like* drought.
- **Shallow pots are especially prone** — roots sit in the perched-water zone and cannot dry between waterings.
- Recovery: **emergency repot into fresh fast-draining mix, cut away rotted roots, withhold water until the mix dries, then resume restrained watering**; improve drainage (holes, elevate pot).

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Directly on-point. This is the single highest-probability killer for the 14"-deep pot, especially after LA winter rains. The repository's standing rule: if the tree wilts while the mix is wet, suspect root rot (not thirst), check root color/smell, and respond by drying and improving drainage — never by watering more. Prevention = coarse mix + leaching-but-draining irrigation + elevated pot.
