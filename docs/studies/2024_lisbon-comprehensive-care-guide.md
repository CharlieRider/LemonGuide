## Summary
Consolidated baseline care parameters specific to Lisbon lemon — the quick-reference numbers (light, water, feeding, pH, mulch) that the rest of the repository refines for the container context.

## Key Findings
- **Light: 6–8 hours direct sun/day**; full sun for best fruiting.
- **Soil/pH: well-drained, slightly acidic pH 5.5–6.5.**
- **Feeding: balanced citrus fertilizer with N + micronutrients (Zn, Mg, Fe) every 6–8 weeks in the growing season** (refine to "weekly weakly" for containers, Topic 3).
- **Mulch the base** to retain moisture, suppress weeds, and moderate soil temperature (keep off the trunk — Topic 8).
- **In-ground spacing 8–10 ft**; in containers, a large well-drained pot.
- Generic watering guides ("weekly in summer, biweekly otherwise") are starting points only — container drying is faster and weather-driven (Topic 4).

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
The Lisbon-specific baseline the container plan adjusts. The 6–8 h sun requirement is satisfied by the west balcony (with heat management, Topic 9); pH 5.5–6.5 and micronutrient-inclusive feeding match Topics 2–3; mulch supports both moisture and root-heat goals (Topic 9). Treat the generic watering/feeding intervals as defaults to be tightened for the fast-draining 14" pot.
