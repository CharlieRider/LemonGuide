## Summary
California extension guidance on how (and how little) to prune container citrus: prune for shape/size, build structure when young, and — unlike deciduous fruit — do NOT thin the canopy. The governing philosophy for managing a vigorous Lisbon in a pot.

## Key Findings
- Prune citrus **gently, mainly for shape and size control**, not heavily.
- **Young trees (<3 yr): light pruning ~10–15% annually** to build a strong framework.
- **Do NOT thin the canopy** — a dense canopy shades and protects trunk/branches from **sunburn** (critical on a west-facing balcony).
- **Best timing: just after fruit is harvested**, because next season's flower buds form on **last summer's growth** — late spring/summer pruning cuts off fruiting wood.
- Buy trees on **dwarf rootstock** to minimize the size-control burden.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Core pruning doctrine for this tree. Lisbon's vigor tempts heavy cutting, but the rules say restraint: shape lightly, keep the canopy dense for sunburn protection (vital on the hot west exposure), and time major cuts to just after the winter main-crop harvest so you don't remove the spring fruiting wood. Combine with dwarfing rootstock so pruning manages, rather than fights, the tree.
