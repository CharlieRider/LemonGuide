## Summary
Mechanistic study of how trifoliate-orange genetics (the basis of Flying Dragon and citrange rootstocks) impose dwarfing, via hormone and hydraulic regulation. Explains *why* the recommended container rootstocks reliably restrain a vigorous scion.

## Key Findings
- Dwarfing in trifoliate orange is governed by **hormone signaling (reduced auxin/gibberellin action) and growth-gene expression**, consistent with the low-IAA finding for Flying Dragon on lemon.
- Associated with **reduced hydraulic conductance** from root to shoot, throttling the water/assimilate supply that drives vigorous extension growth.
- Confirms dwarfing is a **stable, genetically-rooted trait** of the trifoliate lineage, not an environmental fluke — so the size control persists over the tree's life.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Supporting mechanism, not a field number. It reassures that the dwarfing of a Flying Dragon / trifoliate-based Lisbon is durable and physiological, so the 10-year manageability goal rests on a stable trait. The hydraulic-restriction mechanism also hints the tree will be slightly more conservative in water use — compatible with the small container reservoir.
