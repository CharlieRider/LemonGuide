## Summary
Practitioner synthesis of a citrus container mix recipe and, importantly, the particle-grade guidance that determines whether a mix drains. Translates the Purdue/UCANR physical-property targets into an actual buildable recipe.

## Key Findings
- Starting recipe: **~50% bark fines : 30% coco coir (or peat) : 20% coarse perlite or pumice** — bark for structure, coir for moderate water-holding, mineral aggregate for aeration to the pot bottom.
- **Particle grade is decisive**: a coarse/"chunky" (#3) grade drains far better than the fine grades in many bagged blends; fines are what create the soggy perched-water zone.
- Perlite/pumice keep the bottom of the pot oxygenated by creating air pockets that resist saturation.
- Coir dries slightly faster than peat but still holds more water than mineral soil — monitor moisture.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Directly buildable. The 50/30/20 bark-coir-pumice mix at a coarse grade is a sound default for the 14"-deep pot; the explicit warning about fine grades is exactly the failure mode that drowns roots in a shallow container. Pair the coir fraction with the buffering step from the coir study.
