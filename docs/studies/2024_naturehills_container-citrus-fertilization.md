## Summary
Practitioner guidance specific to container (and Dwarf Lisbon) citrus feeding: the NPK ratio, cadence, and the "feed often but dilute" principle that container leaching demands. One of the few Lisbon-named sources in this topic.

## Key Findings
- Use a citrus NPK ratio of **2-1-1 or 3-1-1** (e.g. 6-3-3, 9-3-6); Dwarf Lisbon specifically cited near **6-5-6** balanced.
- **Container citrus leaches nutrients fast** and needs more frequent feeding than in-ground trees.
- Cadence: liquid/water-soluble citrus feed **every 2–4 weeks** in the growing season, or the **"weekly weakly"** approach — small dilute doses to avoid salt spikes.
- Use an **acid-forming / citrus-specific fertilizer with included micronutrients** (Fe, Mn, Zn) since containers rarely supply them.
- Caution against heavy high-N feeds in containers — they disturb media biology and drive soft, pest-prone growth.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Directly on-point and Lisbon-specific. Endorses the "weekly weakly" dilute-feed model as the right answer to the 14" pot's leaching, and a citrus-formulated product with built-in micronutrients covers the Fe/Zn/Mn gap the UF/IFAS studies warn about. Use a 2-1-1/3-1-1 citrus feed as the base, scaled down from in-ground rates.
