## Summary
Care guidance for the dwarf (dwarfing-rootstock) Lisbon specifically grown in a container — the exact configuration this repository targets. Consolidates the variety + container intersection.

## Key Findings
- **Dwarf Lisbon (on dwarfing rootstock) is well-suited to containers**, staying manageable while keeping Lisbon's fruit quality and hardiness.
- **Light feeding, frequent**: container leaching demands steady citrus fertilizer with micronutrients (Zn, Fe, Mg) — "weekly weakly" (Topic 3).
- **Consistent moisture without waterlogging**; fast-draining mix and good drainage essential (Topics 1–2, 4).
- **6–8 h sun**; benefits from afternoon heat management on hot/west exposures (Topic 9).
- **Annual light pruning + periodic root pruning/repot** to hold size (Topics 7, 10); remove thorny suckers.
- Can fruit **multiple times per year** in mild climates (Topic 6).

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
This is the on-label summary for the project's exact tree-and-pot. It confirms a dwarf Lisbon in a 19"×14" pot is a sound, intended configuration and cross-references every operational topic: fast-draining bark/pumice mix, weekly-weakly micronutrient feeding, steady non-waterlogged moisture, afternoon heat management, and the prune/root-prune cycle to hold size for 10+ years.
