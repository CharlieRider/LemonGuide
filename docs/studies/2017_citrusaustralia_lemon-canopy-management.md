## Summary
Commercial lemon canopy-management principles — height control, skirting, and selective limb removal — that scale down to keep a container tree productive and accessible. Lemon-specific (and lemons, especially Lisbon, are among the most vigorous citrus to manage).

## Key Findings
- Lemons are **vigorous and require active canopy management** to control height, maintain light penetration, and keep fruit accessible.
- Standard practices: **height containment (topping/hedging), skirting (removing low branches near the ground), and selective removal of vigorous upright water-sprouts** rather than overall canopy thinning.
- **Open the canopy selectively** for light/air and spray penetration; over-thinning exposes wood to sunburn and stimulates more vigorous regrowth.
- Hard heading cuts provoke **vigorous vegetative regrowth** — a key caution for an already-vigorous variety: heavy cutting begets more cutting.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
The vigor caution is the key transfer. Lisbon's strong response to hard cuts means container pruning should favor **frequent light shaping and whole-branch thinning of vigorous uprights** over occasional severe cutbacks, which would trigger a flush of thorny water-sprouts. "Skirting" translates to keeping the lowest branches off the pot rim/soil to reduce pest bridges and rot. Commercial rates don't transfer; the principles do.
