## Summary
Overview of citrus fruit-set physiology, including the self-fertility and parthenocarpy that let a single lemon tree set fruit without cross-pollination. Clarifies what actually limits fruit number on an isolated balcony tree.

## Key Findings
- **Lemons are largely self-fertile and partly parthenocarpic** — they set fruit from their own pollen or without fertilization, so a lone container tree fruits fine (no pollinizer needed).
- Fruit set is limited not by pollination but by **competition among fruitlets for carbohydrates and the hormonal (auxin/ethylene) abscission program** — the same <2% conversion seen elsewhere.
- **Initial fruit set, then two main drop waves** (post-bloom and "June drop") thin the crop to what the canopy can carry.
- Adequate **leaf area per fruit** is the carrying-capacity constraint — a small pruned canopy supports proportionally fewer fruit.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Reassuring and limiting at once. The balcony Lisbon needs no second tree (self-fertile), but its deliberately small canopy caps how many fruit it can mature — over-setting is self-corrected by June drop. Don't fight natural thinning; instead keep enough healthy leaf area (don't over-prune) so the retained fruit size up well.
