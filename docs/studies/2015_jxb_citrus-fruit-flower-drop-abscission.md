## Summary
Physiology of why citrus flowers and fruitlets drop, and the stresses that trigger it. Essential for a container grower because the pot's stress-prone environment (water swings, heat, nutrient leaching) directly drives abscission.

## Key Findings
- Abscission is controlled by the **auxin:ethylene balance** — a **high ethylene-to-auxin ratio** activates cell-wall-dissolving enzymes in the abscission zone and the organ drops.
- **Ethylene is a stress signal**: water stress, heat, freeze, injury, and decay all raise ethylene and promote drop.
- Post-bloom drop causes: **poor pollination, water or temperature stress, inadequate nitrogen, and carbohydrate/photosynthate limitation** at the fruitlet.
- **Water stress during flowering reduces final fruit number** (yield) — bloom-time drying is especially costly.
- Stress alters the **leaf-to-flower sugar balance**, creating a nutritional-stress abscission signal.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Directly explains container fruit-drop risk. The 14" pot's tendency to swing between wet and dry, plus west-facing heat, are exactly the stresses that raise ethylene and shed fruitlets. Practical defense: keep moisture and feeding *steady* (no big swings) through bloom and early fruit set, and protect from extreme afternoon heat during that window — abscission is largely a stress response you can blunt.
