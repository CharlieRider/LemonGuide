## Summary
Integrates the lifecycle findings into the predictable decline pattern container citrus follows without intervention — and the maintenance cycle that prevents it. The capstone for the 10-year goal.

## Key Findings
- **Untreated decline path (≈5–7 years)**: roots circle and bind → mix degrades to fines and loses air porosity → salts accumulate → uptake falls → canopy thins, fruit shrinks, tree stalls or dies.
- **Prevention is a repeating cycle, not a one-time setup**: every **2–3 years (late winter)** — root-prune (shave 1–2"), refresh media (partial/full), and deep-flush salts; **annually** — light canopy prune after harvest, monitor pests/nutrition; **each watering** — leach ~15–30%.
- **Durable components matter**: dwarfing rootstock (holds size), pine/fir bark + pumice (slow to degrade), light/shaded pot (root heat) all extend the interval between major interventions.
- **Steady feeding** ("weekly weakly," micronutrients) compensates for the low nutrient-scavenging of dwarfing rootstock and the leaching pot.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
The master maintenance schedule for this tree. Hitting 10+ productive years in a 19"×14" pot depends on running the cycle deliberately: **late-winter root-prune + media refresh + salt flush every 2–3 years**, light post-harvest canopy pruning yearly, routine leaching and steady feeding throughout. Skip the 2–3 year service and the tree enters the 5–7 year decline; keep it and the same pot can carry the Lisbon for the long term. This file ties Topics 1–10 into one calendar.
