## Summary
Technique and timing for root-pruning container citrus — the canopy-pruning counterpart that prevents root circling and keeps the tree from outgrowing the pot. The key to the 10-year container goal alongside media refresh (Topic 10).

## Key Findings
- **Root-prune every 2–3 years** to stimulate fresh feeder roots and control size, and to prevent **circling roots that can girdle the trunk** and choke water/nutrient flow.
- Technique: **shear 1–2 inches of roots all around the root ball**, then tease out tangled roots so they grow into fresh mix.
- **Timing: late winter/early spring while dormant**; avoid summer root-pruning (roots are needed for water uptake in heat).
- Repot into the **same pot or one ≤2 inches larger** in diameter — keep, don't enlarge, the pot if holding size.
- Maintain **root-to-canopy balance**: root pruning should be paired with proportional canopy pruning.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Central to long-term container success. For the 14" pot, periodic root pruning (shear 1–2", late winter) is how the tree stays in the same pot for years without becoming root-bound — pair it with the dormant canopy prune so top and roots stay balanced. This is the mechanical complement to dwarfing rootstock for holding size.
