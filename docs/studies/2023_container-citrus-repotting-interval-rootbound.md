## Summary
Establishes how often container citrus must be repotted/refreshed and how to recognize the root-bound decline that ends a pot tree's productive life if ignored. The scheduling backbone for the 10-year goal.

## Key Findings
- **Repot or root-prune-and-refresh every 2–3 years** (lemons commonly 3–4 yr; some sources 3–5 yr for larger pots).
- **Root-bound signs**: roots emerging from drainage holes, water running straight through or pooling on top (mix won't absorb), stunting, faster wilting.
- **Media degrades and sinks after ~5–6 years** — the substrate volume drops and the tree literally settles into the pot.
- Root-bound trees develop **circling roots that can girdle the trunk** (Topic 7) and lose water/nutrient uptake.
- For a tree meant to stay a fixed size, the cycle is **root-prune + media refresh in the same pot**, not endless up-sizing.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
The maintenance calendar for this pot. Because the 19"×14" is meant to be the long-term home (not a way-station), plan on a **late-winter root-prune + media refresh every 2–3 years** rather than up-potting — this resets root-bound decline and replaces degraded mix. Watch the field signs (roots out the holes, water no longer soaking in) as the trigger to do it sooner.
