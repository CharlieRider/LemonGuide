## Summary
Technical explainer on air-pruning fabric containers as an alternative to rigid pots for citrus. Relevant because a fabric pot changes the two variables that most threaten a shallow citrus container: root circling and the perched water table.

## Key Findings
- In a fabric (air-pruning) pot, root tips **desiccate and self-terminate on contact with air at the wall**, triggering lateral branching instead of circling — eliminating the root-girdling that plagues citrus in rigid 14"-deep pots.
- Breathable sidewalls **drain and aerate the whole profile**, shrinking the saturated perched-water zone that is proportionally largest in a shallow pot — directly raises effective air-filled porosity.
- Trade-off for coastal-LA summer: fabric walls **dry out faster and run cooler by evaporation** but demand more frequent irrigation; the evaporative cooling is a benefit for root-zone heat, the faster drying a cost in a small reservoir.
- Fabric pots are widely used by citrus nurseries specifically for these aeration/anti-circling properties.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Decision-relevant. A fabric or air-pruning pot is a strong candidate for the 14"-deep format: it mitigates both the enlarged perched-water zone and root circling, and the evaporative wall-cooling helps against west-balcony heat — at the cost of higher summer watering frequency (manage with the Topic-4 irrigation plan).
