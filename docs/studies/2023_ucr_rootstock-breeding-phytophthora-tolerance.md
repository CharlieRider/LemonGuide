## Summary
Summary of which citrus rootstocks resist Phytophthora root rot and gummosis — the disease most likely to attack roots in a wet/dry-cycling shallow container. Rootstock choice is a primary, built-in defense against the very pathogen the 14" pot's perched-water zone favors.

## Key Findings
- **Most Phytophthora-tolerant rootstocks: trifoliate orange, Swingle citrumelo, citrange (Carrizo/C-35/Troyer), Alemow, and sour orange.**
- **Flying Dragon and C-35 are trifoliate-lineage**, so the dwarfing rootstocks recommended for containers **also carry strong Phytophthora tolerance** — a double benefit.
- UCR's program breeds and trials rootstocks for California, selecting for Phytophthora and salinity tolerance among other traits.
- Phytophthora risk rises with prolonged root-zone wetness — exactly the shallow-pot failure mode (Topic 1/4).

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Strongly relevant and reassuring. The same trifoliate-based rootstocks chosen for dwarfing (Flying Dragon, C-35) are among the most Phytophthora-tolerant, so the container's elevated root-rot risk is partly mitigated by the rootstock that also controls size. This does not replace drainage discipline, but it stacks a genetic defense on top of it.
