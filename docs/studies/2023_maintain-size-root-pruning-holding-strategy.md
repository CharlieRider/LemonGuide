## Summary
The strategy — borrowed from bonsai/patio-fruit practice — for keeping a tree productively at a fixed size in the same container indefinitely by balancing root pruning against canopy. The mechanism that makes a permanent 19" pot viable for a vigorous Lisbon.

## Key Findings
- **Returning a tree to the same pot after root pruning, repeatedly, holds it at a stable size** — the canopy is sustained by continually renewed feeder roots rather than an ever-expanding root mass.
- **Shave 1–2 inches of the outer/circling roots** each cycle; pair with proportional canopy reduction to keep root-to-canopy balance.
- Works because **citrus tolerates root disturbance** when done in dormancy (late winter) and re-establishes quickly in the active season.
- Combined with a **dwarfing rootstock**, this lets even a vigorous scion stay manageable for many years in a modest pot.
- Over-frequent or excessive root removal stresses the tree — keep to the 2–3 year cycle and modest shave depth.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
This is the core technique that makes the stated goal achievable. Rather than chasing ever-larger pots, the Lisbon is held in the 19"×14" by **routine dormant root pruning (shave 1–2") + matched canopy pruning**, indefinitely. On a dwarfing rootstock this is very workable; on a standard rootstock it demands more frequent, careful root/canopy balancing. It is the long-term answer to "keep it manageable for 10+ years."
