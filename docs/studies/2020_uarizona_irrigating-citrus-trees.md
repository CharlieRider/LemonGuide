## Summary
Extension guidance on citrus irrigation intervals and the visible progression of water-stress symptoms. The symptom sequence is directly usable for reading a container tree, even though the interval figures are for in-ground desert trees.

## Key Findings
- In-ground citrus irrigation interval: **every 7–28 days** depending on season/soil — more often in heat, less in cool/wet periods.
- **Water-stress symptom sequence**: leaves first turn **dull green and curl/cup from the edges inward**, then dry crisp from the tip, then defoliate.
- **Reduced fruit size is an early (often unnoticed) drought sign**; stress at flowering increases fruit/flower drop.
- Deep, thorough irrigation that wets the whole root zone, then a dry-down interval, is preferred over frequent shallow watering for in-ground trees.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Symptoms transfer directly; intervals do not. A 14"-deep pot will dry far faster than the 7–28 day in-ground range — expect daily-to-every-few-days in summer. Use the **dull-green leaf curl** as the field cue that the small reservoir has run down, and never let it reach that point during bloom/fruit-set when stress costs yield.
