## Summary
Side-by-side comparison establishing how much more vigorous and thorny Lisbon is than the common alternative, Eureka. Quantifies the management burden that the container/pruning topics must address and explains why Lisbon-specific advice differs from generic "lemon" advice.

## Key Findings
- **Lisbon: vigorous, upright-spreading, large, thorny, dense, very productive.** **Eureka: medium vigor/size, open, virtually thornless, sparse** — Lisbon is markedly the more demanding of the two in a pot.
- **Lisbon can out-yield Eureka by up to ~25%** at wide spacing, and fruits more on the protected interior canopy.
- **Lisbon main crop is winter/early spring**; Eureka spreads its main crop over late winter–early summer with more year-round dribble.
- Lisbon is **more tolerant of frost, heat, wind, and neglect** than Eureka.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Directly relevant trade-off. Lisbon's higher yield and toughness come bundled with **more vigor and serious thorniness** — meaning a Lisbon in a 14" pot needs more pruning and careful handling than a Eureka would. The interior-fruiting habit also means size-control pruning must avoid stripping the protected inner wood that carries much of the crop. Confirms that for a manageable small-container tree, dwarfing rootstock is more important for Lisbon than for less vigorous lemons.
