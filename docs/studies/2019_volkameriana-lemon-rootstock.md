## Summary
Profile of Volkameriana (Volkamer lemon), a vigorous lemon-type rootstock prized for salt/alkaline tolerance and high yield. It is one of the better salt-adapted options — but its vigor and cold-sensitivity push against the manageable-container goal.

## Key Findings
- **Very vigorous** — stronger than sour orange, producing **strong, fast-growing trees with large root systems**, full canopies, **early bearing and high yield**.
- **Salt/alkaline tolerance**: grows well in **alkaline and saline soils**; rated **moderate salinity tolerance** (tetraploid Volkamer lines show even better salt tolerance via stronger antioxidant/osmotic adjustment).
- **Drought**: reasonable — ranked ~3rd among common rootstocks (behind Cleopatra mandarin and C. macrophylla) in comparative drought trials.
- **Disease**: resistant to tristeza, exocortis, and xyloporosis viroids — **but prone to Phytophthora root rot** and some nematodes (a vigorous-lemon-rootstock pattern).
- **Fruit quality**: generally poor on Volkamer **except for lemon scions**, where quality is acceptable — which is why it is used under lemons specifically.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
A genuine trade-off for the Lisbon. Volkamer's **salt and alkaline tolerance suits coastal-LA water**, and it pairs acceptably with lemon scions — but its **high vigor fights the small-container goal** and it is **Phytophthora-prone**, a real risk in a wet, shallow pot with poor drainage. If chosen for salt tolerance, it demands aggressive size control (root + canopy pruning) and a very free-draining mix. Generally a dwarfing trifoliate is the better container pick unless salinity is the overriding problem. Pairs with the rootstock-salinity, Phytophthora, and Alemow studies.
