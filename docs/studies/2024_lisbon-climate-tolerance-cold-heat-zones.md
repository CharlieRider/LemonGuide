## Summary
Defines Lisbon's temperature envelope and why it is the most climate-versatile common lemon — relevant to confirming the variety is well-matched to Brentwood's coastal climate and occasional extremes.

## Key Findings
- **USDA zones 9–11**; Brentwood/coastal LA is ~zone 10b–11a, squarely suitable.
- **Most cold- and heat-tolerant of the common lemons** and the most resistant to frost, heat, wind, and neglect (UCR).
- **Tolerates occasional light frost** but is damaged/killed below ~**30°F (-1°C)** — containers more vulnerable (Topic 9).
- **Does well in both valley heat and coastal cool** — more adaptable than Eureka (which prefers hot inland) — so it suits the marine-influenced balcony.
- **Ideal temperature band ~55–90°F (13–32°C)**; growth optimum 25–30°C (Topic 9).

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Confirms variety-climate fit. Lisbon is arguably the best common lemon choice for a coastal-LA balcony precisely because it handles both the cool marine mornings and hot west-facing afternoons. The ~30°F kill point rarely occurs in Brentwood, but the exposed container root zone means the Topic-9 cold-snap protections still apply on the rare radiative-cold night.
