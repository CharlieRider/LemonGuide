## Summary
Comparison of the two main inorganic aeration aggregates for citrus mixes. Both are pH-near-neutral and inert; the practical difference is density and durability, which matters for a long-lived, occasionally-moved balcony pot.

## Key Findings
- **Pumice (pH 7.0–7.4)**: heavier, **stays mixed and does not float or migrate to the surface**, durable over multi-year container life — favored where the mix should not stratify.
- **Perlite (pH 6.5–7.5)**: very lightweight, excellent aeration, but **floats to the surface over time and crushes/breaks down**, slowly losing aeration value in a long-term pot.
- Both are sterile, inert, and create the air pockets that keep the lower profile of a pot oxygenated; neither contributes meaningful CEC or nutrition.
- For long-term tree containers, pumice's durability and anti-flotation behavior generally outweighs perlite's lower weight.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Tilts the aggregate choice toward **pumice** for the 14"-deep citrus pot: it resists the surface-flotation and breakdown that would degrade aeration over the intended 10-year life, and its weight is acceptable for a pot that stays on the balcony. Perlite remains fine for a lighter mix if pot weight is a concern.
