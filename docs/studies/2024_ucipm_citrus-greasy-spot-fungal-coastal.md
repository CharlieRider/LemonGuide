## Summary
Foliar fungal disease (greasy spot and related) is favored by warm, humid, low-airflow conditions — the kind the marine layer plus a sheltered balcony can create. Lower-risk in dry California than in Florida, but worth monitoring on the coast.

## Key Findings
- **Greasy spot (Zasmidium/Mycosphaerella) produces yellow then greasy brown-black blisters on the leaf underside**, causing premature leaf drop; favored by **warm temperatures and prolonged leaf wetness/high humidity**.
- **Far less severe in arid California** than in humid Florida, but **coastal humidity and overhead watering raise risk** locally.
- Management: **improve air circulation, avoid prolonged leaf wetness (water at the soil, not overhead, late in the day), remove fallen leaves**, and use copper fungicide only if persistent.
- An open (not thinned) canopy and morning watering help leaves dry quickly.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Moderate, marine-layer-linked risk. Brentwood's morning marine layer keeps foliage damp, and a sheltered balcony reduces drying airflow — conditions that nudge greasy-spot and sooty-mold risk upward. Defense aligns with other topics: **water at the soil (not the canopy), water early so leaves dry, keep airflow with open structure, and clear fallen leaves.** Copper is a last resort for the home tree.
