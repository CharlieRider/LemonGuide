## Summary
Background on where Lisbon came from and how it became a dominant commercial lemon — useful context that explains its vigorous, hardy, thorny nature and its standing alongside Eureka.

## Key Findings
- **Portuguese origin**, believed developed from the **Gallego lemon**; name traces to Lisbon, Portugal.
- **Introduced to Australia c. 1824**; reached the **United States ~1843 (Massachusetts catalog)** and **California ~1849**.
- Became **one of the most popular commercial lemon cultivars in the US and Australia** (with Eureka, still the leading CA commercial lemons).
- Tree: **large, upright-spreading, dense, thorny, vigorous, cold- and heat-tolerant**.
- Fruit: medium, oblong with prominent nipple, **thinner peel than Eureka, slightly higher juice yield**, low-seeded, very acidic.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Context, not a how-to. Lisbon's heritage as a vigorous, hardy commercial tree explains the central container challenge documented throughout this repository: it was bred for productive field vigor, not compactness — hence the heavy reliance on dwarfing rootstock (Topic 5) and disciplined pruning (Topic 7) to fit it into a 14" pot. The thinner peel/higher juice is a quality upside for the home grower.
