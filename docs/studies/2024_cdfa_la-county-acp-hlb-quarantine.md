## Summary
The regulatory and disease context that overrides all other pest decisions for a Brentwood citrus tree: all of LA County is under an ACP quarantine and HLB (the fatal, incurable citrus disease the psyllid spreads) is established in the region. This shapes monitoring, treatment, and what you may legally move.

## Key Findings
- **All of Los Angeles County has been under ACP quarantine since 2009** (first detection Echo Park, Aug 2009).
- **HLB (Huanglongbing/citrus greening) is present in LA, Orange, Riverside, San Bernardino, San Diego, and Ventura counties** (first LA-area HLB: Hacienda Heights 2012, San Gabriel 2015).
- On HLB confirmation, **a 5-mile-radius quarantine** restricts purchase, sale, and movement of citrus plants, budwood, and sometimes fruit.
- **Do not move citrus plants or plant material out of the quarantine area**; CDFA inspects, samples, treats, and removes infected trees.
- HLB is **incurable and kills the tree**; the only defense is controlling the ACP vector and removing infected trees early.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Overriding context. The balcony Lisbon sits inside an active ACP/HLB zone, so: (1) **monitor for ACP on every new flush** (the psyllid feeds on tender growth — and Lisbon flushes often in this climate), (2) **never move the tree or cuttings out of the area**, (3) **report suspected HLB** (blotchy-mottle leaves, lopsided bitter fruit) to CDFA (1-800-491-1899). This is the single most consequential pest topic for long-term survival.
