## Summary
UC IPM guidance on the honeydew-producing sucking pests (mealybugs, soft and armored scales) and the sooty mold that follows them — common on sheltered container citrus where airflow is limited and natural enemies are fewer.

## Key Findings
- **Mealybugs and soft scales excrete honeydew → sooty mold grows on it**, blackening leaves/fruit and reducing photosynthesis and fruit grade (cosmetic).
- **Scout inside the canopy** for sooty mold, waxy mealybug masses, and scale crusts on leaves, twigs, and trunk.
- **Armored scales (California red scale)** don't make honeydew but encrust bark/fruit; monitored commercially with pheromone traps and degree-days.
- **Horticultural/narrow-range oil** smothers scale crawlers and mealybugs and is the home-appropriate control; **time to the crawler stage**.
- **Avoid broad-spectrum insecticides and ant traffic** — both protect these pests by removing natural enemies; control ants that farm honeydew.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Likely chronic, low-grade pests for a sheltered balcony tree. Limited airflow and few predators on a balcony favor mealybugs/scale and the resulting sooty mold. Practical program: **regular inside-canopy scouting, horticultural oil for outbreaks, control ants, and keep an open (not thinned) canopy for airflow** (Topic 7). Sooty mold itself is harmless to wipe off once the insects are controlled.
