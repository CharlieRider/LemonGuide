## Summary
Comparative root-heat study across four genera showing citrus has the least heat-tolerant root system of the group. Reinforces the thermal ceiling for container citrus roots and that heat damage is cumulative over daily exposure.

## Key Findings
- **Citrus had the shortest critical exposure duration of the four species**; at **50°C the predicted critical exposure was ~45 minutes** before root membrane injury — confirming the FSHS figure.
- Sustained **40°C root zone for 6 hr/day** produced the **highest shoot:root ratio** in citrus, i.e. root mass was suppressed relative to shoots — a stress signature of chronic supraoptimal root heat.
- Citrus and banana diverged: banana tolerated/responded oppositely to the same regime, underscoring that citrus is genuinely heat-sensitive at the root, not just a generic-plant response.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Directly extrapolable. A west-facing 14"-deep pot experiences exactly this scenario — many hours daily of supraoptimal root-zone heat in summer — which this study links to suppressed root growth and eventual injury. Argues for shading the pot wall and limiting the daily duration of root-zone heat, not just the peak.
