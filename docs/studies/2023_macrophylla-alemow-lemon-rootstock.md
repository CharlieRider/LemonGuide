## Summary
Alemow (*Citrus macrophylla*) is the rootstock most associated with California and Mediterranean **lemon** production — vigorous, salt- and lime-tolerant, very high-yielding, but cold-sensitive, drought-weak, and poor for non-lemon fruit quality. For a Lisbon it's the high-salt-tolerance option with significant container caveats.

## Key Findings
- **Vigorous and very high-yielding** under lemons; promotes **early bearing** and excellent adaptation to **calcareous (high-lime) and saline soils** — historically the standard CA lemon rootstock.
- Comparative trial: Alemow induced the **largest canopy (up to ~60% larger than sour orange)** but the **lowest yield efficiency (~1.8 kg/m³** vs. Carrizo/Swingle ~9.7/9.5) — it makes a big tree per unit fruit.
- **Salinity**: tolerant up to a threshold of **~1.02 dS/m**, then ~**14.2% yield loss per dS/m** increase above that; good active-lime tolerance.
- **Cold-sensitive** (imparts little cold hardiness) and **poor prolonged-drought tolerance** (takes water efficiently from moist soil but suffers when dry).
- **Fruit quality**: induces **low TSS/acidity in oranges/mandarins** — acceptable mainly with **lemon** scions.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
The salt/alkaline-soil champion for lemons — a real plus for coastal-LA water — and lemon-compatible. But the downsides hit a 14" pot hard: it drives a **large, vigorous canopy** (worst case for size control), is **drought-sensitive** (bad in a fast-drying shallow pot), and **cold-sensitive** (minor in LA). Net: choose Alemow only if salinity is the dominant problem and commit to heavy size management and very consistent watering. For most balcony Lisbons a dwarfing trifoliate is the better fit. Connects to the Volkamer, rootstock-salinity, drought-watering, and size/yield studies.
