## Summary
Describes Lisbon fruit quality attributes — high acidity, juiciness, near-seedlessness — that define the payoff of growing this variety and what good container fruit should look like.

## Key Findings
- Lisbon fruit: **medium, elliptical-oblong with a prominent nipple, finely pitted yellow rind, very acidic, juicy, few-to-no seeds** (true "supermarket lemon" profile, like Eureka).
- **High juice acidity** (citric) is the variety's hallmark — culinary-grade tartness.
- **Main crop winter/early spring**, with scattered fruit at other times in mild climates.
- Near-seedless fruit results from Lisbon's self/parthenocarpic set — no pollinizer needed for usable fruit.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Sets the quality target. A well-managed container Lisbon should produce juicy, high-acid, nearly seedless lemons comparable to commercial Eureka/Lisbon; thick-rinded, dry, low-acid fruit signals under-feeding or water stress (Topics 3–4). The winter main crop also tells you when to ease pruning and ensure steady moisture/nutrition for fruit fill.
