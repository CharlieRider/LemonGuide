## Summary
Actionable hot-weather management for citrus: how to prevent heat-wave damage and bark/fruit sunburn. The operational playbook for the hottest days on a west-facing balcony.

## Key Findings
- **Maintain a dense canopy and adequate hydration before heat** — well-watered, well-shaded trees resist heat far better; water-stressed trees sunburn and drop fruit.
- **Sunburn protection: reflective kaolin/whitewash on exposed bark and fruit**; keep interior wood shaded by canopy (don't over-thin — Topic 7).
- **During heat waves: provide afternoon shade (shade cloth ~30–50%), ensure the root zone is moist, and avoid pruning/fertilizing** that pushes vulnerable new growth.
- Heat damage often shows after the event as bleached/scorched leaves and fruit and increased drop.
- **Light-colored, shaded containers** keep the root zone cooler (Topic 9 container-color study).

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
The west-balcony heat-day checklist. On forecast hot days: **water the pot in the morning, deploy ~30–50% afternoon shade cloth over the canopy, optionally kaolin the fruit, and do no pruning/feeding.** Combine with the white/shaded pot for root cooling. The dense Lisbon canopy is an asset here (self-shading), reinforcing the Topic-7 rule not to thin it.
