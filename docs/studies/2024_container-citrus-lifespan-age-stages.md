## Summary
Sets realistic expectations for **how long a container citrus stays productive** and the age stages it passes through — the time horizon the whole "10-year container" goal is measured against, and what determines whether a potted Lisbon reaches it.

## Key Findings
- In the ground, citrus lives **~50–100 years**; **container trees typically live less** — a potted lemon can reach ~50 years but most don't, and **dwarf/indoor trees often <30 years**.
- **Bearing begins early**: most citrus fruit at **2–5 years** (grafted dwarfs sometimes by year 2); a juvenile period precedes this.
- **Productive maturity** spans the long middle years; **natural yield decline** sets in late (orchard trees ~50 years), but a neglected container tree declines far sooner from root-binding, salt buildup, and media collapse.
- **Container longevity is a maintenance outcome**, not fixed: replenishing media, periodic repotting/up-sizing every 1–2 years (early) then root-pruning to hold size, and using durable mineral-based media let a potted tree approach in-ground lifespan.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Grounds the project's "healthy, productive, 10+ years" goal: a container Lisbon **can easily make 10+ productive years** — that's well within reach and not even close to the limit — *if* the lifecycle maintenance is done. The limiting factor isn't the tree's biology but **root-bound decline, salt accumulation, and substrate breakdown**, all of which the other Topic-10 studies address (repotting, root pruning, salt flushing, media refresh, durable aggregates). Expect bearing within a couple of years and decades of potential output. Anchors the decline-cycle, repotting-interval, root-pruning, and substrate-degradation studies.
