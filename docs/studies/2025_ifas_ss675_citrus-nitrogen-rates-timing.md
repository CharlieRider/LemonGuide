## Summary
The authoritative IFAS chapter on how much nitrogen (and proportional P, K, Mg) citrus needs and when to apply it, broken out by tree age. It gives the **per-tree** young-tree rates that translate most directly to a single container Lisbon.

## Key Findings
- **Young (non-bearing) per-tree N**: Year 1 **0.15–0.30 lb N/tree/yr**, Year 2 **0.30–0.60**, Year 3 **0.45–0.90** — these are the most container-relevant numbers.
- Bearing trees scale by area: young bearing 120–200 lb N/acre/yr; mature 125–245 lb N/acre/yr (oranges).
- **K₂O is set equal to the N rate** (bump +25% if leaf K stays low); P₂O₅ for non-bearing ≈ 50–75% of N; Mg ≈ 20% of N if soil tests low.
- **Split frequency by method**: controlled-release ≥1×/yr; dry soluble 3–6×/yr; **fertigation/liquid ≥10×/yr** — more frequent, smaller doses for soluble feeds.
- **Timing (bearing trees)**: three equal increments — (1) Feb→flowering, (2) flowering→mid-June, (3) mid-June→mid-Oct; avoid heavy N into the late-fall/rainy window.
- Foliar micronutrient rates: Mn/Zn/Cu 3–5 lb metallic/acre, B ¼ lb/acre, applied at spring-flush full leaf expansion.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
The young-tree per-tree figures are the practical anchor: a young container Lisbon needs on the order of **~0.2–0.6 lb actual N per year**, split into many small doses (a 14" pot leaches fast, so lean toward the ≥10×/yr fertigation cadence or a controlled-release product). Hold K₂O ≈ N, and split N across the spring–early-fall growth window rather than dumping it. Florida's high-N grove rates per acre do *not* transfer to a pot — use the per-tree numbers and the controlled-release/leaching guidance.
