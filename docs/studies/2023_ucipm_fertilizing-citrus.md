## Summary
California's authoritative home-garden citrus fertilization guidance: the nitrogen schedule by tree age, application timing tied to bloom, and the explicit note that container/dwarf trees need less. The N backbone for a Brentwood Lisbon program.

## Key Findings
- Nitrogen by tree age (actual N/year): **Year 1 ≈ 1 Tbsp N, 3×/yr; Year 2 = 0.25 lb; Year 3 = 0.5 lb; Year 4 = 0.75 lb; Year 5+ = 1.0 lb** for a full-size tree.
- **1 lb actual N ≈ 5 lb ammonium sulfate ≈ 100 lb composted cow manure** per year.
- **Split timing: Jan–Feb just before bloom, again in May, perhaps a third in June.** Avoid late-season N — it roughens rind, delays coloring, hurts fruit quality.
- **Dwarf/container trees with restricted root space need proportionally less** than the full-size rates.
- Most other nutrients are usually adequate in CA garden soil; N is the routine limiter — but containers are the exception (see micronutrient/leaching studies).
- Correct micronutrient deficiency with a **spring foliar chelated micronutrient spray** on emerging growth, or soil-applied sulfated forms (zinc/iron sulfate).

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Core program reference, with a caveat: the 1 lb-N full-size rate must be cut substantially for a 14"-deep pot, and — unlike in-ground CA trees — container leaching means micronutrients are NOT reliably adequate (see UF/IFAS and CRF studies). Adopt the Jan/May/June split but deliver it as smaller, more frequent ("weekly weakly") container doses.
