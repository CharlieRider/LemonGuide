## Summary
How to buy a healthy, disease-free Lisbon — the single most consequential decision for a long-lived container tree, since an infected or poorly grafted tree can't be fixed later. Centers on California's Citrus Clonal Protection Program (CCPP) certification.

## Key Findings
- **Buy a tree budded from CCPP-certified disease-free budwood**: the CCPP eliminates diseases from each variety before release, and registered budwood is **tested annually for Tristeza (CTV), Huanglongbing (HLB), and vein enation; every 3 years for viroids; every 6 years for psorosis**.
- Certified mother/increase trees are kept in **insect-proof structures** (ACP exclusion) — critical given the LA HLB quarantine.
- **Buy from a reputable nursery** to avoid inadvertently importing disease (especially HLB/ACP) into the quarantine zone.
- **Choose the rootstock deliberately** for the use case — for a manageable container, a **size-controlling/dwarfing trifoliate (e.g., Flying Dragon)** rather than a vigorous lemon rootstock (see rootstock studies).
- **Inspect the tree**: a clean, healed graft union above the soil; no circling/girdling roots (slide it out to check); no signs of pests, scale, sooty mold, or HLB-mottled leaves; sturdy, well-branched form.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
The foundation purchase for the Brentwood Lisbon: insist on a **CCPP-certified tree on a dwarfing rootstock**, bought from a reputable California nursery — this protects against bringing HLB/ACP into a quarantine county and sets up the size control a 14" pot needs. Inspect the graft and roots before buying. A good start here prevents most of the chronic problems the rest of the repository works to manage.
