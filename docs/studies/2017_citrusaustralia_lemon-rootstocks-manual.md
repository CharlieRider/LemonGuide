## Summary
Lemon-specific rootstock guidance from a national production manual — useful because it evaluates rootstocks against the *lemon* scion rather than oranges/mandarins, including vigor, disease, and salinity trade-offs relevant to a Mediterranean coastal climate.

## Key Findings
- For lemons, common rootstocks include **trifoliate orange and its hybrids (Troyer/Carrizo citrange, Swingle citrumelo), rough lemon, Volkamer lemon, and sour orange** — each trading vigor against disease and salinity tolerance.
- **Trifoliate/citrange rootstocks**: more compact, better fruit quality, **good Phytophthora tolerance**, but less salt-tolerant.
- **Rough/Volkamer lemon**: very vigorous, drought/salinity-hardy, but produce large trees and lower-quality fruit — wrong direction for a container.
- **Sour orange**: good quality and disease tolerance but susceptible to tristeza virus.
- Rootstock strongly affects **chloride/sodium exclusion**, hence salinity tolerance — relevant where irrigation water is hard.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Confirms the container logic: for a small, manageable, good-quality Lisbon, favor **trifoliate-lineage rootstocks (Flying Dragon ideally, then C-35/Carrizo/Swingle)** and avoid the vigorous rough/Volkamer lemons that would overwhelm a 14" pot. Note the salinity trade-off — trifoliate rootstocks exclude chloride less well, which raises the importance of the Topic-4 leaching discipline with hard LA water.
