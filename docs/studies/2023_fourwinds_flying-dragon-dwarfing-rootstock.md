## Summary
Nursery technical reference on Flying Dragon as the dwarfing rootstock of choice for container citrus, including lemons. Practical complement to the peer-reviewed review, framed for home/container growers.

## Key Findings
- Flying Dragon is described as the **only true dwarfing citrus rootstock**; scions reach **~30–50% of normal size** (mature trees ~2.5 m).
- Its **dwarfing effect holds across lemons** and other vigorous scions (Persian lime, etc.) — directly applicable to vigorous Lisbon.
- **Slows winter metabolism**, giving better cold hardening — useful for the occasional LA cold snap.
- Improved Dwarf Meyer on Flying Dragon is cited as one of the most dependable container lemons — evidence the rootstock performs in pots.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Decisive recommendation. For a vigorous Lisbon intended to stay manageable in a 14"-deep pot for 10+ years, **Flying Dragon is the rootstock to specify when buying the tree.** Its 30–50% size reduction is the difference between a perpetually-overgrown pot tree and one that holds scale with reasonable pruning. If the purchased tree is on a standard rootstock (Carrizo, etc.), expect substantially more pruning and earlier size problems.
