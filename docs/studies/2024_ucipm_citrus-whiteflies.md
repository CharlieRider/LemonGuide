## Summary
UC IPM guidance on whiteflies (woolly, ash, citrus whitefly) — sap-sucking insects whose main harm is copious honeydew → sooty mold. The key message is that they are usually under excellent biological control and rarely need spraying.

## Key Findings
- Whiteflies are **not normally a serious pest** of citrus; populations can build but **biological control usually keeps them in check**.
- **Woolly whitefly** (curly waxy filaments on leaf undersides) is under **complete biological control** in California via the parasitoids *Amitus spiniferus* and *Cales noacki*; **ash whitefly** is similarly well-controlled by introduced parasitoids; *Clitostethus arcuatus* beetle preys on several whitefly species.
- Damage is indirect: **honeydew → sooty mold** (cosmetic; heavy coating can reduce photosynthesis); ants tending whiteflies worsen it.
- **The main mistake is spraying**: imidacloprid (foliar), pyrethroids, and carbaryl **kill the natural enemies** and flare whitefly (and mites) — avoid them.
- Management: inspect leaf undersides above honeydew/sooty mold; control ants; tolerate and conserve parasitoids; wash off sooty mold if desired.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Reassuring and low-effort for the Lisbon: whiteflies on a balcony tree are **best handled by doing little** — conserve the parasitoids (don't spray broad-spectrum insecticides), control ants (which protect whiteflies — see ant study), and rinse sooty mold off foliage. A strong water spray to leaf undersides knocks down adults if numbers climb. Pairs with the ant-management, scale/sooty-mold, and IPM-treatment studies.
