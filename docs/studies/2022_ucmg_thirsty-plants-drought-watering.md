## Summary
Coastal-California UC extension guidance on efficient watering under drought and water restrictions — the regulatory and climatic context for irrigating a Brentwood balcony tree, where summer is fully irrigation-dependent.

## Key Findings
- **Containers dry far faster than ground beds** and need more frequent watering, especially in wind and heat — balconies add both.
- **Water deeply enough to wet the entire root ball and produce some drainage**, then allow appropriate dry-down — matches the leaching-fraction approach for salt control.
- **Mulch the surface and group/shade pots** to cut evaporative loss during drought and heat.
- Early-morning watering reduces evaporative loss and gives roots water before peak afternoon demand (relevant to west-facing afternoon heat).

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Climate- and regulation-matched. Confirms the 14" balcony pot is fully irrigation-dependent in summer, that **deep-water-to-slight-drainage** (the leaching-fraction practice) is correct, and that **early-morning watering + surface mulch** are the right tactics against west-facing afternoon heat and wind desiccation (Topic 9 link).
