## Summary
Capstone integrating the repository's findings into a go/no-go and how-to verdict for growing Lisbon lemon in a 19"×14" pot on a west-facing Brentwood balcony for 10+ years. The single-page decision summary.

## Key Findings
- **Variety fit: strong.** Lisbon is the most climate-versatile common lemon — handles coastal cool mornings and hot west afternoons (zone 10b–11a), self-fertile, productive, high-quality acidic fruit.
- **Main risks, all manageable:** (1) **vigor + thorns** → use **dwarfing rootstock (Flying Dragon)** + a less-thorny strain (Yen Ben) + disciplined post-harvest pruning; (2) **shallow-pot root heat/hypoxia** → coarse bark+pumice mix at 15–20% air-filled porosity, **white/shaded/double-potted container**, elevated for drainage; (3) **salt/leaching with hard LA water** → ~15–30% leaching fraction each watering + monthly deep flush + steady "weekly weakly" micronutrient feeding; (4) **ACP/HLB quarantine** → scout every flush, never move the tree, report symptoms.
- **Lifecycle:** late-winter root-prune + media refresh + salt flush every 2–3 years holds the tree in the 19" pot indefinitely.
- **Realistic yield:** a few dozen fruit/year at maturity, winter main crop.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
This is the actionable bottom line. A **dwarf (Flying Dragon) Lisbon, ideally a low-thorn strain, in a light-colored/double-potted 19"×14" pot with a coarse bark-pumice mix**, fed weekly-weakly with micronutrients, watered to a deliberate leaching fraction, heat-managed on the west exposure, scouted for ACP, and serviced (root-prune/refresh/flush) every 2–3 years, is a sound 10-year plan. Every claim here is detailed in Topics 1–10; this file is the index to that plan.
