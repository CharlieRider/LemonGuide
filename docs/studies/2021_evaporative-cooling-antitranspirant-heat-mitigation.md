## Summary
Reviews active heat-mitigation techniques — **evaporative cooling (overhead misting/sprinkling)** and **antitranspirant/particle-film sprays** — that lower canopy and fruit temperature during heat waves. The "during the heat wave" toolkit complementing passive measures like shade and pot insulation.

## Key Findings
- **Evaporative cooling** (keeping surfaces wet so evaporation pulls out heat) is one of the most effective ways to **lower fruit and leaf temperature** and reduce sunburn on hot days.
- **Cycle misters/sprinklers on and off** — let water mostly evaporate (the cooling step), but re-wet before surfaces dry fully; continuous soaking wastes water and waterlogs.
- Measured effect: tree transpiration dropped **~50%** under overhead micro-spray cooling (vs. ~5% in uncooled controls) — the plant's own water loss is sharply reduced.
- **Evaporative cooling does not block UV**, so it's paired with **particle films (kaolin/calcium carbonate)** or shade for sunburn protection.
- **Antitranspirant / reflective sprays** reduce water loss and heat load but must be used cautiously (can also limit gas exchange).

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Scalable down to a single pot: during an LA heat wave, **light misting of the Lisbon's canopy in the heat of the day (cycled, not soaking)** can shave canopy/fruit temperature and reduce sunburn — and on a balcony it's as simple as a hose mister or spray bottle on the worst afternoons. Mind the trade-offs: don't keep foliage chronically wet (fungal/greasy-spot risk in the marine layer), and don't waterlog the shallow pot. Best combined with **shade cloth + kaolin** (which handle the UV the cooling doesn't). Pairs with the kaolin-film, shade-cloth, marine-layer, VPD/stomatal, and heat-wave-protocol studies.
