## Summary
Space-saving training forms (espalier, compact open shapes) suited to citrus in tight spots like balconies. Offers a structural strategy beyond simple size-reduction for a constrained west-facing space.

## Key Findings
- **Citrus espaliers well** and is considered forgiving; espalier typically needs **pruning twice a year (summer and winter)**.
- Espalier/flat training keeps the tree **against a wall or rail**, ideal where balcony depth is limited.
- For a free-standing patio tree, **tip-prune terminal buds to force outward, bushy growth** rather than upward/leggy form.
- Open-center/vase forms improve light and air to the interior while keeping outer density.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Optional but well-suited. A west-facing balcony rail is a natural espalier support, and a flat-trained Lisbon would occupy minimal floor space while presenting maximum leaf area to the afternoon sun (manage heat per Topic 9). If not espaliering, the tip-prune-for-bushiness approach keeps the tree compact and stable in the shallow pot. Note espalier's twice-yearly pruning load given Lisbon's vigor and thorns.
