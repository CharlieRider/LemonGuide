## Summary
The staged pruning plan over a container tree's life — heavier structural shaping when young, light maintenance when mature. Provides the timeline that ties pruning into the long-term lifecycle (Topic 10).

## Key Findings
- **Years 1–3 (establishment): build the framework** — select well-spaced scaffold branches, remove suckers and inward/crossing growth; light overall removal (~10–15%/yr).
- **Mature phase: light maintenance only** — shape, remove deadwood/suckers, contain height, keep canopy dense.
- **Prune just after the main harvest** so new flushes and fruit wood are preserved.
- Establish a **clear single or few-leader structure early**, before the vigorous Lisbon sets awkward heavy limbs that later require large (sunburn-exposing) cuts.
- Pair structural development with the **2–3 year root-pruning/repotting cycle**.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Gives the multi-year plan. For the 14"-pot Lisbon: invest in framework shaping in years 1–3 (when thorny suckers are most vigorous), then shift to light annual maintenance timed after the winter harvest. Getting the structure right early avoids the large later cuts that would expose bark to west-balcony sun and trigger regrowth. Synchronize with root pruning for whole-plant balance.
