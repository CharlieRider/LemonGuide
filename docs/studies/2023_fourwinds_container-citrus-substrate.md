## Summary
Guidance from a major California citrus nursery on container media, including the popular bark-based 5-1-1 mix and a warning about wetting-agent-laden indoor potting mixes. California-sourced, so the climate framing matches coastal LA better than Southeast references.

## Key Findings
- Recommends a **fast-draining bark-based mix** (e.g. 5-1-1: ~5 parts bark to 1 part each of a citrus/cactus mix and aeration component) that **breaks down slowly** and preserves structure for years.
- **Avoid indoor potting mixes with chemical wetting agents** — they hold water around roots too long, a serious risk in a shallow pot.
- Bark fines supply the chunky, non-compacting structure that keeps drainage and aeration high over a multi-year container life.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Climate-matched and directly applicable. Endorses a durable bark-dominant mix for the 14" pot and specifically warns off the water-retentive wetting-agent mixes that would keep the shallow pot's perched-water zone saturated. Use this as the nursery-side cross-check on the Biology Insights recipe.
