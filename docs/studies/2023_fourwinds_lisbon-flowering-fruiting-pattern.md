## Summary
Lisbon-specific description of bloom timing, flushing, and crop timeline — the framework for scheduling pruning, feeding, and harvest on a Brentwood balcony. Lisbon's multi-flush habit in a mild climate is the opportunity (and the management challenge).

## Key Findings
- Lisbon has **one heavy main bloom in late winter–early spring**, plus **secondary flushes through summer and autumn** (3 flushes: spring/summer/autumn).
- **Main crop ripens in winter/early spring**; fruit is ready **6–9 months after flowering**.
- In mild/warm climates, dwarf Lisbon can produce **fruit nearly year-round**, with flushing **triggered by warm temperatures + adequate moisture** rather than strict dormancy.
- Heavy bloom attracts bees/hummingbirds, but lemons are largely **self-fertile** — a single balcony tree sets fruit without a pollinizer.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Directly Lisbon-specific and central. Coastal LA's mild climate means the balcony Lisbon will likely flush and fruit multiple times per year — so feeding must be steady year-round (Topic 3) and major structural pruning should be timed to protect the dominant spring bloom (Topic 7). Self-fertility means no second tree is needed for fruit set in the container.
