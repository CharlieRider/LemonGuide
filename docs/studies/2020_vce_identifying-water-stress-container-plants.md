## Summary
Extension guide on detecting water stress specifically in container plants — both under- and over-watering — and the monitoring methods (weight, probes, visual) that replace guesswork in a small reservoir.

## Key Findings
- **Container pot weight is the most reliable field signal**: a well-watered pot is heavy, a depleted one noticeably light — lifting/hefting the pot beats surface-feel.
- Under-watering shows as wilting, leaf curl, dull color; **over-watering mimics drought** (wilting, yellowing) because rotted roots cannot take up water — the two are easily confused.
- Recommends **direct moisture monitoring** (probe/tensiometer, or scheduled check of the top few inches) rather than fixed calendars, because container drying rate swings with weather.
- Confined container root systems make plants **far less buffered** against missed irrigations than in-ground plants.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Very applicable. The "wilting despite wet soil = root rot, not thirst" warning is exactly the trap a shallow citrus pot sets after winter rain. Adopt **pot-weight + top-3-inch finger check** as the routine, since the 14" pot's drying rate is too variable for a fixed schedule.
