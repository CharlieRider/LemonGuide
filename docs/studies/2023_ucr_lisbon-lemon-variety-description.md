## Summary
The authoritative UC Riverside varietal description of Lisbon lemon — the single most Lisbon-specific reference in the repository. Defines the vigor, thorniness, and fruiting habit that drive every container-management decision for this scion.

## Key Findings
- Tree is **vigorous, upright-spreading, large, thorny, densely foliated, and highly productive** — explicitly the **most vigorous lemon grown in California**.
- **Outstanding resistance to frost, heat, wind, and neglect** — robust scion, but the vigor is the container challenge, not the climate tolerance.
- Fruit: medium, elliptical-to-oblong with a prominent nipple, finely pitted rind, **very acid**, few-to-no seeds.
- **Main crop in winter and early spring**, with multiple crops annually possible in mild climates.
- This accession is maintained on Yuma Ponderosa lemon rootstock (a CVC curation choice, not a container recommendation).

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Foundational and directly Lisbon-specific. The documented extreme vigor and thorniness are exactly why a 14" pot demands (1) a strongly dwarfing rootstock such as Flying Dragon and (2) committed structural pruning and thorn management — for this scion these are mandatory, not optional. The winter main-crop timing also frames the fruiting and pruning-timing topics.
