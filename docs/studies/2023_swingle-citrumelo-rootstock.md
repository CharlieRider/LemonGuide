## Summary
Profile of Swingle citrumelo (grapefruit × trifoliate hybrid), a disease-resistant trifoliate-type rootstock. Strong on Phytophthora/nematode/CTV but, like the citranges, hampered by high-pH alkaline soils — directly relevant to whether it suits coastal-LA water.

## Key Findings
- **Disease/pest**: tolerant of tristeza (CTV) and *Phytophthora parasitica* root rot, and tolerant of citrus nematode; vigorous, uniform seedlings with an extensive root system.
- Phytophthora resistance is **good but below pure trifoliate (Poncirus)**.
- **Cold**: among the more cold-tolerant rootstocks (ranks with trifoliate/sour orange/Cleopatra).
- **Salt/pH — the limitation**: **sensitive to high chloride** in soil/water, though **more salt-tolerant than Carrizo/Troyer citranges**; **sensitive to high-pH/calcareous soils**, where trees develop **lime-induced chlorosis**.
- Gives a medium-to-large (standard) tree — not a size-control rootstock.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Partial fit. Swingle's excellent Phytophthora/CTV/nematode resistance is valuable for a wet-prone shallow pot, and it beats the citranges on salt — but its **high-pH intolerance (lime chlorosis)** is a concern given alkaline LA tap water, and it does **not control size**. For this container the disease resistance is the draw; if used, manage root-zone pH (acidify, watch for Fe/Mn chlorosis — see pH-management study) and control size by root pruning. A dwarfing trifoliate remains the better size match. Connects to the Carrizo, Flying Dragon, Phytophthora-tolerance, and pH-management studies.
