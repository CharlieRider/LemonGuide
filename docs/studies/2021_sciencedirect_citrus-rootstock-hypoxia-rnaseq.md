## Summary
Transcriptomic study of citrus rootstock seedlings under root-zone hypoxia, characterizing the physiological and gene-expression response to low oxygen. Provides mechanistic confirmation that citrus roots mount a measurable but limited stress response to the exact low-O₂ conditions a saturated shallow pot creates.

## Key Findings
- Root-zone hypoxia in citrus seedlings triggered coordinated changes in genes for **fermentation/anaerobic metabolism, hormone signaling, and antioxidant defense** — the energy-crisis pathway invoked when aerobic respiration is starved.
- Confirms citrus mounts a **reactive, not pre-adaptive** hypoxia response (no aerenchyma); tolerance differences between rootstocks are differences in *biochemical coping*, not avoidance.
- Reinforces that prevention (aeration, drainage) beats tolerance breeding for backyard container culture, where genotype is fixed by what the nursery grafted.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Mechanistic support, not field numbers. It validates that the low-oxygen conditions in a poorly drained 14" pot reach the citrus root and switch on stress metabolism — meaning the practical lever is substrate aeration and irrigation control, since the tree itself cannot structurally adapt.
