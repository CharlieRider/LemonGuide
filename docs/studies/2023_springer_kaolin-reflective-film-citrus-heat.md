## Summary
Tests kaolin particle film (a reflective white clay spray) as a protectant for citrus under combined heat and high-light stress — a practical, home-usable canopy treatment for a sun-exposed balcony tree.

## Key Findings
- **Kaolin film reflects solar radiation off leaf/fruit surfaces**, lowering leaf temperature and reducing photo-oxidative and heat stress under high-temperature, high-light conditions.
- Treated citrus showed **better photosynthetic performance and less stress/oxidative damage** than untreated controls.
- Kaolin is widely used commercially to **prevent fruit sunburn** and ease heat load on citrus.
- It is a benign, washable mineral coating compatible with home/organic use.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Practical canopy tool. On a west-facing balcony, a kaolin spray (e.g. Surround) on the canopy and exposed fruit during heat waves reduces leaf temperature and sunburn without chemicals — complementing the white-pot/shade strategy for the root zone. Useful specifically on the hottest days and for protecting fruit on the sun-facing side. Reapply after rain/overhead watering.
