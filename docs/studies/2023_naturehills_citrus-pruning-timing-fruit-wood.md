## Summary
Guidance on when to prune lemons to avoid removing fruiting wood, and the open-but-dense canopy goal. The timing rule that ties pruning (Topic 7) to the flowering cycle (Topic 6).

## Key Findings
- **Prune late winter–early spring before new growth, after frost risk passes** — OR right after the spring bloom reveals where fruit will set.
- **Avoid late-summer/fall pruning**: it pushes tender growth vulnerable to cold and removes wood carrying next season's buds.
- **Fruiting wood forms on last summer's growth** and on the inner/protected canopy — heavy late-spring/summer cuts destroy the coming crop.
- **Remove suckers and dead/crossing branches; open the center for light/air but do not strip the canopy** (sunburn risk).
- **Tip-prune terminal buds** to push outward/bushy growth and keep a compact patio shape.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Directly operational. For the Brentwood Lisbon (winter main crop), do structural pruning **right after the winter harvest / late winter**, before the spring flush — this removes the least fruiting wood. Use tip-pruning through the year to keep the compact shape a balcony needs, and never hard-prune in summer when both heat stress and fruit-wood loss compound.
