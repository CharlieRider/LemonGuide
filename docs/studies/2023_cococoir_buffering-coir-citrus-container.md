## Summary
Technical guidance on why raw coco coir must be buffered before use as a citrus substrate component, and the pH/EC targets to hit. Relevant because coir is the most attractive water-retention component for a fast-draining bark mix but carries a sodium/potassium liability that buffering corrects.

## Key Findings
- Raw coir's cation-exchange sites are **naturally loaded with Na⁺ and K⁺ and depleted in Ca²⁺/Mg²⁺**; without buffering it will dump Na/K and lock out Ca/Mg when fertilized.
- **Buffering = soaking saturated coir in a Ca/Mg solution for 8–24 hours** to let Ca/Mg displace the native Na/K to equilibrium before planting.
- Targets: **coir pH 5.5–6.5**; growing-media **EC < 1.0 mS/cm** so residual salts do not interfere with uptake.
- Coastal-grown coir can carry high salt loads, so a **fresh-water rinse plus Ca/Mg buffer** is the standard pre-treatment.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Directly actionable. If coir is used (≈20–30%) to add water-holding to a bark/pumice mix, buffer and rinse it first — otherwise the shallow pot, already prone to salt accumulation, starts with a Na load and a Ca deficit that worsen the micronutrient lock-out citrus is prone to at the balcony's elevated tap-water pH.
