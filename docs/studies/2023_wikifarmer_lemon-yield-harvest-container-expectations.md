## Summary
Baseline lemon yield and bearing-timeline figures for field trees, used here to set realistic, scaled-down expectations for a 14"-container Lisbon. Anchors the gap between commercial yields and what a small pot can deliver.

## Key Findings
- Field lemon trees **begin bearing in year 3–4**, reach **peak productivity at ~8–15 years**, mature yield **~85–150 kg/tree/year** (full-size, in-ground).
- **Flower-to-harvest interval 4–12 months (commonly 6–9)** — matches the Lisbon timeline.
- **Warmer, humid climates yield larger, juicier, higher-acid fruit** with more peel oil — coastal LA is moderate, so expect good but not tropical-maximum quality.
- Yield depends heavily on water, nutrition, and pest management — all grower-controllable.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Calibrates expectations. A dwarfing-rootstock Lisbon in a 14" pot will yield a small fraction of the 85–150 kg field figure — realistically a few dozen fruit per year at maturity — because canopy and root volume are deliberately constrained. The value here is the bearing timeline (first fruit ~year 3–4, scaled earlier on dwarfing stock) and the reminder that container yield is set mostly by consistent water/nutrition, not by the pot ever matching field output.
