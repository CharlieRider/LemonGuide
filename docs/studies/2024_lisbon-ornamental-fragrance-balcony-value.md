## Summary
Captures the non-fruit value of a container Lisbon — evergreen structure, fragrant bloom, and ornamental fruit display — which for a balcony specimen is half the point. Rounds out the Lisbon-specific topic with the amenity/landscape dimension.

## Key Findings
- Lemon trees combine **glossy broad-leaved evergreen foliage, intensely fragrant waxy white blossoms, and showy edible fruit** in one container-friendly plant — year-round ornamental interest.
- **Fragrance** is a signature asset: clusters of highly perfumed white blooms (often multiple flushes in mild climates) scent a patio/balcony.
- **Evergreen, year-round structure** makes a single potted lemon function as a living screen / patio centerpiece, framing seating or entries.
- In containers the tree **stays smaller than its in-ground potential but still flowers and sets full-sized fruit** — ornamental and productive at once; pot ≥ ~12–15" deep recommended (the 14" pot qualifies).
- Lisbon specifically adds **dense evergreen foliage** (good screening) and **toughness**, at the cost of thorns (a handling/safety consideration on a balcony).

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Frames the Brentwood Lisbon as an **edible-ornamental balcony amenity**, not just a fruit source: dense evergreen foliage gives privacy/screening on the balcony, the fragrant multi-flush bloom is a year-round sensory feature in mild LA, and developing/ripe lemons are decorative. The main amenity caveat is **thorniness** — site the pot so the thorny canopy isn't a hazard at seating/walkway height, and manage thorns on reachable growth (see thorn-management study). This is the payoff context the whole repository's husbandry serves. Connects to the comprehensive-care, growth-habit, flowering, and Brentwood-suitability-synthesis studies.
