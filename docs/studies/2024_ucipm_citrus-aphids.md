## Summary
UC IPM guidance on citrus aphids, which colonize tender new flush. Mostly a self-correcting cosmetic pest — but relevant because aphids and the ACP both target the same tender growth and because aphids feed sooty-mold-producing honeydew.

## Key Findings
- **Aphids cluster on tender new growth**, curling young leaves and excreting honeydew (→ sooty mold and ant traffic).
- **Natural enemies normally control aphids within ~6 weeks; insecticide is rarely warranted.**
- **Problem mainly on young trees and continually flushing varieties** — numbers fall once foliage hardens off.
- One species (brown citrus aphid, not yet widespread in CA) is an efficient virus vector; general aphids are not the HLB vector (that's the psyllid).
- Hosing off colonies and protecting natural enemies is usually sufficient.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Low-action but worth monitoring. The frequently-flushing balcony Lisbon will get aphid colonies on new growth, but they self-resolve in weeks — **avoid spraying**, just rinse heavy colonies and control ants. The practical reason to watch aphids here is that **their tender-flush feeding sites are the same ones you must inspect for ACP** (Topic 8 priority pest), so aphid scouting doubles as psyllid scouting.
