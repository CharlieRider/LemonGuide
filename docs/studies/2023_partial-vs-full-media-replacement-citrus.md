## Summary
Compares the two media-refresh strategies — partial (topdress/outer-shell replacement) vs full (bare-root and re-pot in all-new mix) — and when each is appropriate. The practical decision at each maintenance cycle.

## Key Findings
- **Partial replacement**: remove and replace the outer/old shell of mix and topdress fresh media at each root-prune — **less stressful, preserves most feeder roots**, good for routine 2–3 year maintenance.
- **Full replacement (bare-root)**: wash off all old mix and repot in entirely fresh substrate — **more stressful but fully resets** degraded media and flushes accumulated salt; reserve for badly degraded/salty mix or disease.
- **Topdressing alone does not fix a degraded lower profile** — the perched-water zone at the bottom is where fines and salt concentrate, so periodic deeper replacement is needed.
- Time either operation for **late winter/early spring** dormancy; keep the crown above the line.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Guides each refresh cycle. For the 14" pot's routine 2–3 year service, **partial replacement (shave outer roots, replace outer/bottom mix, topdress)** is the lower-risk default. Do a **full bare-root replacement less often** (e.g. every other cycle, or when the mix is badly degraded/salty or root rot appears) since the shallow pot's bottom zone is exactly where fines and salt collect. Always pair with the deep salt flush (this topic) on the refreshed mix.
