## Summary
The companion to the Part 2 article already in this repo. Where Part 2 showed *how container height sets air-filled porosity*, Part 3 gives the **target numbers**: how much air and how much water a container medium should hold at container capacity, and the total-porosity budget that must be split between them.

## Key Findings
- **Air-filled porosity (AFP) target: ≥10% and generally ≤25%** of total medium volume at container capacity. Below ~10% roots run short of oxygen; above ~25% the medium dries too fast for a daily-watered pot.
- **Water-holding capacity target: ≥40%** of total volume filled with water at container capacity — roughly enough to carry most plants ~1 day between irrigations.
- **Total porosity should be ≥50%** (AFP + WHC), i.e. air and water together fill at least half the pot volume.
- Worked examples (measured): UC Mix 11% air / 62% water / 73% total; peat+perlite 20% / 73% / 93%; peat+vermiculite 13% / 81% / 94% — note all three already meet the ≥40% water and ≥10% air minimums.
- Oxygen *diffusion rate*, not AFP alone, correlates most closely with growth — a coarse mix that keeps air channels open beats one that merely tests at 12% AFP.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Directly usable as the spec for the Lisbon's mix: aim for ≥10–20% AFP and ≥40% water at container capacity. Because a 14" pot holds a deeper perched water zone than a tall nursery pot, target the **upper** end of AFP (≈20%) at fill so the bottom inches still clear ~10% after the perched table forms. Use with the Part 2 container-height/AFP study and the 10–12% hypoxia threshold.
