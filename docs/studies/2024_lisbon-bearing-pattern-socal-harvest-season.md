## Summary
Describes when a Lisbon actually bears and ripens — its cropping pattern and the Southern California harvest window. Lets a Brentwood grower know what to expect month-to-month, distinct from the generic flowering/fruit-development studies.

## Key Findings
- Lisbon bears **fruit much of the year but concentrates into a strong main crop** — heaviest in **fall through winter into spring**, versus Eureka's more even year-round spread.
- **Regional pattern**: in cooler Northern-California coastal valleys Lisbon bears closer to year-round; in most warmer California areas it bears **winter into summer** with a defined peak.
- **California lemon harvest peaks ~January–February**; fruit is picked from late fall through early spring as it colors fully yellow and glossy.
- Lisbon's value as a global commercial lemon rests on **temperature-fluctuation tolerance, self-fertility, and multiple harvests** through the year.
- Lemons hold well on the tree, so harvest timing is flexible — pick when fully colored and heavy.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Sets harvest expectations for the Brentwood Lisbon: expect the **largest flush of ripe fruit in late fall through winter/early spring**, with scattered fruit at other times thanks to the everbearing tendency — coastal LA's mild winters favor a long, productive window. Because lemons store on-tree, the container grower can pick progressively rather than all at once. This bearing rhythm aligns with the care calendar (feed Feb–Oct, ease off in winter) and the flowering/fruit-development timeline.
