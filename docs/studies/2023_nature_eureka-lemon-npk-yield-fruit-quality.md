## Summary
Field study on how NPK supply affects lemon yield and fruit quality, with concrete juice/acidity/TSS numbers. Lemon-specific (Citrus limon), so the quality benchmarks transfer better than orange data; links nutrition (Topic 3) to fruit outcomes (Topic 6).

## Key Findings
- **Juice quality responded positively to higher NPK rates**: top values included **juice acidity ~6.25%, TSS ~9.45%, vitamin C ~4.27 mg/100 g**.
- TSS, juice acidity, TSS/acid ratio, and vitamin C all improved with adequate NPK — under-fertilized lemons make lower-quality juice.
- Partial substitution of chemical NPK with organic sources sustained productivity while maintaining quality — fully organic was not required for good fruit.
- Confirms lemon fruit quality is **nutrition-responsive**, not fixed by variety alone.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Useful quality benchmark. Lisbon (like Eureka) needs adequate, balanced NPK to reach good juice acidity/TSS — reinforcing that the leaching-prone container must be fed consistently (Topic 3) to get high-acid, juicy lemons rather than thick-rind, low-juice fruit. The ~6.25% acidity / ~9.45% TSS figures are reasonable targets for well-fed container lemons.
