## Summary
Lisbon-specific pruning and the right way to handle its thorns — a real safety issue on a balcony. Lisbon is notably thornier and more vigorous than other lemons, so generic "thornless lemon" advice does not apply.

## Key Findings
- Lisbon grows **dense, vigorous, and thorny** (thornier than Eureka/Meyer) despite marketing that downplays thorns.
- **Do not snip individual thorns** — the cut creates an infection entry point. Instead **remove whole thorny/crossing/inward branches** during the dormant season.
- **Remove suckers/water sprouts promptly** — they are especially vigorous on young trees and steal energy; they are also often the thorniest growth.
- Maintain an **open but not thinned** structure: clear crossing/inward branches for airflow while keeping outer canopy density for sunburn protection.
- Thorniness often **decreases as the tree matures** and on fruiting (non-juvenile) wood.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Directly Lisbon- and balcony-relevant. On a balcony, thorns are a handling/safety hazard, so the standing approach is: remove entire thorny and sucker branches at the dormant pruning, wear protection, and never just clip thorn tips (infection risk). Expect more thorn management in the tree's early years; it eases with maturity.
