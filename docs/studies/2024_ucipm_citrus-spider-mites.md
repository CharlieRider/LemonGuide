## Summary
UC IPM guidance on the spider mites most likely to flare on a hot, dusty, west-facing balcony in summer — the conditions that favor mites and suppress their natural enemies.

## Key Findings
- Feeding produces **yellow-brown stippling/spots on foliage**, worst on the most recent flush; heavy infestations bronze the leaves.
- **High mite numbers in summer and fall may need a miticide, but formal thresholds are not established** — judge by stippling severity and population trend.
- Mites thrive in **hot, dry, dusty conditions**; **dust and water stress aggravate** outbreaks, while overhead rinsing and reduced stress suppress them.
- **Broad-spectrum insecticides flare mites** by killing predatory mites — avoid them.
- Horticultural oil and predatory mites are the home-appropriate controls.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
High-risk pest for this setup. A west-facing balcony is hot and often dusty — prime spider-mite habitat, especially on summer flush. Practical defense: **periodically hose down the foliage (undersides included)** to knock back mites and dust, avoid water stress (Topic 4) and broad-spectrum sprays, and spot-treat with horticultural oil if stippling spreads. Expect mites to be the dominant summer pest.
