## Summary
Places the Lisbon among the common lemon cultivars so the choice is informed — what distinguishes it from Eureka, Meyer, Villafranca, and Bearss in fruit, habit, and climate fit. The "did I pick the right lemon?" reference for this project.

## Key Findings
- **Lisbon vs. Eureka** (the two true commercial lemons): fruit nearly identical (medium, acidic, ~10 segments, prominent nipple) — "even experts can't always tell the fruit apart." But **Lisbon is more vigorous, more upright, denser-foliaged, thornier, and notably more heat/cold/wind/neglect-tolerant**; Eureka is everbearing and nearly thornless but more tender and sparser.
- **Villafranca**: very similar to Lisbon/Eureka but **fewer thorns** (easier picking).
- **Bearss**: larger, very juicy, high rind-oil, few seeds; fast, productive tree.
- **Meyer** (a lemon×orange hybrid, not a true lemon): **sweeter, lower-acid**, smaller, less thorny, bushier — more cold-tender but very popular for containers.
- **Fit summary**: Eureka for steady year-round picking; **Lisbon for toughness (heat/cold/wind) and a strong winter crop**; Villafranca for low thorns; Bearss for juice/oil; Meyer for sweetness and compact container culture.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Honest framing for the Brentwood Lisbon: its big advantage is **ruggedness** — it shrugs off heat, cold snaps, wind, and neglect better than any other true lemon, which suits an exposed west-facing balcony. Its drawbacks for a 14" pot are real, though — **most vigorous + thorniest** of the lemons, so it demands more size-control pruning and thorn management than a Meyer or Villafranca would. If maximum toughness is the priority, Lisbon is well chosen; if easy handling/compactness mattered more, Meyer/Villafranca would be gentler. Connects to the Lisbon-vs-Eureka, growth-habit, thorn-management, and rootstock studies.
