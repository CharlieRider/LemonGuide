## Summary
The ETo × Kc evapotranspiration method and California's CIMIS reference-ET network — the quantitative way to anticipate how much water a coastal-LA citrus tree actually uses across seasons, rather than guessing.

## Key Findings
- **Crop water use ETc = ETo × Kc**: reference ET (ETo) from the local CIMIS station × a citrus crop coefficient (**Kc ≈ 0.6–0.7** for mature citrus).
- Coastal/Westside LA **ETo peaks ~5–6 mm/day in mid-summer and falls to ~1–2 mm/day in winter** (CIMIS coastal stations) — roughly a 3–5× seasonal swing in demand.
- Schedule by **updating root-zone depletion daily** (add ETc, subtract irrigation/rain/fog), not by fixed calendar.
- The **marine layer / fog** reduces effective ET on overcast mornings — coastal demand is lower than inland at the same air temperature.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Useful for scaling, with adaptation. Field ETc (per ground area) does not map onto a pot directly, but the **3–5× summer-to-winter demand swing** is what the container watering schedule must track: frequent summer irrigation, sharply reduced in the cool/wet season to avoid the waterlogging the shallow pot is prone to. Use the local CIMIS station as the seasonal demand signal.
