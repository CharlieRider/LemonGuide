## Summary
The end-use payoff: what Lisbon fruit is good for and how to keep it. Included to round out the variety profile — the reason for the whole project is usable, high-quality lemons.

## Key Findings
- **Flavor: tart, tangy, high-acid** — the classic culinary lemon profile; **thinner peel and slightly higher juice yield than Eureka**.
- **Uses: juice, zest, baking, dressings, marinades, beverages** — a true all-purpose culinary lemon.
- **Storage: ~1–2 weeks at room temperature, up to ~1 month refrigerated**; zest/juice can be frozen for longer keeping.
- Nutritionally a good **vitamin C** source (citrus); high citric acid content drives the tartness.
- Low-seeded/nearly seedless flesh makes juicing and zesting easy.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
The objective, stated plainly. A well-managed container Lisbon yields exactly this high-acid, juicy, thin-peeled culinary fruit — the target that justifies the substrate, watering, feeding, and heat work in Topics 1–10. Thin peel and good juice yield make even a modest container harvest worthwhile in the kitchen. Pick at deep-yellow maturity (Topic 11 harvest file) for peak acidity.
