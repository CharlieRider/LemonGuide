## Summary
A systematic review of how the plant growth regulators **gibberellic acid (GA3)** and auxins like **2,4-D** are used in citrus to manage flowering, fruit set, fruit drop, and rind quality — the chemical-management toolkit, for a grower who wants levers beyond water and fertilizer.

## Key Findings
- **GA3** supports flower-bud development, **reduces premature flower/fruit drop**, and improves the proportion of flowers that set fruit; applied as a **foliar spray around full bloom**.
- GA3 also improves fruit **size, peel smoothness, and rind color/firmness**, and can delay rind senescence (extending on-tree holding).
- **2,4-D (auxin)** reduces fruit drop; **GA3 + 2,4-D combined**, applied in successive fall sprays, cut fruit drop ~**25%** in trials.
- GA3 can **suppress flowering** when applied at the inductive period (high endogenous GA inhibits floral induction) — timing flips its effect, so it must be used deliberately.
- These are optimization tools, not necessities; effects are concentration- and timing-sensitive.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Optional fine-tuning for the Lisbon rather than a requirement (self-fertile + parthenocarpic, it sets fruit unaided). A hobbyist could use a labeled GA3 spray at bloom to lift fruit set and reduce drop, or to firm up rind quality — but timing matters (GA at the wrong time suppresses bloom). For most balcony growers, water-rhythm bloom triggering and good nutrition achieve the goal without PGRs. Pairs with the floral-induction, fruit-drop, and fruit-quality studies.
