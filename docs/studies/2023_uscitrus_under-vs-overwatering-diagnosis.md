## Summary
Nursery guidance on distinguishing the look-alike symptoms of under- vs over-watering in citrus — the most common diagnostic confusion for container growers, where the wrong call (adding water to a drowning tree) is fatal.

## Key Findings
- **Underwatering**: leaves dull, **curl/cup, dry and crisp from the tip**, drop while still attached firmly; soil/mix light and dry; recovery is fast once watered.
- **Overwatering**: leaves **yellow and drop, may be soft/limp, wilt despite wet mix**; soil heavy/saturated; root rot if prolonged.
- **The discriminator is the mix, not the leaves**: check moisture at depth — wilting + wet mix = over-watering/root rot; wilting + dry mix = thirst.
- Container citrus swings between both extremes faster than in-ground trees because of the small reservoir.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Directly the daily decision tool for the 14" pot. Because the same wilt can mean opposite things, the rule is **always check moisture at depth before watering**: a pot that is wet at 3–4 inches and still wilting needs drying and drainage help, not more water. Pairs with the pot-weight method from the VCE water-stress study.
