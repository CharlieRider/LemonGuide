## Summary
Derives critical leaf-tissue nutrient ranges for citrus by the boundary-line method, giving the diagnostic targets a leaf analysis should hit. Provides the "am I feeding correctly" yardstick that converts fertilizer guesswork into measured management.

## Key Findings
- Citrus leaf sufficiency ranges (this study): **N 19.1–22.7 g/kg (≈1.9–2.3%)**, **Ca 20.9–28.4 g/kg (≈2.1–2.8%)**, **Mg 2.0–3.3 g/kg (≈0.20–0.33%)**.
- Leaf analysis via the sufficiency-range method gives a more reliable fertilizer guide than soil testing alone for tree crops.
- Confirms citrus needs substantial **calcium** (highest of the three by mass) and a non-trivial **magnesium** level — nutrients easily depleted by leaching and by coir's Ca/Mg deficit.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Diagnostic anchor. A spring leaf test compared to these ranges tells whether the container program is on target; the high Ca requirement reinforces the gypsum/Ca amendment from Topic 2, and the Mg range argues for dolomite or a Mg supplement in a leaching-prone shallow pot. Values are standardized on field spring-flush leaves, so sample comparably.
