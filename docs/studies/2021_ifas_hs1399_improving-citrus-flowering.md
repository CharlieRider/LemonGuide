## Summary
UF/IFAS management guide translating flowering physiology into practice: induction conditions, the very low flower-to-fruit conversion, and nutrition/timing levers. Frames realistic yield expectations and what actually moves bloom and set.

## Key Findings
- **Cold induction range 59–68°F (15–20°C)** in fall/winter; **drought induction needs ~45–60 days** of deficit (tropical/subtropical).
- **Moderate stress works**: irrigating at **50% of crop demand gave floral induction equal to zero irrigation** without severe damage — over-stressing is unnecessary.
- **Floral development resumes 7–10 days after water deficit ends** (up to 4 weeks in some regions).
- **<2% of citrus flowers become harvestable fruit** (Goldschmidt 1999) — heavy bloom and heavy drop are normal; competition among fruitlets is the main filter.
- **Winter N (urea) plus inductive conditions increased flower numbers** (Lovatt et al. 1988).
- High fall **GA reduces** floral induction.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Reframes expectations and tactics. The <2% flower-to-fruit rate means a balcony Lisbon's dramatic flower drop is normal, not failure — don't panic-feed or overwater in response. A modest winter N application supports the spring bloom. Do not deliberately drought-stress the shallow pot for induction: coastal winter cold already provides it, and severe drying risks the small root system.
