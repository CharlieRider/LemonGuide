## Summary
Covers the slow, years-long accumulation of fertilizer and tap-water salts in container media and the periodic deep flush that prevents it from reaching toxic levels. The chronic counterpart to the per-irrigation leaching fraction (Topic 4).

## Key Findings
- Salts accumulate from **fertilizer residue + dissolved minerals in tap water (Ca, Mg, Na)**; a **white mineral crust on the pot rim/surface** is the visible sign.
- **Periodic deep flush every 1–2 months: run 2–3× (up to 3–5×) the pot's volume** of water through until it runs clear, to leach accumulated salts.
- Untreated salt build-up causes **leaf-tip burn, stunting, reduced water uptake, and eventual root dieback** — citrus is salt-sensitive (Topic 4, ECe ~1.4 dS/m threshold).
- A **well-draining mix and adequate drainage holes** make flushing effective; a clogged/degraded mix traps salt.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Essential long-term maintenance with hard LA water. Beyond the routine ~15–30% leaching fraction at each watering (Topic 4), schedule a **monthly-to-bimonthly deep flush (2–3× pot volume until runoff is clear)** to keep cumulative salts below citrus's low tolerance. Watch for the rim crust as the trigger. This pairs with media refresh (degraded mix won't flush well) to keep the root zone non-saline across years.
