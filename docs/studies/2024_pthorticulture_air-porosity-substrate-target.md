## Summary
Defines the target air-filled porosity band for quality container substrates, giving the number a citrus mix should be engineered to hit. Bridges Topic 1 (hypoxia thresholds) and Topic 2 (mix design): the mix must clear the hypoxia floor with margin.

## Key Findings
- High-quality container substrates target **air porosity ≈ 10–20% at container capacity** — air pockets that allow O₂/CO₂ exchange to the roots.
- Air porosity governs gas exchange in the root zone; too little and roots suffocate, too much and the mix dries out and holds too little water.
- This 10–20% target is measured *at container capacity*, i.e. after drainage — so in a shallow pot, where the saturated zone is proportionally larger, the bulk mix must aim at the high end (≈20%) to keep the wet bottom above the ~10% hypoxia floor.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Sets the design target. To keep a 14"-deep pot's saturated bottom zone above the ~10–12% hypoxia threshold (Topic 1), formulate the bulk mix toward the upper 15–20% AFP end with coarse bark and pumice — the shallow geometry eats the safety margin a deep pot would have.
