## Summary
Addresses a balcony-specific stressor: wind. Elevated, exposed balconies experience higher wind that accelerates water loss and physical stress on a container tree, compounding heat and irrigation demands.

## Key Findings
- **Wind increases transpiration and evaporative loss**, drying both foliage and the small container reservoir faster — raising irrigation frequency.
- **Wind desiccation** can scorch leaf margins and intensify the leaf-curl/water-stress symptoms (Topic 4), independent of soil moisture.
- Exposed balconies also see **greater temperature swings and physical rocking** of a top-heavy potted tree — a tip-over/safety risk for a vigorous Lisbon.
- Mitigation: **windbreak/screening, siting against the building wall, a heavier/wider-based pot or securing the pot, and accounting for higher water use** in wind.
- Wind plus the west sun raises combined evaporative demand beyond what air temperature alone suggests.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Balcony-specific and practical. A west-facing balcony Lisbon will lose water faster in wind, so the irrigation plan (Topic 4) must flex upward on windy days, and leaf-margin scorch may be wind, not just heat or salt. Stabilize the relatively shallow, top-heavy 14" pot against tipping (heavier pot, wall siting, or tie-down) — a real safety consideration for a thorny, vigorous tree on a balcony.
