## Summary
A second buildable citrus-mix recipe that adds a small compost/casting fraction for slow nutrition and CEC, while keeping bark as the structural majority. Cross-checks the Biology Insights recipe and adds the longevity rationale for bark.

## Key Findings
- Recipe: **~40% pine bark fines : 30% potting soil or coco coir : 20% perlite/pumice : 10% compost or worm castings** — bark majority for structure/drainage, small compost fraction for CEC and slow nutrient supply.
- **Aged pine/fir bark fines break down very slowly**, holding the mix's physical structure for **several years before repotting** is needed — the durability that makes bark the right backbone for a 10-year container.
- The 10% compost fraction adds cation-exchange capacity to an otherwise low-CEC bark/perlite mix, buffering nutrient swings between fertigations.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Directly buildable alternative to the 50/30/20 mix, with the 10% compost giving a little nutrient buffering useful in a leaching-prone shallow pot. Keep the compost fraction small (≤10%) so it does not clog aeration; in the 14" pot, structure must win over richness.
