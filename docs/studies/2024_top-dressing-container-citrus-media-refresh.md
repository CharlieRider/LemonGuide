## Summary
Top-dressing — replacing the **top layer** of spent mix with fresh material — is the low-stress alternative to full repotting for maintaining soil quality in a large, established container tree. The maintenance step between the infrequent (and stressful) full repottings.

## Key Findings
- Container mix degrades over time: **organic matter is consumed, fertilizer salts build up, and the surface compacts** where water enters — top-dressing renews exactly that zone.
- **Method**: scrape off the top inch or two of old/compacted mix and replace with **fresh compost / worm castings / fresh potting mix** (e.g., ~1 part castings to 2 parts compost).
- **Much gentler than repotting** — it **doesn't disturb the roots or cause transplant shock**.
- For **big/heavy trees that are impractical to repot**, top-dressing is the primary way to keep feeding the soil ecosystem and replenishing nutrients.
- **Cadence**: roughly **every ~2 years**, at the **start of the growing season**; complements (doesn't replace) periodic full repotting and salt-flushing.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
The practical "in-between" maintenance for the Lisbon once it's near its final pot size and full repotting becomes a chore: every spring or two, **scrape and replace the top 1–2 inches with fresh compost/mix**, restoring nutrients and the surface where water and air enter — without the shock of disturbing the roots. Combine with periodic salt-flushing (it doesn't remove accumulated salts) and the occasional full repot/root-prune.
