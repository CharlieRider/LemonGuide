## Summary
Peer-reviewed review of citrus rootstocks for size control, quantifying how strongly Flying Dragon and other dwarfing rootstocks reduce scion size and why. The mechanistic backbone for choosing a rootstock to keep a vigorous Lisbon manageable in a pot.

## Key Findings
- **Flying Dragon (FD) as rootstock reduces overall growth by up to ~66–75%** vs trifoliate/Troyer standards; scions on FD typically **stay under 2.5 m** (Hayat et al. 2022).
- **FD as interstock gives ~37% average size reduction** — a lesser but still useful dwarfing route.
- Dwarfing acts via **lowered hydraulic conductivity** and **reduced auxin (IAA)/gibberellin** — on **Eureka lemon, FD produced the lowest IAA** of rootstocks tested.
- Critically for containers: **dwarfing rootstocks are LESS responsive to N, K, Ca, Mg and micronutrients** from the root medium — they take up nutrients less aggressively.
- Trifoliate-lineage rootstocks confer cold tolerance and Phytophthora tolerance.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Highly relevant. The 66–75% size reduction from Flying Dragon is precisely what makes a 10-year, manageable Lisbon container realistic. The caveat matters too: because dwarfing rootstocks absorb nutrients less aggressively, the container feeding program must be steady and complete (the "weekly weakly" + micronutrient approach from Topic 3) rather than relying on the root to scavenge.
