## Summary
Survey of the named Lisbon strains maintained by UC Riverside — they differ in vigor, thorniness, and fruit traits, which can matter when selecting a tree for a constrained container.

## Key Findings
- **Frost nucellar Lisbon (CRC 3176)**: the classic California strain — very vigorous, thorny, dense, productive (the baseline this repository assumes).
- **Yen Ben Lisbon (CRC 4067)**: an Australian/NZ selection noted for being **less thorny, more compact, with smoother thinner-skinned fruit** — potentially better suited to a small container.
- **Limoneira 8A Lisbon (CRC 3501)**: a widely planted commercial California strain selected for productivity and fruit uniformity.
- **Prior Lisbon (CRC 3505)**: another nucellar selection in the collection.
- All share the Lisbon profile (acidic, low-seeded fruit; winter main crop) but vary in thorn load and vigor.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Selection-relevant nuance. For a balcony container where thorns and vigor are the main burdens, a **less-thorny, more compact strain like Yen Ben** (on a dwarfing rootstock) could meaningfully reduce the pruning/handling load versus the classic Frost nucellar Lisbon — worth seeking when buying. If only the standard Frost nucellar strain is available, the Topic-5 (rootstock) and Topic-7 (pruning/thorn) measures carry more weight.
