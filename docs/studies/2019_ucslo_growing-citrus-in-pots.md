## Summary
California-specific (coastal) UC extension guidance on container citrus — the closest climate match to Brentwood among the Topic 1 sources. Confirms practical sizing, elevation-for-drainage, and dwarfing-rootstock recommendations for the LA-type climate.

## Key Findings
- Small trees do well in **14–16 inch pots**; step up when root-bound — brackets the 19" pot as a reasonable medium-term home.
- **Raise the pot ≥ ½ inch off the surface** (feet/bricks) so the drainage holes clear standing water — essential to prevent the perched-water zone from backing up in a shallow pot.
- **Cactus/palm mixes drain better** than standard potting mix; avoid garden soil.
- Watering **roughly every 3 days**, more in heat — a coastal-CA baseline cadence (contrast with Clemson's "near-daily" humid-SE figure).
- **Dwarfing rootstock** keeps container trees compact — flags Topic 5 as decisive for a vigorous Lisbon.
- ≥ 6 hours sun/day required — the west balcony's afternoon exposure satisfies light but adds heat load.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Most climate-relevant Topic-1 source. Directly endorses the pot size, prescribes pot elevation for drainage (key for a 14" perched-water zone), and the ~3-day coastal watering cadence is a better starting point for Brentwood than humid-Southeast schedules.
