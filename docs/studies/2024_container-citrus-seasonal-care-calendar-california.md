## Summary
A month-by-month maintenance rhythm for container citrus in California's mild climate — the capstone that sequences feeding, watering, repotting, pest checks, and protection across the year. Ties the repository's topic-specific findings into an annual routine.

## Key Findings
- **Fertilize ~monthly February–October**; first feed **Jan–Feb just before bloom**, more in **May and June**; (or a controlled-release product roughly **March & August**). **Do not fertilize in winter** — it forces tender frost-vulnerable growth.
- Apply **micronutrients every 4–6 weeks Feb–Oct** and **calcium during bloom/fruit set (≈Apr–Aug)** in container culture (where leaching strips nutrients fast).
- **Water more in the warm season** (≈weekly–biweekly in summer) and **much less in winter** (every few weeks, just keeping the mix moist) — citrus is **not drought-tolerant** but resents winter waterlogging.
- **Spring** is the window for repotting/top-dressing/structural pruning and resuming feeding; **summer** = peak water + sunburn/heat protection; **fall** = taper feeding; **winter** = minimal inputs + frost watch.
- Container citrus needs **more frequent feeding and attention than in-ground** trees throughout.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
The operating schedule for the Lisbon, tuned to coastal Brentwood: **feed Feb–Oct (stop for winter), water heavily in summer and sparingly in winter, repot/top-dress and prune in spring, protect from heat/sunburn in summer and from the rare frost in winter.** It sequences and reconciles the rest of the repository — the nutrition timing (SS675), watering seasonality, repotting timing, heat/sunburn protection, and frost thresholds — into one annual loop for a long-lived container tree. Connects to nearly every topic, especially nutrition timing, watering, repotting timing, and heat/cold protection.
