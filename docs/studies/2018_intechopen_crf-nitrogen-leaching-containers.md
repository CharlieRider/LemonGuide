## Summary
Review of controlled-release fertilizer (CRF) behavior in containers, including citrus field trials and the leaching/salt dynamics that make CRF attractive for a fast-draining pot. Addresses the container-specific problem the in-ground UC IPM schedule does not: nutrients washing out.

## Key Findings
- **Young/non-bearing citrus fertilized with CRF at 50% of the recommended rate performed equal to 100% rate with water-soluble fertilizer (WSF)** in Florida field trials — CRF roughly doubles N-use efficiency.
- **CRF reduces total salt accumulation**, lowering leaf-burn risk versus soluble-salt WSF.
- **Substrate moisture/temperature drive CRF release** — hot, frequently-watered containers release faster, so release tracks the plant's summer demand but can over-release in heat.
- **Topdressed CRF leached less N than incorporated CRF**; mechanically incorporated CRF gave the greatest salt/N leaching — placement matters.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Highly applicable. A 14"-deep, frequently-irrigated west-balcony pot leaches nutrients fast; CRF (topdressed, not deeply incorporated) gives steadier feeding at lower salt load than soluble feeds. Watch the heat-accelerated release in summer — split with light liquid feeds rather than one heavy CRF charge.
