## Summary
Quantifies how container color drives root-zone temperature and root growth — the single highest-leverage heat-management decision for a west-facing balcony pot. Pairs directly with the citrus root-death thresholds (Topic 1) to set a hard design rule.

## Key Findings
- Sun-facing substrate temperatures: **gloss/flat white ~36°C vs black control 50.3°C** — a **~14°C difference** from color alone (bean study, Markham et al.).
- **Dark pots run ~8.9–14.7°C hotter** in the root zone than light-colored equivalents.
- **Root growth slows sharply above 30°C and ceases above ~38°C**; black-pot **root biomass was reduced up to 71%** vs white.
- Container **color had a greater cooling effect than micro-sprinkler frequency** (MDPI 2025) — paint/choose color before adding water tricks.
- Shading the container exterior compounds the cooling.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Decisive and directly actionable. A black 14" pot on a west balcony can hit ~50°C at the sun-facing wall — past the level that halts citrus root growth (>38°C) and approaching acute injury (Topic 1: ~50°C for ~45 min damages citrus roots). **Use a white/light pot (or paint the exterior white), shade the sun-facing wall, and double-pot.** This is the cheapest, biggest single intervention against root-zone heat.
