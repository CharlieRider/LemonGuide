## Summary
When and how to harvest Lisbon, the visual/tactile ripeness cues, and the key fact that lemons are non-climacteric (won't ripen meaningfully after picking) — so timing the harvest matters.

## Key Findings
- **Ready 6–9 months after flowering**; main crop matures **late fall through early spring** (year-round dribble in warm climates).
- **Ripeness cues: full size, bright/deep yellow, glossy skin, slight give under thumb pressure, heavy/dense feel.**
- **Lemons are non-climacteric** — they **do not ripen significantly off the tree**, so harvest at proper maturity, not early.
- Lisbon can be **held on the tree** for a period and picked as needed (a practical advantage for a home grower).
- **Storage: ~1–2 weeks at room temperature, up to ~1 month refrigerated**; harvest maturity and cold-storage period affect quality (ScienceDirect).

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
Practical harvest guidance for the balcony tree. Because lemons don't ripen off the tree, **wait for deep-yellow, slightly-soft, heavy fruit** before picking — green fruit picked early stays sour-tart and never sweetens. The tree's ability to hold ripe fruit lets a household harvest a few at a time. Expect a modest container crop (Topic 6), with the winter main flush as the peak.
