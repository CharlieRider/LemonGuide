## Summary
Practical guidance on automating watering for patio/container citrus with drip or micro-spray emitters on a timer — the realistic way to keep a fast-drying balcony pot consistently moist through hot, travel-prone summers without hand-watering twice a day.

## Key Findings
- Precision drip on citrus typically cuts water use **15–30%** and can lift yield **5–20%** vs. coarse watering, by delivering small frequent volumes to the root zone.
- Citrus is **not naturally suited to single-point drip** (one emitter wets too little of the root ball) — run **multiple emitters** or a **micro-spray** to wet a larger fraction of the surface, and run **longer cycles** (≥1 hour) so water penetrates rather than just dampening the top.
- Container kits (e.g., patio drip kits) plus a **hose-bib timer** (programmable up to ~4 cycles/day) automate delivery; match emitter flow to pot size so each cycle refills to container capacity **with a small leaching surplus**.
- Summer interval guidance: in coarse media, citrus is effectively watered **every 3–4 days** in-ground — but a **shallow 14" container will need water far more often (often daily)** because of its tiny reservoir.

## Relevance to 19" × 14" Container
The recommended setup for this Lisbon: 2–3 drip emitters or a micro-spray ringing the root ball on a timer, programmed for short daily (or twice-daily in heat-wave) cycles that each bring the pot to capacity plus ~20% runoff for salt leaching. Avoid a single dribble emitter (dry pockets) and avoid a sealed reservoir (salt trap — see water-quality and self-watering studies). Combine with a moisture sensor or the pot-weight check to tune the timer seasonally.
